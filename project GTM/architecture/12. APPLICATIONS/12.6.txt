**12.6. GLOSSARY OF TERMS**

This glossary contains definitions of key terms used in the architectural
document and during the implementation of the target GTM infrastructure.
Terms are listed in alphabetical order. Each term includes a brief definition
and references to document sections where it is discussed in detail.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Account Executive (AE)** – sales manager responsible for closing enterprise deals.
Conducts negotiations, uses MEDDPICC methodology, updates forecasts.
(*sections 9.2, 8.3.2*)

**Account Manager (AM)** – manager responsible for expansion and contract renewals.
Responsible for upsell and cross‑sell to existing customers, as well as contract
renewals. (*sections 9.4, 8.3.4*)

**Account Score** – numerical indicator (0–1) determining account priority for
outreach. Calculated based on intent signals, product activity, and ICP fit.
(*sections 6.1, 12.1.1*)

**Agent** – software component (AI assistant) that performs automated actions
within the GTM architecture. Agents are divided into levels (1–4) by functionality.
(*sections 4.2, 10*)

**Agent Holding** – the totality of all agents (3,000+), organised into a
four‑level hierarchy for mass analysis, strategy, tactics, and execution.
(*sections 4.2, 12.5.1*)

**Apex** – programming language on the Salesforce platform, used for complex
business logic, triggers, and external API calls.
(*sections 5.2, 12.3.3*)

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** – annual recurring revenue from subscriptions
and contracts. Key growth metric. (*sections 2.3, 6.4*)

**CAPEX (Capital Expenditure)** – capital expenditures, one‑time investments
in development, integrations, training. For the project – $2.0M.
(*sections 2.3, 10*)

**CRM (Customer Relationship Management)** – customer relationship management
system. In the project – Salesforce, the central system of truth.
(*sections 4.4, 5*)

**Customer Success Manager (CSM)** – customer success manager responsible for
onboarding, retention, and customer health.
(*sections 9.3, 8.3.3*)

**CVaR (Conditional Value at Risk)** – conditional value at risk; average
losses in the tail of the distribution (worst 5% of scenarios).
(*sections 11.3, 12.1.4*)

**DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)** – investment valuation method, discounting
cash flows. Used to calculate NPV. (*sections 11.3, 12.1.4*)

**Dead Letter Queue (DLQ)** – queue of unprocessed events that could not be
delivered or processed after several attempts. Handled by control panel operators.
(*sections 8.1, 11.2*)

**Event‑driven architecture** – architecture where actions are triggered by
events (data changes, signals). Implemented via AWS EventBridge + Kafka.
(*sections 4.1, 4.4, 12.4.1*)

**EventBridge (AWS)** – event routing service from various sources to target
systems. Used as the central event bus. (*sections 4.4, 12.4.1*)

**Expansion ARR** – additional revenue from existing customers through upsell
and cross‑sell. Target growth – >30%. (*sections 7.3, 9.4*)

**Expansion Signal** – automatically detected indicator of customer readiness
for expansion (increased activity, feature requests, renewal with growth).
(*sections 6.3, 8.2.2*)

**Forecast Accuracy** – revenue forecast accuracy, measured as 1 – MAPE.
Target value >90%. (*sections 2.3, 6.4*)

**Gainsight** – customer experience management platform (Customer Success).
Used for health score, playbooks, NPS surveys.
(*sections 4.4, 5.3, 6.2*)

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)** – European data protection
regulation. Requires special handling of personal data.
(*sections 11.4*)

**Health Score** – composite indicator of customer health (0–1). Based on
product activity, support tickets, engagement, and NPS.
(*sections 6.2, 12.1.2*)

**Hightouch** – reverse ETL tool, synchronising data from Snowflake to
Salesforce in real time. (*sections 4.4, 5.3*)

**ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** – ideal customer profile. Used to assess
account alignment with target criteria (industry, size, technology stack).
(*sections 6.1, 12.1.1*)

**Intent signal** – data about a potential customer’s expressed interest
(searching for solutions, visiting the site, requests). Provided by 6sense.
(*sections 6.1, 8.1*)

**Kafka (Apache Kafka)** – distributed streaming platform. Used as an event
buffer and job queue between agent levels.
(*sections 4.4, 12.4.1*)

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** – key performance indicators used to
evaluate the performance of roles and the system as a whole.
(*sections 2.3, 9*)

**MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error)** – mean absolute percentage error
of the forecast. Target value <10%. (*sections 6.4, 12.1.3*)

**MEDDPICC** – deal qualification methodology: Metrics, Economic Buyer,
Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain,
Champion, Competition. (*sections 5.1, 9.2*)

**Net Retention (NRR)** – revenue retention metric, accounting for expansion
and churn. Target value >120%. (*sections 2.3, 6.2*)

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)** – customer loyalty metric. Used in Health Score
and for satisfaction measurement. (*sections 6.2, 9.3*)

**NPV (Net Present Value)** – net present value of investments.
Calculated value – from $28.4M to $42.1M. (*sections 2.3, 12.1.4*)

**OPEX (Operational Expenditure)** – operating expenses (cloud resources,
licenses, support). Annual $1.7M. (*sections 2.3, 10*)

**Payback period** – investment payback period. Target – 8–12 months.
(*sections 2.3, 12.1.4*)

**Pipeline Coverage** – ratio of open pipeline sum to quarterly quota.
Target value >3×. (*sections 2.3, 8.2.4*)

**Platform Events** – Salesforce mechanism for publishing events that can
be processed by external systems. Used for event‑driven integration.
(*sections 5.2, 12.3.1*)

**Playbook** – predefined sequence of automated actions (task creation,
email sending, API calls) triggered by a specific event.
(*sections 8.2, 8.3*)

**Process Builder / Flow** – Salesforce tools for automating simple processes
without writing code. (*sections 5.2, 12.3.2*)

**Reverse ETL** – process of synchronising data from a data warehouse (Snowflake)
to operational systems (Salesforce). Implemented via Hightouch.
(*sections 4.4, 5.3*)

**RevOps (Revenue Operations)** – department responsible for managing automation,
data, reporting, and resource planning. (*sections 9.5, 10*)

**ROI (Return on Investment)** – return on investment. Target value >300% over 3 years.
(*sections 2.3, 12.1.4*)

**Sales Strategist** – analytical role using intent data and product activity
to qualify accounts and hand off to AE. (*sections 9.1, 8.3.1*)

**Salesforce** – CRM system, the central system of truth for all operational
GTM data. (*sections 4.4, 5*)

**SalesLoft** – outreach automation platform (email, sequences).
(*sections 4.4, 5.3*)

**Snowflake** – cloud data warehouse. Used for event storage, analytics,
data marts. (*sections 4.4, 12.2*)

**SOC2** – security standard for cloud services. Sourcegraph and the system
must comply. (*sections 11.4*)

**Tableau** – business intelligence and data visualisation platform.
Used for dashboards and reports. (*sections 4.4, 5.4*)

**Territory design** – market division among AEs by geographic, vertical,
or account‑based principles. (*sections 7.2*)

**Time to First Value (TTFV)** – time from deal closure to first active
product usage. Target value <30 days. (*sections 7.4, 9.3*)

**Upsell / Cross‑sell** – expansion: upsell – increasing the volume of the
current product, cross‑sell – selling additional products (Deep Search, MCP).
(*sections 6.3, 9.4*)

**VaR (Value at Risk)** – value at risk; the maximum loss that will not be
exceeded with a given probability (95%). (*sections 11.3, 12.1.4*)

**VAR model (Vector Autoregression)** – time series model linking ARR growth
with macroeconomic indicators (PMI, interest rate, technology index). Used to
adjust forecasts. (*sections 6.4, 12.1.3*)

**WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital)** – weighted average cost of capital.
Used in DCF (12%). (*sections 12.1.4*)

**Win Rate** – share of won deals from total. Target value >25%.
(*sections 2.3, 6.4*)

**Win Probability** – deal closing probability calculated by a logistic
regression model. Used in revenue forecasting. (*sections 6.4, 12.1.3*)

**6sense** – intent signal collection platform. Provides data on which
companies are searching for solutions related to code intelligence.
(*sections 4.4, 5.3*)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON THE GLOSSARY**

- The glossary covers all key terms used in the architectural document
  and ensures a common understanding for all project participants.
- Each definition includes references to the relevant sections of the document
  for deeper study.
- Terms are presented in alphabetical order, facilitating search.
