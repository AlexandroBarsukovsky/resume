**12.1. COMPLETE SET OF FORMULAS (ACCOUNT SCORE, HEALTH SCORE, FORECAST, DCF)**

This protocol contains the full mathematical description of the key models used
in the target GTM architecture. All formulas are presented with detailed explanations
of components, weights, data sources, and calibration methods. The models have been
validated on historical data and simulations (X16, X64, D1).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.1.1. ACCOUNT SCORE (ACCOUNT PRIORITISATION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Formula:**

`S = 0.4 × I + 0.3 × U + 0.3 × F`

where:
- **I** – intent signal strength (0–1), aggregated over the last 7 days with exponential decay.
- **U** – normalised product usage (0–1) over the last 30 days.
- **F** – fit with the ideal customer profile (0–1) based on Clearbit/ZoomInfo.

**12.1.1.1. Intent signal (I)**

Data collection: 6sense API (pull every 30 minutes).

I = (Σ intensity_i × w_i) / Σ w_i, where w_i = exp(-age_days_i / 7)

- `intensity_i` – strength of an individual signal (0–1).
- `age_days_i` – age of the signal in days.
- Normalisation: dividing by the sum of weights ensures I ∈ [0,1].

**12.1.1.2. Product usage (U)**

U = 0.6 × (DAU/MAU) + 0.4 × (active_features / total_features)

- **DAU/MAU** – rolling average over the last 30 days (daily ratio of unique users active on a given day to unique users active in the month).
- **active_features / total_features** – share of features used by at least one user from the account over 30 days.

**12.1.1.3. ICP fit (F)**

F = match_industry × 0.5 + match_tech_stack × 0.3 + match_employee_count × 0.2

where each `match` = 1 if the value matches the ICP rules, otherwise 0.
The ICP rules are defined by RevOps and can be adjusted via the control panel.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.1.2. HEALTH SCORE (CHURN RISK AND EXPANSION READINESS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Formula:**

`H = 0.5 × U + 0.2 × (1 – T_norm) + 0.2 × E + 0.1 × N`

where:
- **U** – product usage (same as in Account Score).
- **T_norm** – normalised number of support tickets.
- **E** – engagement (0–1).
- **N** – NPS (0–1).

**12.1.2.1. Normalising support tickets (T_norm)**

T_norm = (max_tickets – tickets_count) / (max_tickets – min_tickets)

- `tickets_count` – number of tickets in the last 30 days.
- `max_tickets`, `min_tickets` – global maximum and minimum among all accounts over the period.
- If `max_tickets = min_tickets`, then `T_norm = 1` for all.

**12.1.2.2. Engagement (E)**

E = (open_rate + webinar_attendance + doc_downloads_norm) / 3

- `open_rate` – share of opened emails (sent via SalesLoft) over 30 days.
- `webinar_attendance` – 1 if the customer attended at least one webinar in the last 30 days, otherwise 0.
- `doc_downloads_norm` – number of documentation downloads, normalised by the maximum value among all accounts.

**12.1.2.3. NPS (N)**

- For scale -100..100: `N = (NPS_raw + 100) / 200`.
- For scale 0..10: `N = NPS_raw / 10`.
- If no survey has been conducted in the last 90 days, `N = 0.5`.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.1.3. QUARTERLY REVENUE FORECAST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**12.1.3.1. Weighted pipeline with win rates**

`F_base = Σ_{opp ∈ Open} Amount_opp × P_win(opp)`

**12.1.3.2. Deal closing probability (logistic regression)**

`P_win(opp) = 1 / (1 + exp(–(β₀ + β₁·Stage + β₂·Segment + β₃·Region + β₄·DaysInStage + β₅·MacroIndex)))`

- `Stage` – categorical variable (one‑hot encoding).
- `Segment` – Enterprise, Mid‑Market, SMB.
- `Region` – US, EMEA, APAC, LATAM.
- `DaysInStage` – number of days in the current stage (logarithmic transformation).
- `MacroIndex` – macroeconomic index (see below).
- Coefficients β are updated monthly on historical data (logistic regression).

**12.1.3.3. Macroeconomic adjustment (VAR model)**

`Y_t = c + Σ_{i=1..4} A_i·Y_{t-i} + ε_t`

where:
- `Y_t = [ARR_growth_t, PMI_t, FedRate_t, TechSentiment_t]`.
- Estimation of coefficients A_i and c on quarterly data 2015–2025.
- The forecast `δ_macro` is obtained as the difference between the VAR forecast and the historical average ARR_growth.

**12.1.3.4. Seasonal coefficients**

`S_q = (average revenue in quarter q over 3 years) / (average revenue over all quarters)`

| Quarter | S_q  |
|---------|------|
| Q1      | 0.92 |
| Q2      | 0.98 |
| Q3      | 1.00 |
| Q4      | 1.10 |

**12.1.3.5. Final forecast**

`F_final = F_base × (1 + δ_macro) × S_q`

**12.1.3.6. Accuracy assessment (MAPE)**

`MAPE = (1/n) Σ |(F_actual – F_forecast) / F_actual| × 100%`

Target value: MAPE < 10% (accuracy > 90%).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.1.4. FINANCIAL AND ECONOMIC MODELS (DCF, ROI, PAYBACK, VAR, CVAR)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**12.1.4.1. Net Present Value (NPV)**

`NPV = Σ_{t=0}^{T} FCF_t / (1 + WACC)^t`

- `FCF_t` – free cash flow in year t (after CAPEX and OPEX).
- `WACC` – weighted average cost of capital (12%).
- `T` – planning horizon (5 years).

**12.1.4.2. Payback period**

The smallest `t` such that `Σ_{i=0}^{t} FCF_i ≥ 0`.

**12.1.4.3. Return on Investment (ROI)**

`ROI = (Σ_{t=1}^{T} (Revenue_t – Cost_t) – Investment_0) / Investment_0 × 100%`

**12.1.4.4. Value at Risk (VaR) and Conditional VaR (CVaR)**

Historical simulation (1,000 scenarios) is used for the return distribution of the
investment portfolio (CAPEX + OPEX). At the 95% confidence level:

- `VaR_95` – the maximum loss not expected to be exceeded with 95% probability.
- `CVaR_95` – the average loss in the worst 5% of scenarios.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.1.5. ADDITIONAL FORMULAS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**12.1.5.1. Pipeline Coverage**

`PC = (Σ Amount_opp) / Quota`

**12.1.5.2. Net Retention (NRR)**

`NRR = (ARR_start + Expansion – Churn) / ARR_start`

**12.1.5.3. Win Rate**

`WR = Closed_Won / (Closed_Won + Closed_Lost)`

**12.1.5.4. Time to First Value (TTFV)**

Average number of days from deal closure (Closed Won) to the first recorded
product event (login, search).

**CONCLUSIONS**

- Models are calibrated on historical data and periodically retrained

- The presented set covers all key aspects of scoring, forecasting,
  and financial evaluation required to implement the target GTM architecture.
