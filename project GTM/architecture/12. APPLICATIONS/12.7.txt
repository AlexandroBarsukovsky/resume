**12.7. REFERENCES AND SOURCES**

This section contains a list of sources used in the development of the target
GTM architecture. The sources are divided into categories: Sourcegraph corporate
materials, external industry sources, scientific and technical literature,
management and methodology books, as well as internal SIMBIOS PUT B protocols.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.7.1. SOURCEGRAPH CORPORATE MATERIALS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. **Sourcegraph Handbook** (public version) – description of culture, processes,
   OKRs, team structure. Used to analyse the current state of GTM and
   cultural characteristics.  
   *Source:* https://handbook.sourcegraph.com/

2. **Sourcegraph Blog** – articles on GTM transformation, product releases,
   customer case studies (Stripe, Uber, Palo Alto Networks).  
   *Source:* https://sourcegraph.com/blog

3. **Interviews with leadership** – public appearances by Quinn Slack (CEO),
   interviews with CRO and CPO, available on YouTube and podcasts (e.g.,
   “The Changelog”, “Software Engineering Daily”).  
   *Links:* a selection of quotes is provided in Sections 2 and 3.

4. **Open job postings** – descriptions of Sales Strategist, AE, CSM, RevOps roles
   on Sourcegraph’s careers portal. Used to refine KPIs and employee expectations.  
   *Source:* https://boards.greenhouse.io/sourcegraph

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.7.2. EXTERNAL INDUSTRY SOURCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5. **Gartner Market Guide for Code Intelligence Platforms** (2024) – market overview,
   key players, trends. Used for Sourcegraph positioning.
   *Access:* Gartner subscription required.

6. **Forrester Wave: AI‑Powered Code Development Tools** (2025) – analysis of the
   competitive landscape, impact of AI on development tools.

7. **Macroeconomic indicator reports**:
   - PMI (ISM Manufacturing and Services) – data for 2015–2025.
   - Federal Reserve (refinancing rate).
   - S&P 500 IT Index (technology index).  
   Used to calibrate the VAR model.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.7.3. SCIENTIFIC AND TECHNICAL LITERATURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

8. **Logistic regression** – Hastie, T., Tibshirani, R., & Friedman, J.
   (2009). *The Elements of Statistical Learning*. Springer.  
   Used for the deal closing probability model.

9. **VAR models** – Lütkepohl, H. (2005). *New Introduction to Multiple Time
   Series Analysis*. Springer.  
   Applied for macroeconomic adjustment of forecasts.

10. **Quantum key distribution (BB84)** – Bennett, C. H., & Brassard, G.
    (1984). “Quantum cryptography: Public key distribution and coin tossing”.
    *Proceedings of IEEE International Conference on Computers, Systems and
    Signal Processing*.  
    Used for quantum encryption simulation in Phase 4.

11. **Post‑quantum cryptography (CRYSTALS-Kyber, CRYSTALS-Dilithium)** –
    NIST specifications.  
    Considered in the security architecture as a potential direction for development.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.7.4. MANAGEMENT AND METHODOLOGY BOOKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

12. **“The Richest Man in Babylon”** – George S. Clason.  
    Used for the principle of investing 10% of income in automation development.

13. **“Capital”** – Karl Marx.  
    Analysis of class structure and crisis cycles for customer segmentation
    and forecasting.

14. **“Entertaining Mathematics”** – Yakov Perelman.  
    Inspiration for simple models of complex systems (health score, account score).

15. **“The Prince”** – Niccolò Machiavelli.  
    Principles of rapid change and self‑reliance in implementing GTM transformation.

16. **MEDDPICC methodology** – official documentation and training materials.  
    Used to structure fields in Salesforce and automate qualification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12.7.5. TOOL DOCUMENTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

17. **Salesforce Documentation** – Apex Developer Guide, Platform Events,
    Process Builder, Flow. Used to implement automation in the CRM.

18. **AWS Documentation** – EventBridge, MSK (Kafka), Lambda, EKS, S3, RDS,
    CloudWatch, IAM. Used to design the infrastructure.

19. **Snowflake Documentation** – SQL, Snowpark, Streams, Tasks. Applied to build
    data marts.

20. **Hightouch Documentation** – Reverse ETL, Webhooks. Used to configure
    synchronisation.

21. **Gainsight Documentation** – API, Playbooks, Health Score. Applied for
    customer success integration.

22. **6sense API Documentation** – REST API, signals. Used to obtain intent data.

23. **SalesLoft API Documentation** – Cadences, People, Activities. Used for
    outreach automation.

24. **Tableau Documentation** – Live connections, Row‑level security,
    Dashboard publishing. Used for visualisation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON REFERENCES AND SOURCES**

- The presented list covers all key sources used in the development of the target
  GTM architecture.
- Sourcegraph corporate materials ensured the relevance of the analysis,
  external sources provided market context, and scientific and technical literature
  provided mathematical rigour for the models.
- Tool documentation enabled the practical implementation of the described architecture.
