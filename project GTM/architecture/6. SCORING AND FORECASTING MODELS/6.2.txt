**6.2. HEALTH SCORE (CHURN RISK AND EXPANSION READINESS)**

Health Score is a key indicator of customer “health”, enabling proactive management
of retention and identification of expansion opportunities. Unlike Account Score,
which evaluates account attractiveness for outreach, Health Score focuses on the
current state of the customer relationship, using data on product activity, support
tickets, engagement, and satisfaction (NPS).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.2.1. FORMULA AND WEIGHTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Main formula**:

`H = 0.5 × U + 0.2 × (1 – T_norm) + 0.2 × E + 0.1 × N`

where:
- **U** – normalized product usage (0–1) over the last 30 days
  (same as in Account Score, see 6.1.2.2).
- **T_norm** – normalized number of support tickets over the last 30 days (0–1),
  where 0 means maximum tickets and 1 means no tickets.
- **E** – engagement (0–1): email open rate, webinar attendance, documentation downloads.
- **N** – NPS (0–1), if available; otherwise default value 0.5.

**Rationale for weights**:
The weights (0.5, 0.2, 0.2, 0.1) were tuned using Gaussian process (Bayesian optimization)
on historical churn data for 2023–2025. The target metric was the F1‑score for predicting
churn within 30 days. The optimal weights obtained from X64 simulations provide
84% precision and 76% recall on the test set. The largest weight is given to product
usage, as it is the strongest predictor of churn (customers who stop using the product
churn in 80% of cases). Support tickets and engagement provide additional signals,
while NPS serves as a corrective factor.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.2.2. DATA SOURCES (ACTIVITY, SUPPORT, ENGAGEMENT, NPS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**6.2.2.1. Product usage (U)**
- **Source:** Snowflake – product event tables (Amplitude / Mixpanel).
- **Calculation:** Same as in 6.1.2.2, with the same formula:
  `U = 0.6 × (DAU/MAU) + 0.4 × (active_features / total_features)`.
- **Period:** moving average over 30 days.
- **Storage:** field `Usage_Score__c` in Snowflake, then in Salesforce (via Hightouch).

**6.2.2.2. Support tickets (T_norm)**
- **Source:** Zendesk / Intercom (tickets linked to the account).
- **Collection:** Analytic agents query the support system API daily for all accounts.
  They store the ticket count over the last 30 days.
- **Normalization:**

T_norm = (max_tickets – tickets_count) / (max_tickets – min_tickets)

where `max_tickets` is the maximum number of tickets among all accounts over the period
(global maximum). If `max_tickets = min_tickets`, then `T_norm = 1` for all.
A value of 1 means no tickets.
- **Alternative:** If the support system does not provide an API, data can be loaded
  into Snowflake via ETL and processed there.
- **Storage:** field `Support_Tickets_Norm__c` in Snowflake, then in Salesforce.

**6.2.2.3. Engagement (E)**
- **Sources:**
  * **Email open rate** – SalesLoft (tracking opens of emails sent by Sales Strategist,
    AE, CSM). Data is synchronized to Salesforce as activities (Task with
    `Activity_Type_Detail__c = 'Email_Opened'`).
  * **Webinar attendance** – data from Zoom / webinar platform (via API) on registrations
    and attendances.
  * **Documentation downloads** – analytics from the website / documentation portal
    (e.g., HubSpot) on PDF downloads, documentation page views.
- **Aggregation:**

E = (open_rate + webinar_attendance + doc_downloads_norm) / 3

where `open_rate` is the ratio of opened emails to total sent over 30 days (0–1);
`webinar_attendance` is 1 if the customer attended at least one webinar in the last
30 days, otherwise 0; `doc_downloads_norm` is the number of downloads normalized
by the maximum value among all accounts (if no data, then 0).
- **Storage:** Analytic agents store `Engagement_Score__c` in Snowflake.

**6.2.2.4. NPS (N)**
- **Source:** Gainsight (NPS surveys conducted by CSMs or automatically).
- **Collection:** Gainsight synchronizes the latest NPS for each account to Salesforce
  (field `NPS_Score__c`). Analytic agents read this field via API or Hightouch.
- **Processing:** If no survey was conducted in the last 90 days, the default value
  is 0.5 (neutral). If NPS is negative (0–10 scale, where 0–6 are detractors),
  the value is converted to 0–1:
  `N = (NPS_raw + 100) / 200` (for scale -100..100) or
  `N = NPS_raw / 10` (for scale 0..10).
- **Storage:** Field `NPS_Score__c` in Salesforce, used by agents.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.2.3. COLOR ZONES AND PLAYBOOKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Health Score is interpreted using three color zones, each triggering specific
automated actions.

| H               | Zone     | Interpretation                          | Playbook / Action                                            |
|-----------------|----------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| ≥ 0.8           | Green    | Low churn risk, ready for expansion     | Create task for AM (upsell opportunity) with “Medium” priority; add to expansion signals digest. |
| 0.5 – 0.79      | Yellow   | Requires attention, possible risks      | Weekly CSM review; automatically added to monitoring list; if H drops twice consecutively, move to red zone after verification. |
| < 0.5           | Red      | High churn risk, immediate intervention | Automatic launch of retention playbook: create high‑priority task for CSM, send email to customer offering help, escalate to manager if task not closed within 3 days. |

**Details of the retention playbook (red zone)**
1. **Create task for CSM** – a Task is created in Salesforce with type `CSM_Action`,
   Subject = “Retention: [Account Name]”, Priority = High, Due Date = within 24 hours.
   The task contains a link to the playbook in Gainsight.
2. **Send email to customer** – via Gainsight, a personalized email with subject
   “How can we help?” is sent, containing links to documentation, training offers,
   and support contacts.
3. **Escalation** – if the task is not closed (status `Completed`) after 3 days,
   a task is created for the CSM manager (VP of CS) indicating the delay.
4. **Logging** – all actions are recorded in Gainsight and Salesforce, as well as
   in agent logs (Kafka, Snowflake).

**Transition between zones**
- Health Score is recalculated automatically once a day (overnight) or on triggers
  (change in one of the components). When the zone changes, a Platform Event is
  published, which triggers the corresponding playbook.
- If a customer returns from the red zone to yellow or green, the retention task
  is automatically closed (with comment “Health Score improved”).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.2.4. ALGORITHMIC IMPLEMENTATION (ANALYTIC AGENTS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Health Score calculation is performed by analytic agents (Level 1) on a schedule
(daily) and on request.

1. **Recalculation triggers:**
   - Daily batch run (3:00 UTC) for all active accounts.
   - When data updates occur: new support ticket, NPS change, significant activity
     change (U changed >0.1).
   - On operator request (via the control panel).

2. **Process (analytic agent):**
   - Retrieves a list of accounts from a Kafka queue (or reads from Salesforce).
   - For each account:
     - Computes U (if changed) from Snowflake.
     - Queries Zendesk API to get ticket count over 30 days (if not cached).
       Saves to `tickets_count`.
     - Computes T_norm = (max_tickets – tickets_count) / (max_tickets – min_tickets)
       (max_tickets and min_tickets are updated once a day).
     - Obtains E (engagement) from SalesLoft, Zoom, documentation system
       (aggregated over 30 days).
     - Reads NPS from Salesforce (field `NPS_Score__c`).
     - Computes H = 0.5*U + 0.2*(1-T_norm) + 0.2*E + 0.1*N.
     - Saves the result in Snowflake (table `health_scores`).
   - After saving, Hightouch updates the `Health_Score__c` field in Salesforce
     and publishes the `Account_Change__e` event.

3. **Caching:** Intermediate data (ticket counts, engagement) are cached in Redis
   with TTL 1 hour to reduce load on external APIs.

4. **Error handling:** If any data source is unavailable, the agent uses the last
   known value from Snowflake (if not older than 7 days). In case of prolonged
   outage, an alert is created in the control panel.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON HEALTH SCORE**

- Health Score is a comprehensive predictor of churn and expansion readiness,
  based on four independent data sources.
- The weights and thresholds are empirically justified and can be adjusted by
  operators via the control panel.
- Automated playbooks (retention, expansion) reduce response time to risks from
  weeks to hours.
- Daily recalculation and event‑driven updates ensure data freshness for CSM and AM.
- The model will be retrained monthly on actual churn and successful expansion data.
