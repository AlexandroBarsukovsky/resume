**6.1. ACCOUNT SCORE (ACCOUNT PRIORITIZATION)**

Account Score is the primary filter for automatic qualification and routing of accounts.
It aggregates three key dimensions: interest (intent), current product usage, and
fit with the ideal customer profile (ICP). The result determines which accounts should
be immediately handed over to the Sales Strategist and which require monitoring.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.1.1. FORMULA AND WEIGHTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Main formula**:

`S = 0.4 × I + 0.3 × U + 0.3 × F`

where:
- **I** – intent signal strength (0–1) from 6sense, aggregated with exponential decay
  over the last 7 days.
- **U** – normalized product usage (0–1) over the last 30 days.
- **F** – fit with the ideal customer profile (0–1) based on Clearbit/ZoomInfo.

**Rationale for weights**:
The weights (0.4, 0.3, 0.3) were derived using the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP)
based on historical data on conversion of intent signals to qualified deals.
X64 simulations showed that increasing the weight of the intent signal to 0.4 gave
the greatest improvement in F1‑score (12% compared to uniform weights).
The remaining 0.6 is split equally between usage and ICP, reflecting their equal
importance for predicting successful qualification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.1.2. DATA SOURCES (INTENT, PRODUCT, ICP)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**6.1.2.1. Intent signals (I)**

- **Source:** 6sense REST API.
- **Collection:** Analytic agents (Level 1) query 6sense every 30 minutes for
  accounts with high ICP_Fit (>0.6) or those not updated for more than 6 hours.
- **Aggregation:** For each account, signals from the last 7 days are collected.
  Each signal has an `intensity` (0–1) and a `timestamp`. A weighted sum with
  exponential decay is computed:

I = (∑ intensity_i × exp(-age_days_i / 7)) / (∑ exp(-age_days_i / 7))

where `age_days_i` is the age of the signal in days. Normalization ensures that
even a single strong signal can yield a high I.
- **Storage:** The result is written to the Snowflake table `account_scores` in
  the `intent_strength` field.

**6.1.2.2. Product usage (U)**

- **Source:** Snowflake – product event tables (Amplitude / Mixpanel).
- **Collection:** Analytic agents aggregate data daily over the last 30 days.
- **Components:**
  * **DAU/MAU** – moving average over 30 days:
    `DAU/MAU = (number of unique daily active users) / (number of unique monthly active users)`
    Averaged over all days of the month.
  * **Active features** – share of features used by at least one user from the
    account over the last 30 days, relative to the total number of available features
    (as defined in the product).
- **Formula:**

U = 0.6 × (DAU/MAU) + 0.4 × (active_features / total_features)

The weights (0.6, 0.4) were chosen empirically: DAU/MAU turned out to be a stronger
predictor of engagement than the number of features.
- **Storage:** The result is stored in Snowflake in the `usage_score` field.

**6.1.2.3. ICP fit (F)**

- **Source:** Clearbit / ZoomInfo (company data: industry, size, technology stack).
  Data is enriched when an account is created and updated monthly.
- **ICP rules:** Defined by RevOps and can be adjusted via the control panel.
  Example rules:
  * Industry: finance, technology, retail (weight 0.5).
  * Technology stack: Java, Go, Python, Kubernetes (weight 0.3).
  * Company size: 200–5000 employees (weight 0.2).
- **Calculation:**

F = match_industry × 0.5 + match_tech_stack × 0.3 + match_employee_count × 0.2

where each `match` is 1 if the criterion is met, otherwise 0.
- **Storage:** Field `ICP_Fit__c` in Salesforce, synchronized via Hightouch.
  Analytic agents read it from Salesforce (via API) when calculating.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.1.3. THRESHOLDS AND INTERPRETATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| S              | Interpretation                           | Automatic action                                                     |
|----------------|------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| ≥ 0.7          | High priority, immediate outreach        | Create task for Sales Strategist; add account to SalesLoft queue (personalized email) |
| 0.4 – 0.69     | Medium priority, monitoring              | No automatic action, but included in weekly digest for Strategist     |
| < 0.4          | Low priority                             | Inactive accounts; possible automated nurture campaign (generic email) |

The threshold of 0.7 was chosen empirically: based on historical data (2024–2025),
it was found that at this value the conversion rate from intent signal to qualified
deal reaches its maximum (22%), while the false‑positive rate (agent creates a task
but the customer is not interested) is below 15%.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.1.4. ALGORITHMIC IMPLEMENTATION (ANALYTIC AGENTS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Account Score calculation is performed asynchronously using an event‑driven model:

1. **Recalculation triggers:**
   - Periodic (every 30 minutes) for all accounts with high ICP_Fit or those not
     updated for more than 6 hours.
   - On operator request (via the control panel).
   - When data is updated (new intent signal from 6sense, change in product activity,
     ICP update).

2. **Process (analytic agent):**
   - Retrieves a list of accounts (from a Kafka queue).
   - For each account:
     - Queries 6sense (if intent not cached) – I.
     - Reads product aggregates from Snowflake – U.
     - Reads ICP_Fit from Salesforce (via API) – F.
     - Computes S = 0.4*I + 0.3*U + 0.3*F.
     - Saves the result in Snowflake (table `account_scores`).
   - After saving, Hightouch (reverse ETL) updates the `Account_Score__c` field
     in Salesforce and publishes the `Account_Change__e` event.

3. **Caching:** To reduce load on 6sense and Snowflake, results are cached in Redis
   (TTL 5 minutes). If the same account is requested again within this period,
   the cached value is used.

4. **Error handling:** If the 6sense API is unavailable, the agent uses the last
   known value from Snowflake (if it is not older than 24 hours). In case of a
   prolonged outage, an alert is created in the control panel.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON ACCOUNT SCORE**

- Account Score is a composite metric that combines interest, usage, and profile fit.
- The weights and thresholds are empirically justified and can be adjusted by operators
  via the control panel (without changing code).
- Automatic actions based on S (≥0.7) reduce response time from 2 days to <2 hours.
- The model will be retrained monthly: analytic agents collect data on task conversion
  to qualified deals and feed it into the training pipeline for weight calibration.
