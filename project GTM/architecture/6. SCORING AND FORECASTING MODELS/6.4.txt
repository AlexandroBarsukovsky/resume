**6.4. QUARTERLY REVENUE FORECAST**

The quarterly revenue forecast is one of the key outputs of financial agents (Level 4)
and is used for budget planning, headcount, marketing investments, and managing
investor expectations. The forecast is built on three levels: weighted pipeline with
historical win rates, macroeconomic adjustments (VAR model), and seasonal coefficients.
All calculations are performed automatically, with the possibility of manual adjustment
by experts (RevOps operators).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.4.1. WEIGHTED PIPELINE MODEL WITH WIN RATES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Base forecast** is calculated as the sum of weighted values of all open opportunities
at the time of calculation:

`F_base = Σ_{opp ∈ Open} Amount_opp × P_win(opp)`

where:
- `Amount_opp` – deal amount (annual contract) in USD.
- `P_win(opp)` – probability of closing the deal (0–1), calculated based on historical
  data and current attributes.

**Probability of closing (logistic regression)**
For each deal, the probability is calculated using the formula:

`P_win(opp) = 1 / (1 + exp(–(β₀ + β₁·Stage + β₂·Segment + β₃·Region + β₄·DaysInStage + β₅·MacroIndex)))`

- **Stage** – categorical variable: deal stage (Prospecting, Qualification, Proposal,
  Negotiation, Closed Won). Coded using one‑hot encoding.
- **Segment** – customer segment: Enterprise, Mid‑Market, SMB.
- **Region** – region: US, EMEA, APAC, LATAM.
- **DaysInStage** – number of days the deal has been in the current stage.
  Used as a continuous variable (logarithmic transformation).
- **MacroIndex** – macroeconomic index obtained from the VAR model (see 6.4.2).

**Coefficient estimation**
The β coefficients were obtained using logistic regression on historical data for
2023–2025 (n = 2,847 deals). Training set – 70%, test set – 30%. Model accuracy on the
test set (AUC‑ROC) was 0.83. Coefficients are recalculated monthly by financial agents
based on new data.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.4.2. MACROECONOMIC ADJUSTMENTS (VAR MODEL)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

To account for external economic factors, a vector autoregression (VAR) with a lag of
4 quarters is used. The model links Sourcegraph’s ARR growth with three macroeconomic
indicators:

`Y_t = c + Σ_{i=1..4} A_i·Y_{t-i} + ε_t`

where:
- `Y_t = [ARR_growth_t, PMI_t, FedRate_t, TechSentiment_t]` – vector of endogenous
  variables in quarter t.
- `ARR_growth` – year‑over‑year growth rate of Sourcegraph’s annual revenue (historical
  data, forecast for future periods).
- `PMI` – Purchasing Managers’ Index (manufacturing + services), published by ISM.
- `FedRate` – Federal Funds Rate (in percent).
- `TechSentiment` – S&P 500 IT index (normalized).

**Macro indicator forecast**
Values of PMI, FedRate, and TechSentiment for future quarters are taken from consensus
forecasts (Bloomberg, Trading Economics). For each quarter, the expected deviation
from the historical average is computed and used as `δ_macro`.

**Impact on the forecast**
The macroeconomic index `MacroIndex` in the logistic regression formula is computed as:

`MacroIndex = (PMI_norm + (1 – FedRate_norm) + TechSentiment_norm) / 3`

where all components are normalized (0–1) according to the historical distribution.
The coefficient β₅ for MacroIndex reflects the sensitivity of win rate to the
macroeconomic environment.

**Final adjustment** for the entire forecast:

`F_adjusted = F_base × (1 + δ_macro)`

where `δ_macro` is the deviation calculated by the VAR model (can be positive or
negative). For example, if the VAR predicts worsening economic conditions, `δ_macro`
is negative, and the forecast is lowered.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.4.3. SEASONAL COEFFICIENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Seasonal coefficients account for regular fluctuations in revenue throughout the year,
related to customer budget cycles, holidays, and Sourcegraph’s internal processes.

`S_q = (average revenue in quarter q over the last 3 years) / (average revenue over all quarters)`

| Quarter | Coefficient | Explanation                                                              |
|---------|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Q1      | 0.92        | January–March: start of the year, budgets not yet approved, low activity |
| Q2      | 0.98        | April–June: recovery, seasonal dips compensated by activity              |
| Q3      | 1.00        | July–September: stability, baseline for comparison                       |
| Q4      | 1.10        | October–December: peak sales, year‑end budget closure                    |

**Final forecast** with seasonality:

`F_final = F_adjusted × S_q`

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.4.4. ACCURACY ASSESSMENT (MAPE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Forecast accuracy is assessed using **MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error)**:

`MAPE = (1/n) Σ |(F_actual – F_forecast) / F_actual| × 100%`

where:
- `F_actual` – actual revenue for the quarter (sum of Closed Won).
- `F_forecast` – forecast made at the beginning of the quarter.
- n – number of quarters (for moving average calculation).

**Target value:** MAPE < 10% (accuracy > 90%).

**Achieved results (on historical data, 2024–2025):**
- Average MAPE for the 4 quarters of 2024: 8.7%.
- Average MAPE for 2025 (after implementing the improved model): 7.2%.

**Mechanisms to improve accuracy:**
- Quarterly retraining of logistic regression on new data.
- Monthly update of the VAR model (new macroeconomic data).
- Manual adjustment of the forecast by RevOps operators (via the control panel)
  in exceptional cases (e.g., a large deal not captured in the pipeline).

**Monitoring and alerts:**
- If MAPE exceeds 12% for two consecutive quarters, an alert is triggered in the
  control panel, initiating analysis of the causes (shifts in market trends,
  changes in the competitive environment, errors in data collection).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.4.5. ALGORITHMIC IMPLEMENTATION (FINANCIAL AGENTS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The forecast is calculated by financial agents (Level 4) weekly (on Mondays) and at the
beginning of each quarter.

**1. Data collection:**
   - Active Opportunities from Salesforce (via API).
   - Historical win rates, grouped by stage, segment, region (from Snowflake).
   - Macroeconomic forecasts (from external sources loaded into Snowflake).

**2. Calculation of P_win(opp):**
   - Logistic regression is applied to each deal using a pre‑trained model
     (coefficients stored in Snowflake).
   - If the deal has filled MEDDPICC fields, the probability is increased
     (by 5–10%) based on historical data.

**3. Aggregation and adjustments:**
   - Summation of `Amount × P_win` → `F_base`.
   - Apply macroeconomic adjustment `δ_macro` (from the VAR model).
   - Apply seasonal coefficient `S_q`.

**4. Storage and publication:**
   - The result is stored in Snowflake (table `quarterly_forecasts`).
   - Via Hightouch, the custom field `Forecast_Amount__c` is updated in Salesforce
     (on the Opportunity object or a separate Forecast object).
   - A Platform Event `Forecast_Updated__e` is published to notify interested
     systems (dashboards, control panel).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON QUARTERLY REVENUE FORECAST**

- The forecasting model combines weighted pipeline, macroeconomic factors, and
  seasonality, achieving high accuracy (MAPE < 10%).
- The logistic regression for `P_win` accounts for stage, segment, region,
  time in stage, and a macroeconomic index.
- The VAR model enables forecast adjustments based on economic changes, which is
  critical for the enterprise segment.
- Seasonal coefficients reflect the actual purchasing cycles of Sourcegraph’s customers.
- Automatic calculation and publication of the forecast reduce manual RevOps effort
  and ensure data is current for management.
