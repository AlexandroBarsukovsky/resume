**6. SCORING AND FORECASTING MODELS**

Scoring and forecasting models form the “computational core” of the target GTM
architecture. They transform raw data (intent signals, product activity, support
interactions) into management decisions: whom to call, whom to retain, whom to offer
expansion, and how much revenue to expect in the future. All models were developed
based on analysis of historical patterns (X16), agent holding simulations (X64),
and quantum hyperparameter superposition (protocol D1), allowing optimal weights
and thresholds to be selected.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.1. ACCOUNT SCORE (ACCOUNT PRIORITIZATION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**6.1.1. Formula and weights**

Account Score (S) is computed as a weighted sum of three components:

`S = 0.4 × I + 0.3 × U + 0.3 × F`

where:
- **I** – intent signal strength (0–1) from 6sense, aggregated over the last 7 days
  with exponential decay;
- **U** – normalized product usage (0–1), calculated based on DAU/MAU and the number
  of active features;
- **F** – fit with the ideal customer profile (0–1) based on Clearbit/ZoomInfo.

The weights (0.4, 0.3, 0.3) were determined using the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP)
based on historical data on conversion of intent signals to qualified deals.
X64 simulations showed that increasing the weight of the intent signal to 0.4 gave
the greatest improvement in prioritization accuracy (F1‑score +12% compared to uniform weights).

**6.1.2. Data sources**

| Component | Source                         | Details                                                                 |
|-----------|--------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| I         | 6sense (intent signals)        | Signals over the last 7 days, weighted exponentially: `weight = exp(-age_days/7)`. Aggregated by analytic agents. |
| U         | Snowflake (product events)     | `U = 0.6 × (DAU/MAU) + 0.4 × (active_features / total_features)`. DAU/MAU – moving average over 30 days. |
| F         | Clearbit / ZoomInfo            | `F = match(industry, target_industries)×0.5 + match(technologies, target_stack)×0.3 + match(employee_count_range)×0.2`. ICP rules defined by RevOps. |

**6.1.3. Thresholds and interpretation**

| S              | Interpretation                           | Automatic action                                              |
|----------------|------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| ≥ 0.7          | High priority, immediate outreach        | Create task for Sales Strategist; add account to SalesLoft queue |
| 0.4 – 0.69     | Medium priority, monitoring              | No automatic action, but included in weekly digest            |
| < 0.4          | Low priority                             | Inactive accounts; possible automated nurture campaign        |

The threshold of 0.7 was chosen empirically: D1 simulations showed that at this value
the conversion rate from intent signal to qualified deal reaches its maximum (22%),
while the false‑positive rate (agent creates a task but the customer is not interested)
is below 15%.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.2. HEALTH SCORE (CHURN RISK AND EXPANSION READINESS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**6.2.1. Formula and weights**

Health Score (H) is a composite indicator from 0 to 1, reflecting customer health.
It is calculated as:

`H = 0.5 × U + 0.2 × (1 – T_norm) + 0.2 × E + 0.1 × N`

where:
- **U** – product usage (see 6.1.2);
- **T_norm** – normalized number of support tickets over the last 30 days
  (max – the maximum value among all customers);
- **E** – engagement (0–1): email open rate, webinar attendance, documentation downloads;
- **N** – NPS (0–1), if available (otherwise 0.5).

The weights (0.5, 0.2, 0.2, 0.1) were tuned using Gaussian process (Bayesian optimization)
on historical churn data. The target metric was the F1‑score for predicting churn within
30 days. The optimal weights obtained from X64 simulations provide 84% precision and
76% recall (on the test set).

**6.2.2. Data sources**

| Component | Source                         | Details                                                                 |
|-----------|--------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| U         | Snowflake                      | Same as 6.1.2                                                          |
| T_norm    | Zendesk / Intercom             | Number of tickets over 30 days, normalized by the max among all clients |
| E         | SalesLoft / Gainsight          | `E = (email open rate + webinar attendance + downloads) / 3`            |
| N         | Gainsight (NPS surveys)        | If no survey in the last 90 days, default value 0.5                     |

**6.2.3. Color zones and playbooks**

| H               | Zone     | Interpretation                          | Playbook / Action                                            |
|-----------------|----------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| ≥ 0.8           | Green    | Low churn risk, ready for expansion     | Create task for AM (upsell opportunity)                       |
| 0.5 – 0.79      | Yellow   | Requires attention, possible risks      | Weekly CSM review; priority monitoring                         |
| < 0.5           | Red      | High churn risk, immediate intervention | Automatic launch of retention playbook (task for CSM, email to customer) |

Transition from yellow to red zone (or vice versa) publishes a Platform Event,
which triggers a review of the customer’s history and, if necessary, escalation to management.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.3. EXPANSION SIGNALS (SIGNALS FOR UPSELL/CROSS‑SELL)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**6.3.1. Trigger conditions**

Expansion Signal is a boolean flag set to `true` if at least one of the following
conditions is met:

1. **High activity and user growth**  
   `U > 0.7` and `(new_users_last_30d / users_previous_30d – 1) > 0.3`

2. **Request for additional functionality**  
   The customer requested a demo or submitted a feature request (field `Feature_Request__c` on Account or activity in Gainsight).

3. **Contract renewal with increased volume**  
   In a renewal‑type Opportunity, the new amount > 110% of the previous contract amount.

Conditions 1 and 2 are checked by analytic agents (Level 1) weekly.
Condition 3 is checked when a renewal Opportunity is created.

**6.3.2. Automatic actions**

When `Expansion_Signal__c = true`, the following actions are performed:

- Create a high‑priority task for the AM (Account Manager);
- Prepare a commercial proposal (via financial agents) based on current consumption
  and requested features;
- Update the `Expansion_Score__c` field (0–1), which reflects the likelihood of
  successful expansion (calculated by logistic regression on historical data).

The CS agent (Level 4) may also initiate the “Expansion” playbook through Gainsight,
which includes a series of communications with the customer and coordination with the technical team.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.4. QUARTERLY REVENUE FORECAST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**6.4.1. Weighted pipeline model with win rates**

The base forecast for quarterly revenue is the sum of weighted values of all open
opportunities:

`F_base = Σ_{opp ∈ Open} Amount_opp × P_win(opp)`

where `P_win(opp)` – probability of closing the deal, determined by the formula:

`P_win(opp) = 1 / (1 + exp(–(β₀ + β₁·Stage + β₂·Segment + β₃·Region + β₄·DaysInStage + β₅·MacroIndex)))`

The β coefficients were obtained using logistic regression on historical data for
2023–2025 (with training and test splits).

**6.4.2. Macroeconomic adjustments (VAR model)**

To account for external factors, a VAR (Vector Autoregression) model linking ARR growth
with macroeconomic indicators is used:

`Y_t = c + Σ_{i=1..4} A_i·Y_{t-i} + ε_t`  
`Y = [ARR_growth, PMI, FedRate, TechSentiment]`

- **PMI** – Purchasing Managers’ Index (manufacturing + services);
- **FedRate** – Federal Funds Rate;
- **TechSentiment** – S&P 500 IT index.

Forecast values for the indicators are taken from consensus forecasts (Bloomberg,
Trading Economics). The model is recalculated monthly by financial agents, and its
results are used to adjust `P_win(opp)` (coefficient β₅) and to compute seasonal coefficients.

**6.4.3. Seasonal coefficients**

Quarterly seasonal coefficients `S_q` are calculated based on data from closed deals
over the last 3 years:

`S_q = (average revenue in quarter q) / (average revenue over all quarters)`

Typical for Sourcegraph:
- Q4 – peak (1.1), Q1 – low (0.92), Q2 – recovery (0.98), Q3 – stable (1.0).

Final forecast:  
`F_final = F_base × S_q × (1 + δ_macro)`

where `δ_macro` is the deviation calculated by the VAR model (can be positive or negative).

**6.4.4. Accuracy assessment (MAPE)**

Forecast accuracy is assessed using MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error):

`MAPE = (1/n) Σ |(F_actual – F_forecast) / F_actual| × 100%`

Target value – **MAPE < 10%**. Quarterly, financial agents compare the forecast with
actual revenue and retrain the model by adding new data to the regression. If MAPE
exceeds 12% for two consecutive quarters, an alert is triggered in the control panel,
and RevOps operators analyze the causes (market trend shifts, changes in the competitive environment).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON SCORING AND FORECASTING MODELS**

- All models are empirically justified: weights, thresholds, and coefficients were
  obtained based on historical data and simulations.
- They are integrated into the agent architecture: calculations are performed by agents
  (Level 1 and Level 4), results are synchronized with Salesforce via Hightouch.
- Automatic actions based on scoring are iteratively improved through operator feedback
  (models retrained monthly).
- The forecasting model, which includes macroeconomic factors, provides the high accuracy
  needed for resource and investment planning.
