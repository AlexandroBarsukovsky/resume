Project: Sourcegraph GTM Infrastructure

**1.1. CONTEXT AND OBJECTIVE**
Sourcegraph, a leader in code intelligence (Code Search, Deep Search, MCP platforms), is undergoing a major GTM transformation: shifting from product‑led growth (PLG) to enterprise sales. The product is already used by major technology companies (Stripe, Uber, Palo Alto Networks), but internal operational processes do not meet the requirements of the enterprise segment. Fragmented data, manual account routing, the absence of accurate forecasts, and unautomated customer experience management hinder growth, increase operational costs, and create risks for customer retention.

This document provides a complete architectural specification of the target GTM infrastructure, developed based on analysis of the current state, historical management patterns, multi‑variant simulations, and mathematical modeling. The goal is to build a scalable, event‑driven GTM engine that will deliver:
- automation of routine operations,
- improved forecasting accuracy,
- proactive customer experience management,
- sustainable ARR growth while reducing operational costs.

**1.2. METHODOLOGY: METICULOUS DESIGN FROM MICRO TO MACRO LEVEL**
The architecture follows a “depth‑to‑form” principle: every level of detail, from atomic events to the global market, is reflected in the technical implementation.

- **Atomic events** (intent signals, product clicks) → the foundation of the Data Fabric: all data is captured as indivisible units.
- **Business scenarios** (intent → qualification → meeting) → three analytical dimensions (intent, product usage, ICP fit).
- **Unified customer profile** (Account) → centralized storage in Salesforce of all attributes and interaction history.
- **Customer as an atom** → standardized interface via CRM, API, and playbooks.
- **Teams within a customer** → role‑based segmentation for personalized communication.
- **Industry specialization** → tailored GTM strategies for finance, technology, retail, manufacturing.
- **Partner ecosystem** → sustainable layers of integrators, consultants, technology partners.
- **Sourcegraph business unit** → core (platform), mantle (GTM), bark (community), atmosphere (brand).
- **Product portfolio** → unified platform with three core solutions.
- **Brand** → creating gravitational pull, reducing CAC.
- **Developer community** → self‑sustaining ecosystem.
- **Code intelligence market** → dominance in the segment.
- **Strategic alliances** (GitHub, GitLab, cloud providers) → strengthening market positions.
- **Software development industry** → influence on standards.
- **Data, capital, talent networks** → intersecting flows that enable scaling.
- **Global market** → recognition across all segments.
- **Scenario multiverse** → continuous foresight to adapt to uncertainty.

This approach ensures that every architectural decision is justified at all levels of detail, from micro‑events to global strategy.

**1.4. KEY ARCHITECTURE ELEMENTS**
The proposed architecture is based on four‑level orchestration with hybrid governance (people + AI agents):

- **Level 1 – mass analysis (1,024 analyst agents)**  
  Data collection and enrichment: intent signals (6sense), product events (Snowflake), enrichment (Clearbit/ZoomInfo). Account scoring formula:  
  `S = 0.4·Intent + 0.3·Usage + 0.3·ICP_Fit`.  
  Identification of risks and priority accounts.

- **Level 2 – strategic ideologies (3 agents)**  
  Conservative, balanced, aggressive resource allocation scenarios. Ideology selection based on customer profile and business objectives.

- **Level 3 – tactical groups (96 groups)**  
  Specialization by industry, region, customer size. Adaptation of messaging, channels, pricing.

- **Level 4 – execution cluster (3,000 agents)**  
  – 500 agents – intent signal processing.  
  – 800 agents – funnel management (creating/updating Opportunities, demo tasks, follow‑ups).  
  – 700 agents – customer success (health monitoring, playbook execution).  
  – 600 agents – financial operations (commission calculation, forecasts, plan‑to‑actual).  
  – 400 agents – reporting and dashboards (QBR, board‑pack).

- **Event‑driven automation**  
  Triggers: Account Score >0.7 → Sales Strategist task; Health Score <0.5 → retention playbook; Pipeline Coverage <3× → CRO alert, etc.

- **Scoring and forecasting models**  
  **Health Score** = 0.5·Usage + 0.2·(1‑Support) + 0.2·Engagement + 0.1·NPS.  
  **Quarterly forecast** = Σ(Value_opp × P_win(opp)) × S_season,  
  where P_win is logistic regression considering stage, segment, region, and macroeconomic factors (VAR model with lags up to 4 quarters).

- **Integration with existing stack**  
  Salesforce (CRM), Snowflake (warehouse), Hightouch (reverse ETL), 6sense (intent), SalesLoft (outreach), Gainsight (CS), Tableau (BI). All integrations are built on APIs and event streaming (Salesforce Platform Events + AWS EventBridge).

**1.6. IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (4 PHASES)**
- **Phase 1 (1–2 months): pilot**  
  Launch of analyst agents and 200 executors. Integration with 6sense, Snowflake, Salesforce. Automatic task creation.  
  *Expected KPIs:* reaction time ≤2 h, win rate +15%, forecast accuracy >85%.

- **Phase 2 (3–4 months): expansion**  
  Add strategist agent and dispatcher. Full automation of account distribution. Expand cluster to 800 agents.  
  *Expected KPIs:* pipeline coverage >3×, deal cycle reduction by 20%, accuracy >88%.

- **Phase 3 (5–6 months): scaling**  
  Launch CS agents (700), integrate with Gainsight, Tableau, deploy operator console.  
  *Expected KPIs:* net retention >120%, green zone health score >80%, time savings of 20 h/month on reporting.

- **Phase 4 (7–12 months): optimization**  
  Model retraining (every 3 months), quantum encryption implementation, foresight sessions.  
  *Expected KPIs:* forecast accuracy >92%, time to insight <1 h, ROI >300%.
