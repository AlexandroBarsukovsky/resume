**7.1. HEADCOUNT MODEL (AE, CSM, AM, REVOPS STAFFING)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1.1. PURPOSE AND CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The headcount model determines the optimal number of key GTM roles (Account Executive,
Customer Success Manager, Account Manager, Revenue Operations) to achieve target
ARR, net retention, and win rate given budget constraints. The model is an integral
part of annual planning (see 7.4) and is recalculated quarterly based on current
revenue forecasts (Section 6.4) and team performance data.

**Key principles:**
- **Data‑driven:** all calculations are based on historical metrics (performance,
  hiring time, ramp‑up) and forecast models.
- **Automation:** financial agents (Level 4) update the calculations monthly,
  providing recommendations to RevOps and management.
- **Flexibility:** RevOps operators can adjust coefficients (e.g., CSM service ratio)
  via the control panel.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1.2. INPUT DATA AND METRICS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Metric                                      | Symbol    | Source                                  | Update Frequency    |
|---------------------------------------------|-----------|-----------------------------------------|---------------------|
| Target ARR (new customers)                  | ARR_new   | Strategic OKRs / forecast               | Year / Quarter      |
| Target ARR from expansion                   | ARR_exp   | Strategic OKRs / forecast               | Year / Quarter      |
| Average AE productivity (ARR/year)          | AE_prod   | Historical data (Salesforce)            | Monthly             |
| Win rate (enterprise)                       | WR        | Historical data (Salesforce)            | Monthly             |
| Average sales cycle length (days)           | Cycle     | Historical data (Salesforce)            | Monthly             |
| Number of active enterprise customers       | N_cust    | Salesforce (Account)                    | Monthly             |
| CSM service ratio (customers per CSM)       | CSM_ratio | Industry benchmark / history            | Configurable        |
| AM workload ratio (customers per AM)        | AM_ratio  | Industry benchmark / history            | Configurable        |
| Number of active agents (in the holding)    | N_agents  | Agent system monitoring                 | Daily               |
| RevOps support ratio (agents per RevOps)    | RevOps_ratio | Complexity estimate / experience      | Configurable        |

All metrics are stored in Snowflake (data marts) and accessible to financial agents
via Snowpark.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1.3. CALCULATION FORMULAS BY ROLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**7.1.3.1. Account Executive (AE)**

The number of AEs needed to achieve the target ARR from new customers:

`AE_needed = (ARR_new_target / AE_prod) × (1 / (1 – vacancy_rate))`

where:
- `ARR_new_target` – target annual ARR from new customers (excluding expansion).
- `AE_prod` – average annual productivity of one AE (historical, adjusted for
  expected win rate and cycle length). Detailed formula:
  `AE_prod = (average deal size) × (number of closed deals per year)`
- `vacancy_rate` – vacancy factor (usually 5–10%) accounting for hiring and ramp‑up time.

Example (hypothetical):
- ARR_new_target = $30M
- AE_prod = $2.5M (average for enterprise AEs)
- vacancy_rate = 0.1
  `AE_needed = (30 / 2.5) × (1 / 0.9) = 12 × 1.111 ≈ 13.3` → 14 AEs.

**7.1.3.2. Customer Success Manager (CSM)**

The number of CSMs is determined by the number of active enterprise customers
and the service ratio:

`CSM_needed = N_cust_enterprise / CSM_ratio`

where:
- `N_cust_enterprise` – number of customers requiring a dedicated CSM
  (usually enterprise segment, possibly also mid‑market with high risk).
- `CSM_ratio` – service ratio, e.g., 50–100 customers per CSM
  (depending on product complexity and lifecycle stage).

Example:
- N_cust_enterprise = 400
- CSM_ratio = 80
  `CSM_needed = 400 / 80 = 5` CSMs.

**7.1.3.3. Account Manager (AM)**

The number of AMs is calculated based on the number of customers ready for expansion
(or in the “green” Health Score zone) and the AM workload ratio:

`AM_needed = N_cust_expansion_ready / AM_ratio`

where:
- `N_cust_expansion_ready` – number of customers with `Health_Score__c ≥ 0.8`
  and/or with active expansion signals.
- `AM_ratio` – workload ratio, e.g., 100–150 customers per AM.

Example:
- N_cust_expansion_ready = 300
- AM_ratio = 120
  `AM_needed = 300 / 120 = 2.5` → 3 AMs.

**7.1.3.4. Revenue Operations (RevOps)**

The number of RevOps staff depends on the complexity of automation, the number of agents,
and the volume of integrations:

`RevOps_needed = (N_agents × complexity_factor + N_integrations × 0.1) / RevOps_ratio`

where:
- `N_agents` – total number of agents in the holding (see Section 4.2).
- `complexity_factor` – complexity factor (e.g., 0.05 for simple agents,
  0.1 for complex ones).
- `N_integrations` – number of external systems (Salesforce, 6sense, Gainsight, …).
- `RevOps_ratio` – support ratio: how many agents can one RevOps support
  (e.g., 50–100).

Example:
- N_agents = 3000
- complexity_factor = 0.07 (average)
- N_integrations = 7
- RevOps_ratio = 80
  `RevOps_needed = (3000 × 0.07 + 7 × 0.1) / 80 = (210 + 0.7) / 80 ≈ 2.63` → 3 RevOps.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1.4. AUTOMATION AND THE ROLE OF AGENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Financial agents (Level 4)** perform the following steps monthly:
  1. Extract target OKRs (ARR_new_target, ARR_exp_target) from the planning system
     (e.g., from a custom object in Salesforce).
  2. Query historical performance data (AE_prod, CSM_ratio, …) from Snowflake.
  3. Calculate the required headcount using the formulas above.
  4. Save the results in Snowflake (table `headcount_forecast`).
  5. Via Hightouch, update fields in Salesforce (e.g., on the `Plan__c` object)
     and publish an event to notify RevOps.

- **The control panel** displays the current headcount, forecasted demand,
  and the difference. RevOps operators can adjust coefficients (CSM_ratio,
  AM_ratio, vacancy_rate) and rerun the calculation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1.5. EXAMPLE FINAL CALCULATION (Q1, 2027)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Role      | Formula                                      | Result (rounded) |
|-----------|----------------------------------------------|------------------|
| AE        | (ARR_new=30M / AE_prod=2.5M) × (1/(1-0.1))  | 14               |
| CSM       | N_cust=400 / CSM_ratio=80                    | 5                |
| AM        | N_exp=300 / AM_ratio=120                     | 3                |
| RevOps    | (3000×0.07 + 7×0.1) / 80                     | 3                |

**Total headcount:** 25 people (excluding managers, who are considered separately).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1.6. CALIBRATION AND ADJUSTMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Quarterly:** actual metrics (ARR attainment, AE productivity, number of customers)
  are compared to the forecast. If the deviation exceeds 15%, the model is recalculated
  with new parameters.
- **Annually:** coefficients (CSM_ratio, AM_ratio, vacancy_rate) are reviewed based on
  industry benchmarks and feedback from management.
- **Manual adjustment:** RevOps operators can temporarily increase headcount for
  strategic initiatives (e.g., launch of a new product) via the control panel,
  with comments recorded.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON THE HEADCOUNT MODEL**

- The model provides objective, data‑driven planning of key GTM role headcount,
  aligned with revenue forecasts and strategic goals.
- Automation through financial agents reduces the RevOps workload and increases
  calculation accuracy.
- Flexible parameter adjustment allows the model to adapt to business changes
  without rewriting code.
