**7.3. COMPENSATION MODEL (BASE + VARIABLE, OPTIMAL CONTRACT)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.3.1. PURPOSE AND KEY PRINCIPLES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The compensation model is designed to incentivize GTM employees (AE, CSM, AM, RevOps)
to achieve company goals, ensuring fair compensation, talent retention, and alignment
with Sourcegraph’s culture (high agency, transparency).

**Key principles:**
- **Transparency:** all compensation rules (base, variable, multipliers) are openly
  documented and accessible to every employee.
- **Tied to results:** the variable component directly depends on key metrics
  (ARR, net retention, win rate, forecast accuracy, health score).
- **Balance of risk and motivation:** the structure should encourage long‑term
  success rather than short‑term risk (adjustments for chargebacks and customer
  experience are used).
- **Competitiveness:** target total compensation (base + variable at plan attainment)
  is set above market average to attract and retain top talent.
- **Automation:** commission and bonus calculations are performed by financial agents
  (Level 4) monthly, with RevOps verification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.3.2. COMPENSATION STRUCTURE BY ROLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**7.3.2.1. Account Executive (AE)**

| Component               | Description                                                                  |
|-------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Base salary**         | Fixed portion, determined by level (M3) and market data.                     |
| **Variable component**  | Commission on revenue from closed deals (typically 5–15% of annual contract).|
| **Quota**               | Target ARR per quarter / year. Set based on territory potential.             |
| **Accelerators**        | When quota is exceeded, the commission rate increases (e.g., 1.5× on amounts above 100% of quota). |
| **Adjustments**         | Chargebacks – if a customer churns within the first year, the commission is reversed. |

**AE monthly commission formula:**

Commission = Σ (ARR_closed × rate) × multiplier

where:
- `ARR_closed` – annual revenue of the closed deal.
- `rate` – base commission rate (e.g., 10%).
- `multiplier` – acceleration factor: 1.0 for deals up to 100% of quota, 1.5 for amounts exceeding quota.

**Payment frequency:** monthly (upon deal close).

**7.3.2.2. Customer Success Manager (CSM)**

| Component               | Description                                                                  |
|-------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Base salary**         | Fixed portion.                                                               |
| **Variable component**  | Bonus tied to net retention (NRR) and health score.                          |
| **Goals**               | NRR >120%, share of customers in the green zone (>80%).                      |
| **Adjustments**         | Bonus may be reduced if churn is high in the CSM’s portfolio.                |

**CSM quarterly bonus formula:**

Bonus = Base_Bonus × (0.6 × (NRR_achieved / NRR_target) + 0.4 × (Green_Zone_achieved / Green_Zone_target))

where `Base_Bonus` is the target bonus at plan attainment (e.g., 20% of annual base).

**7.3.2.3. Account Manager (AM)**

| Component               | Description                                                                  |
|-------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Base salary**         | Fixed portion.                                                               |
| **Variable component**  | Commission on expansion ARR (upsell/cross‑sell) and renewal bonus.           |
| **Goals**               | Expansion ARR (target), renewal rate (>90%).                                 |

**Formula:**

Commission = Σ (Expansion_ARR × rate) + (Renewal_Bonus × renewal_rate_factor)

**7.3.2.4. Revenue Operations (RevOps)**

| Component               | Description                                                                  |
|-------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Base salary**         | Fixed portion.                                                               |
| **Variable component**  | Bonus tied to forecast accuracy (MAPE), report preparation time, and team satisfaction. |
| **Goals**               | MAPE <10%, QBR preparation time <2 hours, internal NPS >80.                  |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.3.3. OPTIMAL CONTRACT (PRINCIPAL‑AGENT THEORY)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

For AEs, a **linear commission without a cap** is used, which aligns with principal‑agent
theory under the assumption that the agent cannot influence deal risk. Formally:

`U_AE = E[bonus] - ½·ρ·Var(bonus) - c(e)`

where:
- `E[bonus]` – expected bonus, linearly dependent on effort e.
- `ρ` – coefficient of risk aversion (determined empirically).
- `c(e) = θ·e²/2` – cost of effort.

The optimal (linear) contract minimizes agency costs while satisfying participation (IR)
and incentive compatibility (IC) constraints. The coefficients (`rate`, `quota`) are
calibrated based on historical AE performance data and their risk preferences.

**Practical calibration:**
- Quota is set at a level achievable with average effort (70th percentile of historical performance).
- The base rate is calculated so that the target total compensation (OTE) matches the
  market, with variable component making up 30–50% of OTE.
- Accelerators kick in after 100% quota attainment, encouraging over‑achievement without a cap.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.3.4. AUTOMATION OF CALCULATION (FINANCIAL AGENTS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Commission and bonus calculations are fully automated:

1. **Data collection:**
   - Closed deals (Closed Won) from Salesforce, with AE, amount, date.
   - Net retention, health score, expansion ARR – from Snowflake (aggregates).
   - Goals (quotas) – from the custom object `Sales_Quota__c` (loaded at the beginning of the period).

2. **Calculation:**
   - Financial agents (Level 4) run the calculation monthly using the formulas above.
   - Results are stored in Snowflake (table `commission_calculations`).

3. **Verification and publication:**
   - A summary is sent to the control panel for RevOps operator review.
   - After confirmation, the results are synchronized with Salesforce (object `Commission_Statement__c`)
     and the payroll system (via API or export).

4. **Feedback:**
   - Each AE can view their calculation in Salesforce (via a custom Lightning component).
   - A “Dispute” button creates a task for RevOps.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.3.5. EXAMPLE CALCULATION FOR AE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Input data:**
- Quarterly quota: $500,000 ARR.
- Base rate: 10%.
- Accelerator: 1.5× on amounts above quota.

**Performance:**
- Closed deals during the quarter: $600,000 ARR.
- Commission:
  * On quota: $500,000 × 10% = $50,000.
  * On excess: $100,000 × 10% × 1.5 = $15,000.
- **Total commission:** $65,000.

**Total compensation (assuming base salary $120,000/year):**
- Base for the quarter: $30,000.
- Commission: $65,000.
- Total for the quarter: $95,000.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.3.6. ADJUSTMENT MECHANISMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Chargebacks:** if a customer brought in by an AE churns within 12 months,
  the commission for that deal is reversed (deducted from future payments). This
  encourages customer quality over quantity.
- **Customer experience quality:** for CSM and AM, part of the bonus may be adjusted
  based on NPS or health score to prevent “turning a blind eye” to problems.
- **Team bonuses:** for RevOps and managers, part of the variable payout may depend
  on company‑wide metrics (ARR, NRR) to promote collaboration.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON THE COMPENSATION MODEL**

- The model ensures fair and transparent compensation that incentivizes achievement
  of strategic goals.
- Differentiation by role (AE – commission, CSM/AM – bonuses tied to retention/expansion)
  reflects the specifics of each function.
- Using principal‑agent theory allows optimal calibration of linear contracts
  (quotas, rates, accelerators).
- Automated calculation by financial agents reduces the RevOps workload and eliminates
  manual data entry errors.
