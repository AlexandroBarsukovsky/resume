**7.2. TERRITORY DESIGN (MARKET SPLIT AMONG AEs)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.2.1. PURPOSE AND PRINCIPLES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Territory design defines how the market (accounts and opportunities) is distributed
among Account Executives (AEs). The goal is to ensure balanced workload, minimize
conflicts, maximize market coverage, and create conditions for quota attainment.

**Key principles:**
- **Fairness:** each AE receives a pool of accounts with comparable potential.
- **Scalability:** the design must easily adapt to changes in AE headcount,
  regional expansion, and the emergence of new segments.
- **Transparency:** account assignment rules are documented and accessible
  to all AEs (via the handbook and Salesforce interface).
- **Flexibility:** RevOps operators can make adjustments in exceptional cases
  (strategic accounts, temporary redistribution).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.2.2. SPLIT CRITERIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Market splitting is performed along three main dimensions, which can be combined
depending on market size and region maturity.

**1. Geographic split (primary level)**
- **Regions:** US (further split into East, West, Central), EMEA (EMEA North, EMEA South),
  APAC, LATAM.
- Within a region – by country or groups of countries.
- For the US, additional splitting by state or metro areas may be used.

**2. Vertical split (for large markets)**
- By industry: finance, technology, retail, manufacturing, healthcare.
- By customer size: named accounts (enterprise), mid‑market (by territory),
  SMB (self‑service, no AE).

**3. Account‑based split (for top customers)**
- The largest customers (e.g., Fortune 500) are assigned to specific AEs
  (named accounts) regardless of geography. This ensures personal attention
  and deep understanding of their needs.

**Combination rules:**
- In the US: geographic split first, then vertical specialists within each region
  (e.g., a finance AE on the East Coast).
- In EMEA and APAC: primarily geographic split (due to language and cultural diversity),
  with named accounts for global customers.
- In LATAM: a single region with several AEs covering the entire territory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.2.3. TOOLS AND AUTOMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Salesforce Territory Management** is the primary tool for account and deal assignment.
The following rules are configured:

- **Territory hierarchy:** a tree is created corresponding to geographic and vertical
  splits. Each territory is assigned one or more AEs (with primary / secondary roles).
- **Automatic assignment:** when a new account is created (or its attributes change,
  such as country, industry), Salesforce automatically determines the territory based
  on the rules and assigns the owner (OwnerId).
- **Escalation:** if an account cannot be automatically assigned (e.g., country not
  specified), it goes into a queue for manual assignment by a RevOps operator.

**Role of agents:**
- **Analytic agents (Level 1)** monitor AE workload (number of accounts, pipeline value,
  number of active deals). When a threshold is exceeded (e.g., >120% of average load),
  an event is published for RevOps.
- **Financial agents (Level 4)** quarterly model territory reallocation scenarios
  when AE headcount or regional expansion changes. Results are stored in Snowflake
  and available in the control panel.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.2.4. PERIODIC REVIEW PROCESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Quarterly** (2 weeks before the end of the quarter) a territory review is conducted:

1. **Data collection:** analytic agents generate a report for each AE:
   - Number of accounts in the territory.
   - Pipeline amount, pipeline coverage.
   - Number of active deals, win rate.
   - Quota attainment (YTD).
2. **Analysis:** RevOps operators identify imbalances:
   - Overloaded AEs (accounts >130% of average).
   - Underloaded AEs (<70% of average).
   - Regions/verticals with high customer density requiring expansion.
3. **Adjustments:** based on the analysis, changes are made:
   - Move accounts between AEs (manually via Territory Management UI or via bulk update).
   - Modify automatic assignment rules (e.g., add new vertical specialists).
   - If necessary, recommend headcount increase (via the headcount model, Section 7.1).
4. **Communication:** changes are documented in the handbook, AEs are notified
   via Salesforce (tasks) and Slack.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.2.5. CONFLICT RESOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Conflicts arise when two AEs claim the same account (e.g., due to duplicate creation
or territory overlap). Procedure:

1. **Automatic detection:** when an Opportunity is created, Salesforce checks whether
   there is already an active deal for the same account with a different AE. If so,
   a task is created for RevOps with the tag “Conflict”.
2. **Manual resolution:** a RevOps operator reviews the interaction history,
   the primary contact, and account data. Resolution:
   - Assign the account to the AE who first recorded activity (based on tasks or
     SalesLoft activity).
   - Split the account into two (if it involves different departments or regions).
3. **Recording:** the resolution is recorded in the `Conflict_Resolution__c` field on the
   account to prevent future disputes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.2.6. INTEGRATION WITH FORECASTING AND RESOURCE PLANNING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Revenue forecast (Section 6.4)** uses territory data to group deals and evaluate
  win rates by region.
- **Headcount model (Section 7.1)** relies on territory load information when calculating
  the required number of AEs. If average load in a region exceeds 120% of the norm,
  the model may recommend headcount increase.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON TERRITORY DESIGN**

- Territory design ensures a structured, fair, and scalable distribution of the market
  among AEs.
- Using Salesforce Territory Management automates account assignment and makes the
  process transparent for the entire team.
- Quarterly reviews with agent and RevOps participation enable timely adjustments
  to boundaries and workload.
- Clear conflict resolution rules minimize friction and improve sales efficiency.
