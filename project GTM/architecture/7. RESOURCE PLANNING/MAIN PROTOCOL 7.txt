**7. RESOURCE PLANNING**

Resource planning in the target architecture is a continuous, data‑driven process
based on revenue forecasts (Section 6.4), historical team performance, and the
company’s strategic goals. It covers four key areas: team size and composition
(headcount), geographic market division (territory design), compensation system
(base + variable), and the annual planning process (OKRs, budgeting). All decisions
are made with the participation of agents (financial, analytical) and RevOps operators,
ensuring transparency, flexibility, and scalability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1. HEADCOUNT MODEL (AE, CSM, AM, REVOPS STAFFING)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Objective:** Determine the optimal headcount for each role to achieve target
ARR, net retention, and win rate within the given budget.

**Approach:** The headcount model is based on three factors:
- **ARR forecast** (from Section 6.4) and target growth rates.
- **Productivity per employee** (historical data and industry benchmarks).
- **Workload ratios** (e.g., number of customers per CSM, number of deals per AE).

**Example calculations:**
- **AE:** required number of AE = (target ARR from new customers) /
  (average annual AE productivity). Win rate and sales cycle length are also considered.
- **CSM:** number of CSM = (number of enterprise customers) /
  (service ratio, e.g., 50–100 customers per CSM).
- **AM:** number of AM = (number of customers ready for expansion) /
  (workload ratio, e.g., 100–150 customers per AM).
- **RevOps:** number of RevOps = (number of active agents and integrations) /
  (support ratio, e.g., 5–10 agents per RevOps).

All calculations are automated by financial agents (Level 4) and updated quarterly.
RevOps operators can adjust the coefficients via the control panel.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.2. TERRITORY DESIGN (MARKET SPLIT AMONG AES)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Objective:** Ensure balanced workload for AEs, minimize conflicts, and maximize market coverage.

**Principles:**
- **Geographic division:** primary split by region (US, EMEA, APAC, LATAM). Within regions, by country or country groups.
- **Vertical division:** for large markets (US), split by industry (finance, technology, retail, etc.) or customer size (named accounts for enterprise, territorial for mid‑market).
- **Dynamic rebalancing:** analytic agents (Level 1) monitor AE workload and initiate boundary reviews when thresholds are exceeded (e.g., >120% of norm). Final decisions are made by RevOps operators.

**Tools:** Salesforce Territory Management (standard functionality) is used to assign accounts and deals. Rules are automatically applied when new accounts are created.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.3. COMPENSATION MODEL (BASE + VARIABLE, OPTIMAL CONTRACT)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Objective:** Design a compensation scheme that incentivizes achieving company goals,
attracts and retains talent, and aligns with Sourcegraph’s transparency principles.

**Main components:**
- **Base salary:** determined by level (e.g., M3 for this role) and market data. Transparent to all employees.
- **Variable component (commissions / bonuses):**
  * **AE:** commission on revenue from closed deals (typically 5–15% of ARR)
    with possible accelerators for exceeding quota.
  * **CSM / AM:** bonus tied to net retention and expansion ARR.
  * **RevOps:** bonus tied to forecast accuracy, automation task completion time,
    and team satisfaction.

**Optimal contract (principal‑agent theory):**
- Linear commission without a cap (to avoid effort limitation when quota is exceeded).
- Quota set at a level achievable with average effort (historical data + forecast).
- Payment frequency: monthly or quarterly (for large deals).
- To prevent short‑termism (focus only on closing rather than long‑term relationships),
  adjustments for chargebacks and customer experience (health score) are introduced.

**Automation:** financial agents (Level 4) calculate commissions monthly based on closed deals
and compensation rules. Results are synchronized with Salesforce and the payroll system.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.4. ANNUAL PLANNING PROCESS (OKRS, BUDGETING)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Objective:** Set goals for the upcoming year, allocate budget and resources,
ensuring alignment between strategy and operational plans.

**Process steps:**
1. **Foresight session (Q3 of current year)** – leadership (CRO, CPO, CFO)
   together with RevOps operators define strategic priorities:
   ARR, net retention, win rate targets, geographic expansion, new products.
2. **OKR development (Q4)** – OKRs are cascaded from strategic goals to each
   department (Sales, CS, RevOps, Marketing). Analytic agents help model scenarios
   (e.g., “what headcount is needed for 50% ARR growth?”).
3. **Budgeting** – based on revenue forecasts (Section 6.4) and the headcount model (7.1),
   the budget for the next year is formed. It includes:
   - CAPEX for automation and integrations (if needed).
   - OPEX (salaries, licenses, cloud resources, marketing).
   - Reserve fund (10% of the budget).
4. **Approval** – the budget and OKRs are approved by the board of directors
   (or company leadership) in December.
5. **Quarterly adjustments** – after each quarter, actual results are compared with the plan.
   If deviation exceeds 10%, a review process is initiated (with RevOps operators and leadership).

**Tools:** A combination of Excel / Google Sheets (for alignment) and Salesforce / Snowflake
(for execution monitoring) is used. Financial agents automatically generate reports on OKR execution.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON RESOURCE PLANNING**

- Resource planning is fully integrated with forecasting models and team performance data.
- The headcount model, territory design, compensation model, and annual planning process
  are automated as much as possible, while maintaining manual control at key stages
  (approval, adjustments).
- Using agents (financial, analytical) reduces the RevOps workload and improves calculation accuracy.
