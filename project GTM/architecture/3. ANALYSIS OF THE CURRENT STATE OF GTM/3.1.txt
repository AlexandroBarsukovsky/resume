**3.1. SALES AND MARKETING ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.1.1. OVERALL SCHEMA (BASED ON HANDBOOK AND INTERVIEWS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The GTM organizational structure of Sourcegraph follows a functional principle,
with roles responsible for different stages of the customer lifecycle. The main
departments are:

- **Sales** – sales (AE, Sales Strategist)
- **Customer Success** – retention and expansion (CSM, AM)
- **Marketing** – marketing (ABM, content, events)
- **Revenue Operations (RevOps)** – data management, CRM, reporting

All departments report to the CRO (Chief Revenue Officer). Interaction takes place
within quarterly plans and weekly operational meetings.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.1.2. DETAILED ROLE DESCRIPTIONS AND FUNCTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Sales Strategist (formerly SDR)**
- **Headcount:** 8–12 people.
- **Functions:** analytical qualification of incoming intent signals (6sense),
  data enrichment (Clearbit/ZoomInfo), primary outreach (email/LinkedIn),
  handover of qualified accounts to AEs.
- **Tools:** 6sense, Snowflake (product data), Excel, Salesforce (task creation),
  SalesLoft (sequences).
- **KPIs:** number of qualified opportunities, response time, conversion to Opportunity.

**Account Executive (AE)**
- **Headcount:** 15–20 people.
- **Functions:** managing enterprise deals, MEDDPICC qualification,
  demonstrations, negotiations, contract closing.
- **Tools:** Salesforce (Opportunity), MEDDPICC templates, ZoomInfo,
  contract management system (Ironclad).
- **KPIs:** ARR quota, win rate, deal cycle, forecast accuracy.

**Customer Success Manager (CSM)**
- **Headcount:** 8–10 people.
- **Functions:** onboarding, training, retention, risk identification.
- **Tools:** Gainsight (being implemented), Salesforce, product analytics
  (Amplitude/Snowflake).
- **KPIs:** net retention, health score (planned), time to first value,
  churn rate.

**Account Manager (AM)**
- **Headcount:** 4–5 people.
- **Functions:** expansion (upsell, cross‑sell) and contract renewals
  for top‑100 clients.
- **Tools:** Salesforce, Gainsight, product analytics.
- **KPIs:** expansion ARR, renewal rate, number of upsell deals.

**Marketing (ABM team)**
- **Headcount:** 6–8 people.
- **Functions:** development of ABM campaigns, content marketing, events,
  lead generation (in collaboration with Sales Strategists).
- **Tools:** 6sense, Marketo, LinkedIn Ads, web analytics.
- **KPIs:** number of incoming intent signals, conversion to demo,
  campaign ROI.

**Revenue Operations (RevOps)**
- **Headcount:** 4–6 people.
- **Functions:** Salesforce administration, integration configuration,
  reporting (QBR, board packs), compensation management, performance analysis.
- **Tools:** Salesforce, Tableau, Snowflake, Hightouch, Excel/Google Sheets.
- **KPIs:** data accuracy, report preparation time, team satisfaction.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.1.3. HIERARCHY AND REPORTING LINES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- CRO (Chief Revenue Officer) – leads all GTM functions.
- VP of Sales – manages AEs and Sales Strategists.
- VP of Customer Success – manages CSMs and AMs.
- VP of Marketing – manages marketing.
- Director of RevOps – manages the RevOps team.

All leaders participate in weekly GTM syncs and quarterly OKR sessions.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.1.4. ISSUES IN THE CURRENT ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. **Blurred responsibility** – Sales Strategists spend up to 40% of their time
   on data collection, reducing qualification efficiency.
2. **Lack of a unified system for CS** – Gainsight is being implemented, but health score
   is not automated and playbooks are absent.
3. **RevOps overload** – manual report preparation distracts from strategic tasks.
4. **Weak cross‑department alignment** – marketing data (intent) is not always
   synchronized with sales and CS.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**PROJECT CONCLUSIONS**
- The current structure generally fits the enterprise model, but requires
  automation to eliminate manual work and improve consistency.
- The introduction of an agent holding should support existing roles,
  not replace them, enhancing analytical and execution functions.
