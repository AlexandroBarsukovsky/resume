**3.4. KEY METRICS AND THEIR RELIABILITY**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.4.1. LIST OF KEY METRICS (COLLECTION METHOD AND ACCURACY)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Metric                      | Current Value         | Collection Method / Frequency          | Reliability (Issues)                     |
|-----------------------------|-----------------------|----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)| $50–100M (estimate) | Post‑factum, based on closed contracts | No forecasting model, delays             |
| Net retention (NRR)         | 100–110%              | Quarterly, from Salesforce data        | Calculated manually, reasons for changes not analyzed |
| Win rate (enterprise)       | ~20%                  | Based on historical data (Closed Won / Closed Lost) | Not segmented, seasonality and macro factors not considered |
| Deal cycle (days)           | 90–120                | Manual extraction from Salesforce (date difference) | No automatic tracking, no alerts for delays |
| Pipeline coverage           | <2×                   | Calculated manually before QBR          | No regular monitoring, data often outdated |
| Forecast accuracy (MAPE)    | 20–30%                | Subjective AE estimates + weighted pipeline | Macro factors, seasonality, stage win rates not considered |
| Health score                | Not measured          | –                                      | Absent                                   |
| Churn rate (enterprise)     | 5–10%                 | Post‑factum (Lost ARR)                 | Causes not analyzed, no early warning    |
| CAC Payback                 | >24 months            | Not calculated regularly               | Rough estimate                           |
| Time to first value (days)  | 30–45                 | Manual CSM survey                      | No objective data                        |
| Expansion rate              | Not measured          | –                                      | Absent                                   |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.4.2. ISSUES WITH RELIABILITY AND TIMELINESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. **Data lag** – most metrics are calculated post‑factum (quarterly or monthly),
   preventing timely response.
2. **Subjectivity of forecasts** – AEs assess close probability based on intuition,
   without historical data.
3. **Fragmented sources** – ARR from Salesforce, activity from Snowflake,
   intent from 6sense – no unified view.
4. **Lack of automatic alerts** – deviations from plan are not automatically flagged.
5. **No forecasting models** – a simple weighted pipeline does not account for
   macroeconomics, seasonality, or segment‑level win rates.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.4.3. EXAMPLES OF INACCURACIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Example 1:** An AE forecasts a deal will close in the current quarter with
  80% probability, but the deal is delayed due to the client’s budget constraints.
  The macroeconomic factor (PMI) was not considered in the model.
- **Example 2:** The quarterly forecast shows 95% of the target achieved,
  but actual closing is 80% because several large deals were not included in the pipeline.
- **Example 3:** A CSM does not notice a decline in client activity because
  product usage data is not synchronized with CRM. The client churns without receiving attention.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.4.4. IMPACT OF INACCURATE METRICS ON THE BUSINESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Resource planning** – forecast errors lead to premature hiring or shortage of AEs.
- **Investment decisions** – investors rely on ARR and forecasts; inaccuracies can affect the company’s valuation.
- **Operational decisions** – without a health score, CSMs cannot prioritize clients, increasing churn.
- **Team motivation** – incorrect forecasts distort performance evaluation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**PROJECT CONCLUSIONS**
- A unified real‑time metrics collection system is required.
- Forecasting models must account for macroeconomics, seasonality, and historical win rates.
- Health score and expansion signals must be automated.
- All metrics should be accessible through dashboards with drill‑down capability.
