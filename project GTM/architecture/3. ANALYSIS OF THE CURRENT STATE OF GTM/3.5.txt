PROTOCOL: 3.5. IDENTIFIED PAIN POINTS
Project: Sourcegraph GTM Infrastructure
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**3.5. IDENTIFIED PAIN POINTS (DATA FRAGMENTATION, MANUAL WORK, INACCURATE FORECASTS)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.5.1. DATA FRAGMENTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Symptoms:**
- Sales Strategist spends up to 40% of time collecting data from 6sense, Snowflake,
  Excel, and Salesforce.
- Product data (activity) is stored in Snowflake but not synchronized with CRM
  in real time.
- Intent signals from 6sense are exported manually, causing delays and errors.
- No unified customer profile (Account) with up‑to‑date fields: ICP_Fit,
  Intent_Strength, Health_Score.

**Consequences:**
- Lost opportunities due to slow reaction.
- Incomplete customer picture for AEs and CSMs.
- Manual data transfer errors.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.5.2. MANUAL WORK AND LOW AUTOMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Symptoms:**
- Account prioritization is done in Excel.
- Task creation in Salesforce is manual.
- Opportunity fields (MEDDPICC, probability) are updated subjectively.
- Preparing QBR and board packs takes 10–20 hours of analyst time per quarter.
- CSM monitors clients manually, no automatic alerts.

**Consequences:**
- High operational load on teams.
- Inability to scale (manual work grows linearly with client count).
- Distraction from strategic tasks.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.5.3. INACCURATE FORECASTS AND LACK OF FORECASTING MODELS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Symptoms:**
- MAPE = 20–30% (target <10%).
- Forecasts based on subjective AE assessments.
- Macroeconomic factors (PMI, interest rate, tech index) and seasonality not considered.
- Pipeline coverage calculated manually, often outdated.

**Consequences:**
- Errors in headcount and budget planning.
- Unpredictable revenue for investors.
- Difficulty in making expansion or reduction decisions.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.5.4. ABSENCE OF HEALTH SCORE AND EXPANSION SIGNALS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Symptoms:**
- No automatic detection of clients at high churn risk.
- CSM learns about problems only when the client contacts them or after churn.
- Expansion opportunities (upsell, cross‑sell) are discovered by chance.
- No uniform criteria for assessing customer “health.”

**Consequences:**
- Churn higher than target (5–10%).
- Lost revenue from expansion.
- Inefficient allocation of CSM time.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.5.5. WEAK INTEGRATION AND LACK OF EVENT‑DRIVEN ARCHITECTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Symptoms:**
- Changes in one system do not trigger automatic actions in others.
- No triggers: when Account Score >0.7, a task is not created; when Health Score
  <0.5, a playbook is not launched.
- Data is synchronized in batch mode (every few hours).

**Consequences:**
- Delays in executing critical actions.
- Loss of synchronization between teams.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.5.6. CULTURAL ISSUES (RELATED TO AUTOMATION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Symptoms:**
- Teams are used to manual work; new tools meet resistance.
- Lack of documented processes, hindering automation.
- High turnover in some roles due to routine.

**Consequences:**
- Difficulty implementing changes.
- Risk of automation rejection.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.5.7. FINAL PAIN POINT MATRIX
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Pain Point                     | Impact on Business                          | Priority |
|--------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|----------|
| Data fragmentation             | Delays, errors, lost opportunities          | High     |
| Manual work                    | Inability to scale                          | High     |
| Inaccurate forecasts           | Planning errors, instability                | High     |
| Lack of health score           | High churn, missed expansion                | High     |
| Weak integration               | Inefficiency, delays                        | Medium   |
| Cultural resistance            | Risk of project failure                     | Medium   |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**PROJECT CONCLUSIONS**
- All identified pain points are interconnected and require a systemic solution.
- The target architecture must provide: a unified data fabric, automation of routine
  operations, forecasting models, health score, and event‑driven triggers.
- Without addressing these issues, Sourcegraph will not be able to scale enterprise sales.
