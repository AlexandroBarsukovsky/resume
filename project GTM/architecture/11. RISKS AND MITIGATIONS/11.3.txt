**11.3. FINANCIAL RISKS (BUDGET OVERRUN, UNREALISTIC EXPECTATIONS)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.3.1. DESCRIPTION AND CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The implementation of the target GTM architecture requires significant investments:
CAPEX $2.0M (development, integrations, training) and annual OPEX $1.7M (cloud resources,
licenses, support). Financial risks are associated with the possibility of exceeding
these costs, as well as failure to achieve the projected returns (ARR growth, efficiency
gains), which could lead to reduced ROI and increased payback period.
Managing financial risks involves careful budgeting, phased funding allocation,
regular monitoring of deviations, and plan adjustments.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.3.2. KEY FINANCIAL RISKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Risk                                     | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation                                                                 | Responsible |
|------------------------------------------|-------------|--------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| **CAPEX overrun** – exceeding the budget for development, integrations, equipment. | Medium | High | • Phased budget allocation (by phases).<br>• Contingency fund of 10% of CAPEX ($200K).<br>• Regular cost review (monthly).<br>• Use standard components instead of custom development where possible. | CFO, Technical Lead |
| **OPEX growth** – increase in cloud resources, licenses, support costs above plan. | Medium | Medium | • Use spot instances for non‑critical workloads.<br>• Optimise Snowflake queries (reduce scanned volume, clustering).<br>• Monitor costs in AWS Cost Explorer, set up alerts.<br>• Review licenses when consumption decreases. | CFO, RevOps |
| **Failure to achieve expected ARR growth** – automation does not lead to planned revenue growth (50% YoY). | Low | High | • Quarterly review of actual KPIs vs forecast.<br>• Adjust forecasting model on deviations.<br>• Additional marketing activities if pipeline insufficient.<br>• Plan B: increase headcount if pipeline falls short. | CRO, CFO |
| **Increased payback period** (payback period) >12 months. | Low | High | • Accelerate implementation by working on phases in parallel.<br>• Reduce scope of Phases 1–2 if necessary.<br>• Engage additional resources for critical paths.<br>• Adjust target KPIs to more realistic levels. | CRO, CFO |
| **Unforeseen integration rework costs** – complexity of integrating with legacy systems or API instability. | Medium | Medium | • Allocate 15% of CAPEX for unforeseen work.<br>• Use standard connectors where possible.<br>• Close collaboration with vendors (Salesforce, Gainsight) for early issue resolution. | Technical Lead |
| **Inefficient license utilisation** – purchasing excessive Salesforce, Gainsight, SalesLoft licenses. | Low | Medium | • Monitor actual usage.<br>• Review needs quarterly.<br>• Use pay‑as‑you‑go models for new features. | RevOps, CFO |
| **Reduced ROI due to incomplete automation** – if automation does not cover the expected volume of processes, labour savings are below plan. | Medium | Medium | • Continuously collect feedback to expand scenarios.<br>• Plan iterative expansion in the roadmap.<br>• Regularly demonstrate achieved savings to justify further investment. | RevOps, CRO |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.3.3. MONITORING AND METRICS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Metric                                     | Source                               | Target / Alert Condition                |
|--------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **CAPEX vs plan** (cumulative)             | Financial reporting                  | Deviation >5% – warning, >10% – alert   |
| **OPEX vs plan** (monthly)                 | AWS Cost Explorer, licenses          | Deviation >5% – warning, >10% – alert   |
| **ARR growth (actual vs forecast)**        | Salesforce (Closed Won)              | Deviation >10% – root cause analysis    |
| **Forecast accuracy (MAPE)**               | Financial agents                     | >12% – requires adjustment              |
| **Payback period** (actual)                | DCF model, updated quarterly         | >12 months – investment review          |
| **ROI (calculated vs actual)**             | Financial reporting                  | Below plan by >20% – review             |
| **Percentage of manual interventions** (automation effectiveness indicator) | Control panel                       | >5% – insufficient automation           |
| **Labour time savings** (hours/month)      | Surveys, agent logs                  | Below plan by >20% – review             |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.3.4. RESPONSE PLAN FOR FINANCIAL DEVIATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Deviation                                   | Action                                                                 |
|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CAPEX overrun >10%**                      | • Freeze non‑critical work.<br>• Move some functionality to later phases.<br>• Draw from contingency fund.<br>• Revise plan with management approval. |
| **OPEX overrun >10% for two consecutive months** | • Optimise cloud resources (reduce instances, switch to spot).<br>• Review licenses, drop unused ones.<br>• Reduce monitoring scope (if excessive). |
| **ARR underperformance >10%**               | • Analyse root causes (win rate, pipeline coverage).<br>• Strengthen marketing (ABM campaigns).<br>• Adjust forecasts for remaining quarters.<br>• Consider headcount reduction (if not automation‑related). |
| **Payback period >12 months**               | • Reassess budget for subsequent phases.<br>• Accelerate implementation (additional resources).<br>• Adjust target KPIs (lower the bar).<br>• Suspend investment until performance recovers. |
| **ROI below plan**                          | • Analyse factors (CAPEX, OPEX, ARR growth).<br>• Adjust financial model.<br>• Amend roadmap to improve efficiency. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.3.5. CONTINGENCY FUND AND CONTROL MECHANISMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Contingency fund:** $200K (10% of CAPEX) reserved for unforeseen expenses.
  Use only with approval from CRO and CFO, upon occurrence of critical unforeseen costs.
- **Monthly financial review:** in the operational meeting of RevOps and the finance
  department, actual costs vs plan are analysed, deviations identified.
- **Quarterly budget review:** when market conditions or revenue forecasts change
  significantly, the budget is adjusted.
- **Investment efficiency control:** ROI (additional revenue / automation costs)
  is calculated each quarter, broken down by function.
  If ROI <3 (over three years), a review is initiated.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON FINANCIAL RISKS**

- Financial risks require constant monitoring, strict budget control,
  and flexibility in plan adjustments.
- Key measures: phased funding, contingency fund, regular comparison
  of forecasts and actuals, optimisation of cloud resource costs.
- Transparency of financial indicators (dashboards, monthly reviews) enables
  timely detection of deviations.
- Managing financial risks is a shared responsibility of the technical
  team, RevOps, and the finance department.
