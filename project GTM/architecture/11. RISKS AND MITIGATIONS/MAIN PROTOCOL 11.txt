Project: Sourcegraph GTM Infrastructure

**11. RISKS AND MITIGATIONS**

Successful implementation of the target GTM architecture requires systematic risk
management for risks that may arise during implementation phases and during operation.
This section classifies risks into four categories (organisational, technical,
financial, security and compliance), assesses their likelihood and potential impact,
and proposes specific mitigation, monitoring, and response measures. At the end of the
section, a response plan and fallback measures are presented to ensure operational
continuity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.1. ORGANISATIONAL RISKS (RESISTANCE TO CHANGE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**11.1.1. Description**
The introduction of automation and new processes may encounter resistance from
Sales, CS, and AM teams who are accustomed to existing manual processes.
Roles whose work might seem “replaceable” by agents (e.g., Sales Strategist)
are particularly vulnerable. Sourcegraph’s high‑agency culture requires that
automation be perceived as assistance, not control.

**11.1.2. Risks and Mitigation Measures**

| Risk                                     | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation                                                                 |
|------------------------------------------|------------|--------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Strategist resistance** – refusal to use automated priority account lists | Medium | High | • Demonstrate quick wins (response time reduction).<br>• Involve Strategists in pilot testing.<br>• Train and explain that automation frees them from routine. |
| **AE distrust of automated account distribution** | Medium | High | • Transparency of rules (Account Score, ICP) – document in handbook.<br>• Manual override capability via control panel.<br>• Show metrics: pipeline coverage improved after implementation. |
| **CSM/AM misunderstanding of Health Score role** | Low | Medium | • Train on Gainsight usage, interpretation of colour zones.<br>• Show successful retention/expansion cases.<br>• Regular Q&A sessions. |
| **Loss of engagement due to “black box”** | Medium | Medium | • Full transparency of agent logic (formulas, triggers).<br>• Control panel with decision history viewing.<br>• Regular automation effectiveness reviews. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.2. TECHNICAL RISKS (INTEGRATION, RELIABILITY)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**11.2.1. Description**
The complexity of integrating Salesforce, 6sense, Gainsight, SalesLoft, Hightouch,
Snowflake, and the agent holding creates risks of failures, delays, data loss,
and unavailability of critical functions.

**11.2.2. Risks and Mitigation Measures**

| Risk                                     | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation                                                                 |
|------------------------------------------|------------|--------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **External system API failures (6sense, Gainsight)** | Medium | High | • Exponential backoff retries (3 attempts).<br>• Dead Letter Queue (DLQ) for events not processed after attempts.<br>• Fallback mechanisms (manual CSV export if API is unavailable for a long time). |
| **Salesforce API limit breaches**         | Low        | High   | • Use Bulk API for mass operations.<br>• Batch processing, real‑time limit monitoring (CloudWatch).<br>• When approaching limits, automatically reduce request frequency. |
| **Reverse ETL (Hightouch) delays**        | Medium     | Medium | • Configure real‑time synchronisation via webhooks.<br>• Monitor latency, alert on delays >10 sec.<br>• Backup channel: agents can update Salesforce directly via API if Hightouch fails. |
| **Data loss during migration**            | Low        | High   | • Full backup of all data in Snowflake.<br>• Pilot run on a copy of data.<br>• Rollback plan to previous CRM version. |
| **Insufficient agent performance when scaling** | Medium | High | • Load testing up to 10⁵ events/second.<br>• Auto‑scaling (HPA in Kubernetes, Lambda concurrency).<br>• Code profiling, optimisation of bottlenecks. |
| **Complexity of debugging distributed system** | Medium | Medium | • Centralised logging (CloudWatch, ELK).<br>• End‑to‑end tracing with correlation ID.<br>• Detailed metrics per agent type. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.3. FINANCIAL RISKS (BUDGET OVERRUN, UNREALISTIC EXPECTATIONS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**11.3.1. Description**
The project requires investments (CAPEX $2.0M, OPEX $1.7M/year). Incorrect cost
estimation, delays, or failure to achieve expected returns may lead to budget
overruns and reduced ROI.

**11.3.2. Risks and Mitigation Measures**

| Risk                                     | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation                                                                 |
|------------------------------------------|------------|--------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CAPEX overrun** (development, integrations) | Medium | High | • Phased budget allocation (by phases).<br>• Contingency fund of 10%.<br>• Regular cost review (monthly). |
| **OPEX growth** (cloud resources, licenses) | Medium | Medium | • Use spot instances for non‑critical workloads.<br>• Optimise Snowflake queries (reduce scanned volume).<br>• Monitor costs in AWS Cost Explorer. |
| **Failure to achieve expected ARR growth** | Low | High | • Quarterly review of actual KPIs vs forecast.<br>• Adjust forecasting model on deviations.<br>• Additional marketing activities if pipeline insufficient. |
| **Increased payback period**              | Low        | High   | • Accelerate implementation by working on phases in parallel.<br>• Reduce scope of Phases 1–2 if necessary.<br>• Engage additional resources for critical paths. |
| **Unforeseen integration rework costs**   | Medium     | Medium | • Allocate 15% of CAPEX for unforeseen work.<br>• Use standard connectors where possible.<br>• Close collaboration with vendors (Salesforce, Gainsight). |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.4. SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE RISKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**11.4.1. Description**
The system processes sensitive customer data (contracts, product metrics,
negotiation data). Security breaches could lead to data leaks, legal consequences,
and reputational damage. Non‑compliance with SOC2, GDPR, and other standards
may affect the ability to work with enterprise customers.

**11.4.2. Risks and Mitigation Measures**

| Risk                                     | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation                                                                 |
|------------------------------------------|------------|--------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Data leak through agents**             | Low        | Critical | • All agents run in isolated environments (containers, Lambda).<br>• API access via IAM and secrets (AWS Secrets Manager).<br>• Encryption at rest (AES‑256) and in transit (TLS 1.3).<br>• Audit of all agent actions (CloudTrail). |
| **Unauthorised access to control panel** | Low        | High   | • Multi‑factor authentication (SSO + MFA).<br>• Role‑based model (admin, operator, viewer).<br>• Log all control panel actions. |
| **SOC2 / GDPR violation**                | Low        | High   | • Regular compliance audits.<br>• Automatic PII masking in logs.<br>• Data storage in compliant regions (US/EU). |
| **Quantum encryption pilot failure**     | Low        | Medium | • Use hybrid mode (quantum + classical).<br>• Fallback channel with classical encryption.<br>• Thorough sandbox testing. |
| **Unauthorised data modification**       | Low        | High   | • Integrity control via hashing of critical records.<br>• Change audit in Salesforce (Field History).<br>• Backup in Snowflake with 7‑year retention. |

11.5. RESPONSE PLAN AND FALLBACK MEASURES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**11.5.1. General Approach**
For each risk category, escalation levels and response procedures are defined.
The plan includes:
- **Monitoring** – automatic alerts based on metrics (error rate, latency,
  API limits, DLQ size).
- **Escalation** – clear chain: control panel operator → RevOps → technical team
  → management (depending on criticality).
- **Fallback procedures** – manual actions allowing work to continue when
  automation fails.

**11.5.2. Response Matrix**

| Criticality Level | Example Incident                              | Action                                                                 | Response Time |
|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------|
| **Critical**      | Complete unavailability of Salesforce API, data loss | • Immediate PagerDuty escalation.<br>• Activate fallback plan: manual task processing via control panel.<br>• Notify leadership. | 15 minutes    |
| **High**          | Gainsight integration failure, mass agent errors | • Control panel operator analysis.<br>• Restart failed agents.<br>• If unresolved in 1 hour – escalate to RevOps. | 1 hour        |
| **Medium**        | Isolated errors, processing delays            | • Control panel operator processes DLQ.<br>• Regular RevOps report.<br>• Configuration adjustment. | 2 hours       |
| **Low**           | Stale data, minor errors                      | • Planned fix during retraining cycle.<br>• Log for analysis.           | 24 hours      |

**11.5.3. Fallback Procedures (Manual Mode Playbook)**

| Scenario                                     | Manual Action                                                                 |
|----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **6sense API unavailable**                   | Strategist manually downloads intent signals from 6sense reports (CSV) and imports into Salesforce. |
| **Gainsight unavailable**                    | CSM manually creates tasks in Salesforce, tracks Health Score via Snowflake reports. |
| **Hightouch unavailable**                    | RevOps runs batch data export from Snowflake to Salesforce using Data Loader. |
| **Executor agent failure**                   | Control panel operators manually perform critical actions (task creation, field updates) via Salesforce UI. |
| **Data loss in Salesforce**                  | Restore from Snowflake backup (daily dump).                                     |

**11.5.4. Contingency Fund and Resources**
- **Financial reserve:** 10% of CAPEX ($200K) allocated for unforeseen expenses.
- **Technical reserve:** additional 20% of AWS capacity (EC2, Lambda) to handle peak loads.
- **Staff reserve:** engagement of external contractors (2–3 specialists) in case of development delays.

**11.5.5. Drills and Testing**
- **Scheduled recovery drills** (quarterly) with RevOps and control panel operators.
- **Chaos engineering** – simulate API failures, component failures in sandbox to verify resilience.
- **Incident analysis** – post‑mortem with root cause identification and improvements.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON RISKS AND MITIGATIONS**

- A systematic approach to risk management minimises the impact of
  organisational, technical, financial, and compliance threats.
- Specific mitigation measures, monitoring, and response plans are defined for each risk.
- Fallback procedures and a financial reserve ensure operational continuity
  even during critical failures.
- Regular drills and incident analysis contribute to continuous improvement
  of system reliability.
