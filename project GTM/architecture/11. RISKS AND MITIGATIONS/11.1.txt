**11.1. ORGANISATIONAL RISKS (RESISTANCE TO CHANGE)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.1.1. DESCRIPTION AND CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The implementation of the target GTM architecture involves profound changes in the
work processes of the Sales, CS, and AM teams. Automating some tasks (qualification,
account distribution, health score, playbooks) may be perceived by employees as
a threat to their roles, a loss of control, or distrust of new tools. The high‑agency
culture characteristic of Sourcegraph requires that changes be implemented with
maximum transparency and involvement, not imposed from above. The risk of resistance
is one of the key ones, because without acceptance by the teams, even the most
advanced architecture will not deliver the expected results.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.1.2. KEY ORGANISATIONAL RISKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Risk                                     | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation                                                                 | Responsible |
|------------------------------------------|-------------|--------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| **Strategist resistance** – refusal to use automated priority account lists, ignoring tasks created by agents. | Medium | High | • Demonstrate quick wins (response time reduction from 2 days to 2 hours) in the pilot group.<br>• Involve Strategists in testing and feedback during Phase 1.<br>• Training: automation frees them from routine (export CSV, Excel), allowing focus on qualification.<br>• Introduce KPIs for using automated lists. | VP Sales, RevOps |
| **AE distrust of automated account distribution** – doubts about fairness of assignment, fear of losing best accounts. | Medium | High | • Full transparency of rules: publish Account Score formula, weights, thresholds in handbook.<br>• Manual override capability via control panel (with reason comment).<br>• Show metrics: after implementation, pipeline coverage increased, win rate improved.<br>• Regular Q&A sessions with RevOps and management. | VP Sales, RevOps |
| **CSM/AM misunderstanding of Health Score role** – ignoring red zone, distrust of automated playbooks. | Low | Medium | • Training on Gainsight usage, interpretation of colour zones and playbooks.<br>• Demonstrate successful cases: customer retention for clients on the verge of churn thanks to automatic alerts.<br>• Regular effectiveness reviews (e.g., percentage of customers returning from red zone). | VP CS, RevOps |
| **Loss of engagement due to “black box”** – feeling that decisions are made by unknown entities, reduced initiative. | Medium | Medium | • Full transparency of agent logic: all formulas, triggers, playbooks documented.<br>• Control panel with ability to view decision history (why agent created a task, what Account Score was).<br>• Regular automation effectiveness reviews with teams. | RevOps |
| **Resistance to change from managers** – unwillingness to change established processes, pressure on subordinates. | Low | High | • Involve managers from the start (participation in foresight sessions, approval of OKRs).<br>• Demonstrate benefits for their departments (productivity gains, forecast accuracy).<br>• Regular progress reports and achieved KPIs. | CRO, CPO, CFO |
| **High turnover due to routine** – before automation, Strategists may have experienced burnout from manual work. After automation, if new roles are not explained, dissatisfaction may persist. | Medium | Medium | • Clearly define new roles: Strategist becomes an analyst, not an “Excel processing robot”.<br>• Provide opportunities for professional growth (participation in model tuning, analytics).<br>• Monitor satisfaction (NPS surveys of teams). | VP Sales, HR |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.1.3. CHANGE MANAGEMENT PLAN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**11.1.3.1. Communication Strategy**

| Stage                | Target Audience | Key Messages                                                                 | Channels                        |
|----------------------|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Before launch (Phase 1) | All GTM teams   | • Why we are automating: to eliminate routine and give more time for strategy.<br>• Which tasks remain with people and which go to agents.<br>• There will be a way to influence (manual override, feedback). | All‑hands, handbook, email      |
| During pilot         | Pilot participants | • Regular progress updates.<br>• Collect feedback, demonstrate quick wins.<br>• Discuss issues, adjust. | Weekly syncs, Slack             |
| After pilot          | All GTM teams   | • Show pilot results (KPIs).<br>• Scaling plan.<br>• Invitation to participate in model retraining. | All‑hands, handbook             |
| During scaling       | All GTM teams   | • Continuous training, answering questions.<br>• Acknowledge teams’ contributions to improving automation. | Control panel, Q&A sessions     |

**11.1.3.2. Training and Support**

- **Mandatory training:**
  * For Strategists: working with the control panel, interpreting Account Score, rules for creating Opportunities.
  * For AEs: working with automatic distribution, MEDDPICC in Salesforce, forecasting with agents.
  * For CSMs/AMs: Gainsight, health score, playbooks, interpreting signals.
- **Documentation:** detailed handbook with sections “How automation works”, “Frequently asked questions”, “What to do during failures”.
- **Support:** dedicated Slack channel (#gtm-automation-support) with control panel operators and RevOps, response time <1 hour.

**11.1.3.3. Feedback Mechanisms**

- **“Feedback” button** in the control panel for each automated action – ability to mark “incorrect decision”, “false positive”.
- **Regular NPS surveys** (quarterly) for Sales, CS, AM teams with the question: “How much does automation help you in your work?”.
- **Focus groups** after each phase to gather detailed suggestions.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.1.4. MONITORING AND METRICS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

To detect signs of resistance early, the following metrics are used:

| Metric                                     | Source                               | Target / Alert Condition                |
|--------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Satisfaction (NPS)** of Sales, CS, AM teams | Surveys (quarterly)                 | >70 (target), <50 – alert               |
| **Percentage of manual overrides**         | Control panel (logs)                 | <5% – normal, >10% – analysis required  |
| **Response time to automated tasks**       | Salesforce (Task)                    | If tasks are ignored >2 days – signal   |
| **Number of support requests** (Slack, tickets) | Slack, Jira                       | Growth >20% – possible issues           |
| **Turnover in GTM teams**                  | HR reports                           | Comparison with historical level        |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11.1.5. RESPONSE PLAN UPON DETECTING SIGNS OF RESISTANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Sign                                     | Action                                                                 |
|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| NPS < 50 (or drop by 20 points)          | • Conduct a focus group with the team, identify causes.<br>• Make adjustments to interface, training. |
| Increase in manual overrides >10%        | • Analyse causes (false signals, interface inconvenience).<br>• Adjust model weights or thresholds. |
| Task neglect (overdue >2 days)           | • Remind manager of importance.<br>• Check if there are too many tasks (overload). |
| Increase in support requests             | • Assign an additional operator temporarily.<br>• Improve documentation. |
| High turnover                            | • Conduct exit interviews, identify link to automation.<br>• Adjust roles, add motivation. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON ORGANISATIONAL RISKS**

- Managing organisational risks requires no less careful planning
  than technical implementation.
- Key principles: transparency, involvement, demonstration of quick wins,
  preserving human‑in‑the‑loop.
- Continuous monitoring of satisfaction and engagement metrics enables
  early problem detection.
- The success of implementation depends on how teams perceive automation
  as a helper, not a threat.
