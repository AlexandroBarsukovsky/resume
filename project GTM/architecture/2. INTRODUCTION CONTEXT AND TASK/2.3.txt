**2.3. PROJECT GOALS AND CONSTRAINTS**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.3.1. STRATEGIC GOALS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The project to create the target GTM infrastructure for Sourcegraph is aimed at systematically overcoming the limitations of the current model (PLG) and building a scalable, automated platform for enterprise sales. The main strategic goals are:

1. **Complete the GTM transformation**  
   Transition from a manual, fragmented organization to a unified event‑driven system where all actions are triggered by data, and people focus on strategy and exceptions.

2. **Ensure scalability**  
   Create an architecture capable of supporting ARR growth to $100M+ and handling up to 10⁵ events per second without a proportional increase in manual labor.

3. **Improve management accuracy**  
   Implement mathematical scoring and forecasting models that provide transparency and predictability of the business.

4. **Strengthen competitive advantage**  
   Through speed of response to market signals (intent), proactive customer experience management, and accurate resource planning.

5. **Preserve and strengthen culture**  
   All automation must align with the principles of high agency, remote‑first, written transparency, enhancing team capabilities rather than limiting them.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.3.2. OPERATIONAL GOALS (KPIs)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Metric                                     | Baseline Value | Target Value                         |
|---------------------------------------------|----------------|--------------------------------------|
| Reaction time to intent signal              | up to 2 days   | ≤2 hours                             |
| Win rate (enterprise)                       | ~20%           | >25% (+15–20% relative increase)     |
| Deal cycle                                  | 90–120 days    | ≤96 days (-20%)                      |
| Net retention (NRR)                         | 100–110%       | >120%                                |
| Share of customers in green health score zone| not measured  | >80%                                 |
| Quarterly forecast accuracy (MAPE)          | 20–30%         | <10% (accuracy >90%)                 |
| Pipeline coverage                           | <2×            | >3×                                  |
| Churn rate (enterprise)                     | 5–10%          | <5%                                  |
| CAC Payback                                 | >24 months     | <18 months                           |
| Rule of 40 (ARR Growth + EBITDA Margin)     | <30%           | >40%                                 |

All target values are based on benchmarking of enterprise‑segment leaders (SaaS companies with similar models) and have been validated through multi‑variant simulations.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.3.3. FINANCIAL AND ECONOMIC GOALS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Return on investment** – payback period no more than 12 months.
- **Profitability** – ROI >300% over three years (conservative scenario).
- **Net present value (NPV)** – at least $25 million over five years.
- **Budget compliance** – CAPEX within $2.0–2.5M, OPEX ≤$2.0M/year.
- **Efficiency** – each dollar invested in automation must yield at least $3 in additional revenue over three years.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.3.4. TECHNICAL GOALS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Single source of truth** – integration of Salesforce, Snowflake, Hightouch,
  6sense, Gainsight, SalesLoft, Tableau into a unified data fabric.
- **Event‑driven architecture** – all data changes generate events that automatically trigger actions (triggers).
- **Automation of routine operations** – at least 80% of repetitive tasks (task creation, field updates, notification sending) must be performed by agents without human involvement.
- **Scalability** – the system must withstand loads of up to 10⁵ events per second and 3,000 simultaneously active agents.
- **Reliability** – 99.9% availability, automatic recovery after failures, recovery time (RTO) <1 hour.
- **Security** – encryption at rest and in transit, compliance with SOC2 Type II and GDPR, audit of all actions.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.3.5. ORGANIZATIONAL GOALS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Reduce manual labor** – reduce the time RevOps spends preparing reports by 80% (from 20 hours per month to 4 hours).
- **Transparency** – ensure access to up‑to‑date data on pipeline, health score, and forecasts for all management levels in real time.
- **Training and adaptation** – develop documentation and playbooks that enable new employees to reach full effectiveness twice as fast.
- **Team satisfaction** – prevent a drop in engagement due to automation; instead, increase it by eliminating routine.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.3.6. PROJECT CONSTRAINTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Budget constraints**
- CAPEX (one‑time costs for development, integration, training) – no more than $2.5M. Preliminary estimate: $2.0M (agent development, integration, training).
- OPEX (annual operating costs) – no more than $2.0M. Preliminary estimate: $1.7M (cloud resources, licenses, support).

**Timeline**
- Phase 1 (pilot) – no more than 2 months.
- Full implementation (all 4 phases) – within 12 months from start.
- Key milestones: after 6 months – scaling to all enterprise accounts.

**Technical constraints**
- Preserve the existing stack: cannot replace Salesforce, Snowflake, Hightouch, 6sense, Gainsight, SalesLoft, Tableau. Modifications via API, custom objects, and asynchronous integrations are allowed.
- All integrations must use official APIs and comply with request rate limits.
- The system must be deployed in AWS (current cloud provider) using services already approved by security (Lambda, Kubernetes, EventBridge, KMS).
- Compatibility with the current version of Salesforce (API version 58+).

**Cultural constraints**
- Automation must not create a “police” feeling; all rules must be transparent, and people must have the ability to override agent decisions in exceptional cases.
- Written communication remains a priority; interfaces and documentation must be self‑sufficient.
- High agency – the system should provide tools, not impose rigid scenarios; agents are assistants, not replacements.

**Security and compliance**
- All data processed by the system must comply with SOC2 Type II and GDPR requirements.
- Access to critical functions (changing model weights, manual intervention) must be restricted and logged.
- Agents must not be able to perform actions beyond their authority (principle of least privilege).

**Volume constraints**
- The system must support at least 3,000 simultaneously active agents and up to 10⁵ events per second at peak loads.
- The data warehouse (Snowflake) must be designed to accommodate volume growth of 50% per year.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.3.7. SUCCESS CRITERIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The project is considered successfully implemented if, after 12 months, the following results are achieved:

1. **Operational** – all target KPIs from section 2.3.2 are met or exceeded.
2. **Technical** – the system operates stably under load, recovery time after failures <1 hour, no security incidents.
3. **Financial** – CAPEX and OPEX within budget, payback <12 months.
4. **Organizational** – Sales, CS, and RevOps teams confirm a reduction in manual labor and increased satisfaction (survey).
5. **Scalability** – the system is ready for further growth without requiring architectural changes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.3.8. DEPENDENCIES AND PREREQUISITES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The following prerequisites are necessary for successful project implementation:

- Availability of up‑to‑date data in Salesforce and Snowflake (historical depth of at least 2 years for model calibration).
- Access to the APIs of 6sense, Gainsight, SalesLoft with the necessary permissions.
- Dedicated RevOps team (at least 2 people) to participate in setup and acceptance of results.
- Approval of budget and resources by management (CRO, CPO, CFO).
- Coordination of the implementation plan with Sales, CS, and Marketing teams.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**PROJECT CONCLUSIONS**
- The project goals and constraints are clearly defined, measurable, and aligned with the company’s current state.
- The success of the project depends on the balance between automation and the preservation of human control, as well as the teams’ readiness for change.
- Subsequent sections of the document (architecture, models, roadmap) are built upon these goals and within the specified constraints.
