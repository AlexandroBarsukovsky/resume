**2.4. DOCUMENT STRUCTURE**

This document, “Target GTM Infrastructure for Sourcegraph”, is a complete architectural specification intended for company leadership, RevOps, Sales, CS, and technical specialists involved in implementation. The document follows a “general to specific” structure and includes 12 main sections, as well as appendices with detailed data and materials for various audiences.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**2.4.1. GENERAL DOCUMENT STRUCTURE LOGIC**
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. **Introductory Part (Sections 1–2)** – sets the context, formulates objectives, describes the current state and constraints. Aimed at a broad audience, including C‑level, investors, key stakeholders.

2. **Diagnostic Part (Section 3)** – detailed analysis of the current GTM state, identification of pain points, quantitative assessment of the gap between the current and target states.

3. **Architectural Part (Sections 4–6)** – description of the target architecture, CRM design, scoring and forecasting models. Aimed at RevOps, technical specialists, architects.

4. **Planning and Organizational Part (Sections 7–10)** – resource planning, process automation, roles and responsibilities, implementation roadmap. Aimed at managers, executives, implementation teams.

5. **Analytical Part (Section 11)** – risks and mitigations, uncertainty management. Aimed at management and the project office.

6. **Appendices (Section 12)** – technical details, formulas, SQL queries, configurations, materials for non‑technical audiences, glossary. Aimed at all stakeholders.

**2.4.3. PRESENTATION STYLE**
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Style** – businesslike, direct, without excessive detail, but with the necessary depth. All statements are supported by data, references to sources, or modeling results.
- **Target audience** – the document is intended for a mixed audience: leadership (C‑level), operational teams (RevOps, Sales, CS), technical specialists (engineers, architects). Appropriate levels of detail are provided for each group (Executive Summary for leadership, technical specifications for engineers, simple language for a broad audience).
- **Visualization** – the final version will include diagrams (four‑level orchestration scheme, data flows, microservice architecture), which are placed in the appendices (section 12.4).

- **Change Management Plan** – based on section 11 (risks) and section 10 (roadmap).

**PROJECT CONCLUSIONS**
- The document structure ensures a logical transition from the general context to detailed technical solutions and an implementation plan.
- Each section has a clear purpose and target audience, making the document easier to use for different stakeholder groups.
