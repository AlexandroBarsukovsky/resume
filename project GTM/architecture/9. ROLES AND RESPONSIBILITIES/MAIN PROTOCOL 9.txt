**9. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES**

In the target GTM architecture, six key roles are clearly defined, and their
interaction ensures an end-to-end process from customer acquisition to expansion.
Each role has an area of responsibility, a set of tasks, KPIs, and is integrated
with automated agents (Levels 1–4). Automation does not replace people but
augments them, freeing them from routine tasks and providing timely data
for decision-making. Below is a general description of the roles, their place
in the customer lifecycle, and their interaction with the system. Separate protocols
(9.1–9.6) will detail each role, including job descriptions, competency requirements,
a typical workday, and KPIs.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1. SALES STRATEGIST (DATA ANALYST, INTENT WORK, HANDOFF TO AE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Sales Strategist is an analytical role that replaces the traditional SDR.
The main task is to turn intent signals and customer data into qualified
opportunities for Account Executives. The Strategist does not engage in blind
“cold” calling but uses data (Account Score, ICP_Fit, product activity) to select
the most promising accounts and personalise outreach.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Monitor intent signals (6sense) and prioritise accounts with a high
  Account Score (S>0.7).
- Conduct initial outreach (email, LinkedIn) using personalised templates
  generated by tactical groups.
- Qualify accounts according to MEDDPICC criteria (at an initial level).
- Create an Opportunity in Salesforce and hand it over to the AE with the necessary context.
- Participate in weekly syncs with RevOps to adjust scoring thresholds.

**KPIs:**
- Number of qualified Opportunities per month.
- Outreach to Opportunity conversion rate (target >20%).
- Response time to intent signals (target <2 hours).
- Handover accuracy (percentage of Opportunities handed over to AE that progress to a demo).

**Tools:** Salesforce (tasks, Opportunity), SalesLoft, 6sense, Tableau.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2. ACCOUNT EXECUTIVE (DEAL MANAGEMENT, MEDDPICC QUALIFICATION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Account Executive is responsible for closing enterprise deals, managing the funnel,
and accurate forecasting. In the target architecture, the AE receives qualified
opportunities from the Strategist but can also initiate deals independently
(e.g., through partner channels). The main focus is on MEDDPICC methodology and
using data to increase win rates.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Conduct demos and negotiations with potential customers.
- Qualify deals using MEDDPICC, fill in MEDDPICC_Fields__c
  in Salesforce (via a custom interface).
- Regularly update stages, closing probability (Win_Probability__c),
  and forecast category (Forecast_Category__c).
- Coordinate with Legal and technical specialists during contract preparation.
- Participate in weekly forecast sessions (using agent-provided data).

**KPIs:**
- ARR quota (quarterly plan attainment).
- Win rate (enterprise, target >25%).
- Average sales cycle length (target <96 days).
- Forecast accuracy (individual MAPE <15%).

**Tools:** Salesforce (Opportunity, MEDDPICC), Ironclad, ZoomInfo, Tableau.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3. CUSTOMER SUCCESS MANAGER (RETENTION, HEALTH SCORE, PLAYBOOKS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Customer Success Manager is responsible for onboarding, retention, and customer health.
In the target architecture, the CSM receives automatic signals (Health Score, Risk Flag)
and uses playbooks (retention, expansion) for timely intervention.
Agents take over monitoring, and the CSM focuses on human interaction.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Conduct onboarding of new customers (TTFV <30 days).
- Respond to risk signals (Health Score <0.5) – launch retention playbook,
  personal meetings.
- Conduct regular business reviews (QBRs) with customers.
- Collect NPS and feedback, record in Gainsight.
- Collaborate with AM when expansion signals are identified.

**KPIs:**
- Net Retention (NRR) – target >120%.
- Percentage of customers in the green Health Score zone (>0.8) – >80%.
- Time to First Value (TTFV) – <30 days.
- Churn rate (enterprise) – <5%.

**Tools:** Gainsight, Salesforce (Account, Task), Tableau, product analytics.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4. ACCOUNT MANAGER (EXPANSION, CONTRACT RENEWALS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Account Manager focuses on increasing revenue from existing customers
(upsell, cross‑sell) and contract renewals. In an automated environment, the AM
receives expansion signals from agents, as well as commercial proposals
generated by financial agents.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Work with expansion signals: prepare and present expansion proposals.
- Conduct contract renewal negotiations.
- Coordinate with CSM to ensure a positive customer experience.
- Record results in Salesforce (create expansion Opportunities,
  update contracts).

**KPIs:**
- Expansion ARR (additional revenue from existing customers).
- Renewal rate (percentage of renewed contracts) – target >90%.
- Response time to expansion signals (target <2 business days).
- Conversion of expansion signals into closed deals – >30%.

**Tools:** Salesforce (Opportunity, Account), Gainsight, Ironclad, Tableau.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.5. REVOPS (AGENT HOLDING MANAGEMENT, AUTOMATION CONFIGURATION, REPORTING)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Revenue Operations (RevOps) serves as the architect and administrator of the GTM system.
In the target architecture, RevOps manages the agent holding, configures triggers,
thresholds, monitors data quality, and prepares analytics for leadership.
RevOps operators also handle exceptions (Dead Letter Queue) and train models.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Monitor agent operations (load, errors, performance) via the control panel.
- Configure weights in Account Score, Health Score formulas, and trigger thresholds.
- Manage integrations (Salesforce, 6sense, Gainsight, SalesLoft).
- Prepare weekly digests and quarterly reports (QBR, board‑packs)
  using reporting agents.
- Retrain scoring and forecasting models on new data.
- Coordinate the annual planning process (OKRs, budgeting).

**KPIs:**
- Forecast accuracy (MAPE <10%).
- Report preparation time (QBR <2 hours).
- Incident response time (DLQ) – <2 hours.
- Internal customer satisfaction (NPS from Sales, CS teams).

**Tools:** Control panel, Salesforce, Snowflake, Tableau, AWS, Kafka.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.6. CONTROL PANEL OPERATORS (MONITORING, EXCEPTION HANDLING)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Control Panel Operators are a specialised subgroup within RevOps (or a separate role)
that monitors the agent holding in real time and handles situations that cannot be
automated. They provide “human in the loop” for critical decisions and ensure that
automation does not become a black box.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Monitor control panel dashboards (overall view, agent load, problem areas).
- Process the Dead Letter Queue (DLQ): manually execute actions that
  could not be automated, or restart failed tasks.
- Manually override agent decisions (e.g., change ideology,
  manually assign AE for a strategic account).
- Escalate complex cases to managers.
- Maintain an incident log and provide feedback for model retraining.

**KPIs:**
- DLQ processing time (average) – <1 hour.
- Percentage of manual interventions (to decrease over time).
- Number of incidents that escalated.

**Tools:** Control panel, Salesforce, Slack, PagerDuty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**ROLE INTERACTION IN THE AUTOMATED ENVIRONMENT**

All roles work within a unified information environment where automated handovers
(see Section 8.3) ensure seamless transfer of data and tasks:

- Strategist → AE: through Opportunity creation and automatic notification.
- AE → CSM: via the Closed Won event, which creates an onboarding task.
- CSM → AM: through expansion signals and the expansion playbook.
- RevOps ↔ all roles: via the control panel, reports, and alerts.

Automation does not reduce people’s responsibility; on the contrary, it gives them
more time for strategic tasks and human interaction.
