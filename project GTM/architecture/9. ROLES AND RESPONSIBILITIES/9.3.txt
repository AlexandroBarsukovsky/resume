**9.3. CUSTOMER SUCCESS MANAGER (RETENTION, HEALTH SCORE, PLAYBOOKS)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3.1. PURPOSE AND PLACE IN THE ORGANISATIONAL STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Customer Success Manager (CSM) is responsible for the long‑term success of customers
after the deal is closed. The main objective is to ensure the customer achieves their
target outcomes (time to first value, active usage), minimise churn, and identify
opportunities for expansion. In the target GTM architecture, the CSM uses automated
signals (Health Score, Risk Flag) and playbooks (retention, onboarding) to proactively
manage the customer experience. The CSM reports to the VP of Customer Success,
interacts with the AE (during handover), the AM (for expansion), and RevOps (data
feedback).

**Key value:** customer retention, increasing net retention, and turning users
into loyal partners.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3.2. MAIN RESPONSIBILITIES AND AREAS OF ACCOUNTABILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Area                         | Details                                                                  |
|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Onboarding**               | Conduct kick‑off meetings, training, assist with integration setup. Achieve Time to First Value (TTFV) <30 days. |
| **Health monitoring**        | Regularly track Health Score, Risk Flag, Expansion Signal. Use data from Gainsight and the control panel. |
| **Retention**                | When Health Score drops (<0.5), launch the retention playbook: personal communication, identify root causes, propose solutions. |
| **Regular reviews (QBR)**    | Conduct quarterly business reviews with customers, record results in Gainsight. |
| **Feedback collection**      | Run NPS surveys, record comments, pass them to product and marketing.    |
| **Opportunity identification** | When expansion signals appear (increased activity, requests), pass information to the AM and participate in proposal preparation. |
| **Product interaction**      | Report customer issues and requests to the product team (via tickets or regular syncs). |
| **Contract renewal**         | Coordinate renewals, prepare the proposal together with the AM.           |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3.3. COMPETENCIES AND REQUIREMENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Competency                 | Requirements                                                             |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Empathy and customer orientation** | Ability to build trusting relationships, understand the customer’s business needs. |
| **Analytical thinking**    | Interpret Health Score, usage metrics, NPS. Ability to identify root causes of problems. |
| **Product knowledge**      | Deep understanding of Code Search, Deep Search, MCP, as well as typical usage scenarios for developers and engineering teams. |
| **Technical literacy**     | Experience with Gainsight, Salesforce, product analytics (Amplitude/Snowflake). |
| **Communication**          | Excellent written and verbal communication, ability to give presentations, conduct training webinars. |
| **Experience**             | 3+ years in Customer Success in B2B SaaS (preferably in technology companies). Experience with enterprise customers is an advantage. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3.4. TYPICAL WORKDAY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Time        | Action                                                                   |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 9:00–9:30   | Check control panel: list of customers with red (H<0.5) and yellow (0.5≤H<0.8) status. |
| 9:30–11:00  | Customer meetings: QBRs, problem resolution, training (usually 2–3 meetings). |
| 11:00–12:00 | Onboarding new customers (introductory sessions, setup).                 |
| 12:00–13:00 | Lunch.                                                                   |
| 13:00–14:00 | Data analysis: review usage reports, prepare for meetings.               |
| 14:00–15:00 | Retention playbook work: calls to red‑zone customers, record results in Gainsight. |
| 15:00–16:00 | Internal syncs: discuss expansion signals with AM, pass requests to product. |
| 16:00–17:00 | Complete tasks, log activity, plan for the next day.                     |

**Note:** times may vary depending on the number of customers in the portfolio and their status.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3.5. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| KPI                                  | Formula / Measurement Method                     | Target Value                |
|--------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Net Retention (NRR)**              | (ARR_start + Expansion – Churn) / ARR_start      | >120%                       |
| **Percentage of customers in green zone** | Number of customers with Health_Score ≥0.8 / total active customers | >80%                        |
| **Time to First Value (TTFV)**       | Average time from deal closure to first active usage (product event) | <30 days                    |
| **Churn rate (enterprise)**          | (Lost ARR) / ARR_start                           | <5%                         |
| **Retention playbook success rate**  | Percentage of customers where Health Score returned to ≥0.5 after playbook launch | >60%                        |
| **NPS (portfolio average)**          | Survey results                                   | >50 (0–100 scale)           |

All KPIs are tracked in Gainsight and Tableau; the CSM has access to a personal
dashboard with per‑customer dynamics.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3.6. INTERACTION WITH AUTOMATION AND AGENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Automated component      | Role in CSM’s work                                                       |
|--------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Analyst agents (Level 1)** | Daily update Health Score, Risk Flag, Expansion Signal. The CSM receives up‑to‑date data without manual collection. |
| **Retention playbook (8.2.1)** | When H<0.5, a task is automatically created for the CSM and an email is sent to the customer. The CSM focuses on personal communication rather than task creation. |
| **Gainsight integration**      | Automatically synchronises Health Score, NPS, playbooks. The CSM uses Gainsight to execute playbooks and track history. |
| **Control panel**          | The CSM sees a dashboard of their customers with colour‑coded status, can filter by risks, quickly access tasks. |
| **Financial agents**       | Provide forecast data for expansion, but the CSM does not use them directly. |

**Human actions not replaceable by automation:**
- Conducting meetings, training, empathetic communication.
- In‑depth analysis of Health Score decline causes (beyond automated signals).
- Communicating complex customer requests to product.
- Interacting with C‑level customers during strategic discussions.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3.7. INTERACTION WITH OTHER ROLES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Role            | Interaction                                                              |
|-----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **AE**          | Receives information about the customer after deal closure; may participate in initial meetings for support. |
| **AM**          | Joint work on expansion signals: the CSM shares information about customer readiness, participates in proposal preparation. |
| **RevOps**      | Reports data issues (inaccurate Health Score, delays), helps configure playbooks. |
| **Product**     | Transfers feature requests, bug reports, analyses of feature usage.      |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.3.8. TRAINING AND ONBOARDING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Onboarding:** 4 weeks: deep product familiarisation, tools
  (Gainsight, Salesforce), Customer Success methodology, playbooks.
- **Mentoring:** first 2 months the CSM works with a mentor on complex cases.
- **Certification:** internal certification on product and Gainsight usage.
- **Continuing education:** quarterly training on advanced negotiation skills,
  data analysis, working with enterprise customers.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON THE CUSTOMER SUCCESS MANAGER ROLE**

- The CSM is a key link in retaining and expanding the customer base,
  using automated signals (Health Score) and playbooks for proactive
  management.
- Automation (monitoring, task creation, playbooks) frees the CSM from routine,
  allowing focus on human interaction and strategy.
- KPIs focus on long‑term success (NRR, churn, TTFV) and quality
  of customer experience.
- The role requires high empathy, analytical skills, and deep product
  knowledge.
