**9.1. SALES STRATEGIST (DATA ANALYST, INTENT WORK, HANDOFF TO AE)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1.1. PURPOSE AND PLACE IN THE ORGANISATIONAL STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Sales Strategist is a key role in the target GTM architecture, replacing
the traditional SDR (Sales Development Representative). Unlike an SDR who makes
“cold” calls blindly, the Strategist acts as an analyst, using data (intent signals,
product activity, ICP) to prioritise accounts and personalise outreach. The Strategist
sits at the intersection of Marketing (data) and Sales (handoff to AE). In the
hierarchy, the Strategist reports to the VP of Sales, but works closely with RevOps
and Marketing.

**Key value:** turning raw data into qualified opportunities, reducing response time
to market signals, and improving the quality of handoffs to AEs.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1.2. MAIN RESPONSIBILITIES AND AREAS OF ACCOUNTABILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Area                         | Details                                                                  |
|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Intent data monitoring**   | Regularly track the list of accounts with Account Score >0.7 (automatically generated by agents). Verify the validity of high scoring (e.g., not a false signal). |
| **Account prioritisation**   | Based on Account Score, ICP_Fit, historical activity, determine the order of outreach. Use data from Salesforce and the control panel. |
| **Outreach (initial)**       | Conduct personalised outreach via SalesLoft (email) and LinkedIn. Use templates adapted by tactical groups (Level 3). Record results in Salesforce. |
| **Qualification**            | Determine whether the account meets the criteria for handoff to an AE. Use initial MEDDPICC questions (pain points, budget, timeline). |
| **Opportunity creation**     | Upon positive qualification, create an Opportunity in Salesforce, filling in fields: Account, Contact, Stage (Prospecting), Amount (estimate), MEDDPICC_Fields__c (initial data). Assign an AE (if the system does not do so automatically). |
| **Handoff to AE**            | Conduct handoff: a brief briefing (in a task or meeting) with key information about the customer, identified pain points, contacts. |
| **Data feedback**            | Inform RevOps of cases where Account Score was high but qualification was not confirmed (false positives) to improve the model. |
| **Participation in planning** | Participate in weekly GTM syncs, share observations about signal quality and conversion. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1.3. COMPETENCIES AND REQUIREMENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Competency                 | Requirements                                                             |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Analytical thinking**    | Ability to interpret data (Account Score, Intent_Strength, ICP_Fit) and make decisions based on it. |
| **GTM process knowledge**  | Understanding of the sales cycle, MEDDPICC methodology (initial level).  |
| **Communication skills**   | Strong written and verbal communication, ability to tailor messages to different segments (industries, customer size). |
| **Technical literacy**     | Experience with Salesforce (creating tasks, Opportunities), SalesLoft (sequences), 6sense (interpreting signals). |
| **Self‑organisation**      | Ability to work in a remote environment, meet SLAs (response time), log activities. |
| **Experience**             | 2–4 years in sales, SDR, analytics, or related roles. Knowledge of the technology sector (DevOps, software development) is an advantage. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1.4. TYPICAL WORKDAY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Time        | Action                                                                   |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 9:00–9:30   | Check control panel: list of new accounts with Account Score >0.7, review history, intent signals. |
| 9:30–11:00  | Outreach: send personalised emails via SalesLoft, connect on LinkedIn. Log results. |
| 11:00–12:00 | Qualification: handle inbound responses, phone calls (if necessary).     |
| 12:00–13:00 | Lunch.                                                                   |
| 13:00–14:00 | Create Opportunities for confirmed accounts, handoff to AE (brief briefing). |
| 14:00–15:00 | Data analysis: review conversion reports, discuss signal quality with RevOps. |
| 15:00–16:00 | Additional outreach to accounts with medium Score (0.4–0.7) for nurturing. |
| 16:00–17:00 | Complete tasks, log activities, plan next day.                           |

**Note:** time distribution may vary depending on region and seasonality.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1.5. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| KPI                                  | Formula / Measurement Method                     | Target Value                |
|--------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Number of qualified opportunities** (Opportunities created) | Total Opportunities created per month          | >15 per month               |
| **Outreach → Opportunity conversion** | (Opportunities created) / (number of outreach activities) | >20%                        |
| **Response time to intent signal**   | Time from Account Score >0.7 appearing to first contact (email/call) | <2 hours                    |
| **Handoff accuracy to AE**           | Percentage of Opportunities handed over to AE that reach Demo stage (or move to Proposal) | >60%                        |
| **Data quality**                     | Percentage of Opportunities with MEDDPICC_Fields__c filled (at least 3 fields) | >80%                        |

All KPIs are automatically tracked via Tableau and Salesforce dashboards; the Strategist
has access to their personal dashboard.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1.6. INTERACTION WITH AUTOMATION AND AGENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Automated component      | Role in Strategist’s work                                                |
|--------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Analyst agents (Level 1)** | Daily generate the list of priority accounts (Account Score >0.7) and update Intent_Strength, ICP_Fit. The Strategist relies on this data. |
| **Qualification playbook (8.2.3)** | Automatically creates a task in Salesforce when S>0.7; the Strategist receives a notification. |
| **SalesLoft integration**   | Automatically adds the account to a cadence (sequence) when S>0.7. The Strategist can personalise the message before sending. |
| **Control panel**          | The Strategist sees their task dashboard, KPIs, and the list of accounts to process. |
| **Financial agents**       | Not directly used, but provide data on potential deal size (Estimated_Amount__c field), which the Strategist can use when creating an Opportunity. |

**Human actions not replaceable by automation:**
- Decision on qualification (even with a high Score, there may be reasons to delay).
- Personalisation of outreach (templates provide a foundation, but the Strategist adapts to the specific context).
- Handoff to AE – a brief verbal or written briefing (although the system transfers data, human clarification is important).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1.7. INTERACTION WITH OTHER ROLES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Role            | Interaction                                                              |
|-----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **AE**          | Receives qualified Opportunities from the Strategist; the Strategist may participate in initial meetings if needed. |
| **RevOps**      | The Strategist reports data issues (false signals, inaccurate ICP) to help improve models. RevOps configures scoring thresholds. |
| **Marketing**   | The Strategist provides feedback on the quality of intent signals, helping to adjust ABM campaigns. |
| **CSM / AM**    | Not directly involved during the qualification stage, but the Strategist may share information about the customer if the account already exists. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1.8. TRAINING AND ONBOARDING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Onboarding:** 2–3 weeks: familiarisation with tools (Salesforce, SalesLoft, 6sense), MEDDPICC methodology, target segments and ICP.
- **Mentoring:** first two weeks the new Strategist works with a mentor (experienced colleague) on complex accounts.
- **Documentation:** access to a handbook describing playbooks, templates, qualification rules.
- **Continuing education:** quarterly workshops on data analysis, intent work, and communication skills development.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON THE SALES STRATEGIST ROLE**

- The Strategist is the analytical link between data and sales, using
  automation to increase efficiency.
- The role requires a combination of analytical skills, communication,
  and process knowledge.
- Automation (priority account lists, automatic tasks, templates)
  frees the Strategist from routine work, allowing focus on qualification
  and handoff.
- KPIs focus on quality and speed, not on call volume,
  aligning with a high‑agency culture.
