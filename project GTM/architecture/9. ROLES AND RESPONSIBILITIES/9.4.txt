**9.4. ACCOUNT MANAGER (EXPANSION, CONTRACT RENEWALS)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4.1. PURPOSE AND PLACE IN THE ORGANISATIONAL STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Account Manager (AM) is the role responsible for maximising revenue from existing
customers through upsell and cross‑sell, as well as for successful contract renewals.
In the target GTM architecture, the AM receives automatic signals about a customer’s
readiness for expansion (Expansion Signal) and uses commercial proposals prepared
by financial agents. The AM works closely with the Customer Success Manager (CSM)
to ensure a positive customer experience and with the Account Executive (AE) when
handing over new opportunities. The AM reports to the VP of Customer Success (or
VP of Sales, depending on the organisational structure).

**Key value:** increasing net retention, growing expansion ARR,
strengthening long‑term relationships with key customers.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4.2. MAIN RESPONSIBILITIES AND AREAS OF ACCOUNTABILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Area                         | Details                                                                  |
|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Opportunity identification** | Regularly track expansion signals (increased activity, feature requests, renewals with growth). Use data from the control panel and Gainsight. |
| **Proposal preparation**     | Based on consumption data and input from financial agents, prepare commercial proposals for upsell/cross‑sell. Approve discounts within delegated authority. |
| **Negotiations**             | Conduct negotiations with customers on expansion and renewals. Participate in new product demonstrations (jointly with SE). |
| **Contract renewals**        | Track contract expiration dates, initiate the renewal process 90–120 days prior to expiry. Prepare renewal proposals, coordinate with Legal. |
| **Coordination with CSM**    | Share information about customer health, jointly plan QBRs, participate in discussions of health score and expansion plans. |
| **CRM updates**              | Create expansion and renewal Opportunities in Salesforce, update stages, amounts, probabilities. Record negotiation outcomes. |
| **Feedback**                 | Provide RevOps with feedback on the quality of expansion signals (false positives, missed opportunities) to improve models. |
| **Performance analysis**     | Regularly analyse conversion from expansion signals to closed deals, average expansion size, cycle time. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4.3. COMPETENCIES AND REQUIREMENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Competency                 | Requirements                                                             |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Negotiation skills**     | Proven experience in commercial negotiations for expansion and renewals, ability to work within customer budget constraints. |
| **Analytical thinking**    | Interpret expansion signals, calculate ROI for the customer, assess expansion potential. |
| **Product knowledge**      | Deep understanding of Code Search, Deep Search, MCP, including expansion capabilities (licensing, new modules). |
| **Technical literacy**     | Experience with Salesforce (Opportunity), Gainsight, Tableau. Understanding of data principles. |
| **Communication**          | Excellent written and verbal communication, ability to present commercial proposals, build relationships with C‑level. |
| **Experience**             | 3+ years in Account Management, Sales, Customer Success in B2B SaaS. Experience with enterprise customers is an advantage. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4.4. TYPICAL WORKDAY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Time        | Action                                                                   |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 9:00–9:30   | Check control panel: list of customers with expansion signals, upcoming renewals, open tasks. |
| 9:30–11:00  | Customer meetings: expansion negotiations, new product presentations (1–2 meetings). |
| 11:00–12:00 | Proposal preparation: analyse consumption data, calculate commercial terms, approve discounts. |
| 12:00–13:00 | Lunch.                                                                   |
| 13:00–14:00 | Internal calls: sync with CSM on customer status, discuss expansion strategy. |
| 14:00–15:00 | Renewal work: send reminders, prepare renewal proposals, coordinate with Legal. |
| 15:00–16:00 | Update Salesforce: create Opportunities, update stages, forecasts.       |
| 16:00–17:00 | Analytics: review reports on expansion signal conversion, prepare for QBR. |

**Note:** times may vary depending on deal stage and seasonality.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4.5. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| KPI                                  | Formula / Measurement Method                     | Target Value                |
|--------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Expansion ARR**                    | Total additional revenue from upsell/cross‑sell (annual) | >30% of base ARR (or per quota) |
| **Renewal rate**                     | (Number of renewed contracts) / (Number of expiring) | >90%                        |
| **Expansion signal conversion**      | (Number of closed expansion deals) / (Number of expansion signals) | >30%                        |
| **Response time to expansion signal**| Time from signal appearance to first customer contact | <2 business days            |
| **Average expansion size**           | Average additional contract value                | >20% of current ACV         |
| **Pipeline coverage (expansion)**    | Expansion pipeline sum / quarterly target        | >3×                         |

All KPIs are tracked in Tableau and Salesforce; the AM has access to a personal
dashboard with per‑customer dynamics and trends.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4.6. INTERACTION WITH AUTOMATION AND AGENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Automated component      | Role in AM’s work                                                        |
|--------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Analyst agents (Level 1)** | Daily compute expansion signals based on activity, user growth, requests. AM receives ready‑made signals. |
| **Expansion playbook (8.2.2)** | When Expansion_Signal = true, a task is automatically created for the AM and a commercial proposal is prepared (by financial agents). The AM focuses on negotiations. |
| **Financial agents (Level 4)** | Generate commercial proposals (preliminary calculations) that the AM can use as a basis. |
| **Gainsight integration**      | Tracks renewals and expansion signals; the AM can launch the “Expansion” playbook from Gainsight. |
| **Control panel**          | The AM sees the list of customers with expansion signals, upcoming renewals, KPIs. |
| **Triggers (T13)**            | When a renewal with an increase >110% occurs, a task is automatically created for the AM. |

**Human actions not replaceable by automation:**
- Negotiations with C‑level customers, aligning expansion with customer business goals.
- Deep understanding of needs, adapting proposals to specific situations.
- Involvement in complex renewals (legal approvals, competitive bidding).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4.7. INTERACTION WITH OTHER ROLES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Role            | Interaction                                                              |
|-----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CSM**         | Regular syncs on customer status, joint identification of expansion signals. CSM helps prepare the customer for expansion. |
| **AE**          | For cross‑selling new products, the AE may participate in demos and negotiations, especially if the customer is unfamiliar with the new module. |
| **RevOps**      | Provides feedback on the quality of expansion signals, helps refine models. RevOps configures playbooks. |
| **Legal**       | For renewals and large expansions, the AM coordinates with Legal to prepare contracts (Ironclad). |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.4.8. TRAINING AND ONBOARDING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Onboarding:** 4 weeks: familiarisation with the product (focus on expansion
  capabilities), tools (Salesforce, Gainsight), negotiation methodology,
  playbooks.
- **Mentoring:** first 2–3 months the AM works with a mentor on complex deals.
- **Certification:** internal product and commercial process certification.
- **Continuing education:** quarterly training on negotiations, financial
  modelling, renewal management.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON THE ACCOUNT MANAGER ROLE**

- The AM is a key driver of revenue growth from existing customers,
  using automated expansion signals and proposals prepared by financial
  agents.
- Automation (signal identification, task creation, calculation preparation)
  frees the AM from routine work, allowing focus on negotiations and
  strategic portfolio management.
- KPIs focus on expansion (expansion ARR, conversion) and retention
  (renewal rate).
- The role requires high proficiency in negotiation, analytical thinking,
  and deep product knowledge.
