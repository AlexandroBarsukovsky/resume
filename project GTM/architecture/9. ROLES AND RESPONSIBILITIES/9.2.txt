**9.2. ACCOUNT EXECUTIVE (DEAL MANAGEMENT, MEDDPICC QUALIFICATION)**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2.1. PURPOSE AND PLACE IN THE ORGANISATIONAL STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Account Executive (AE) is the central role in the sales cycle, responsible for turning
qualified opportunities into closed deals. In the target GTM architecture, the AE
works closely with the Sales Strategist (receives ready‑made Opportunities),
but can also initiate deals independently through partner channels or direct contacts.
The main task is to conduct complex enterprise negotiations, apply MEDDPICC methodology,
and ensure accurate forecasting. The AE reports to the VP of Sales (or Sales Director),
interacts with RevOps, Legal, and with the CSM after deal closure.

**Key value:** closing large contracts that drive ARR growth,
and maintaining forecast accuracy for business planning.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2.2. MAIN RESPONSIBILITIES AND AREAS OF ACCOUNTABILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Area                         | Details                                                                  |
|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Acceptance and qualification** | Receive Opportunities from Strategist or create them independently. Perform in‑depth qualification using MEDDPICC (see below). |
| **Deal management**          | Conduct product demonstrations (with SE involvement if needed), negotiate pricing, terms, timelines. Use standard contract templates. |
| **MEDDPICC qualification**   | Fill structured MEDDPICC_Fields__c (JSON) in Salesforce: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition. |
| **Forecasting**              | Regularly (weekly) update deal stage, closing probability (Win_Probability__c), and forecast category (Forecast_Category__c). Participate in forecast sessions with management. |
| **Contract work**            | Coordinate contract preparation with Legal (Ironclad), track signature. Ensure compliance with internal policies (discounts, terms). |
| **Feedback**                 | Record loss reasons (Lost_Reason__c) to improve models and processes. Report data or automation issues to RevOps. |
| **Interaction with CSM/AM**  | After deal closure, hand over information to CSM (onboarding). For expansion, coordinate with AM. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2.3. COMPETENCIES AND REQUIREMENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Competency                 | Requirements                                                             |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Negotiation skills**     | Proven experience in complex B2B negotiations, working with C‑level, legal departments. |
| **MEDDPICC methodology**   | Strong command of MEDDPICC, ability to ask the right questions and record answers in a structured way. |
| **Product expertise**      | Deep understanding of the code intelligence platform (Code Search, Deep Search, MCP), needs of developers and engineering teams. |
| **Technical literacy**     | Experience with Salesforce (Opportunity, reports), ZoomInfo, Ironclad. Understanding of how technology products work. |
| **Analytical skills**      | Ability to interpret data (win rates, deal cycles) and make prioritisation decisions. |
| **Experience**             | 5+ years in enterprise sales in SaaS/technology companies. Experience with developer‑focused products is an advantage. |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2.4. TYPICAL WORKDAY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Time        | Action                                                                   |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 9:00–9:30   | Check Salesforce: new Opportunities, MEDDPICC tasks, updates from Strategist. |
| 9:30–11:00  | Demos and meetings with potential customers (usually 2–3 meetings).      |
| 11:00–12:00 | Meeting preparation: gather customer data, configure demo, coordinate with SE. |
| 12:00–13:00 | Lunch.                                                                   |
| 13:00–14:00 | Update deals in Salesforce: stages, Win_Probability, MEDDPICC fields.    |
| 14:00–15:00 | Contract negotiations, communication with Legal, term alignment.         |
| 15:00–16:00 | Internal calls: forecast session with management, information exchange with CSM. |
| 16:00–17:00 | Work on incoming requests, log activities, plan next day.                |

**Note:** distribution may vary depending on deal stage and region.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2.5. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| KPI                                  | Formula / Measurement Method                     | Target Value                |
|--------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **ARR quota attainment**             | Closed revenue / quarterly quota                 | ≥100%                       |
| **Win rate (enterprise)**            | (Closed Won) / (Closed Won + Closed Lost)        | >25%                        |
| **Average sales cycle length**       | Average days from Opportunity creation to Closed Won | <96 days                    |
| **Forecast accuracy (individual)**   | MAPE (forecast vs actual)                        | <15%                        |
| **MEDDPICC completeness**            | Percentage of deals with MEDDPICC_Completeness__c >0.7 | >80%                        |
| **Pipeline coverage**                | Pipeline sum / quarterly quota                   | >3×                         |

All KPIs are tracked in Tableau and Salesforce; the AE has access to a personal
dashboard with trends and comparisons to the plan.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2.6. INTERACTION WITH AUTOMATION AND AGENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Automated component      | Role in AE’s work                                                        |
|--------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Analyst agents (Level 1)** | Provide customer data (Intent_Strength, Health_Score) that the AE can use when preparing for meetings. |
| **Financial agents (Level 4)** | Weekly update Win_Probability__c (based on model) and Forecast_Category__c, helping the AE verify their own estimates. |
| **Triggers (T12)**            | Remind to fill MEDDPICC fields if completeness is low.                   |
| **Automated contract creation (T05)** | When moving to “Proposal” stage, the system creates a contract in Ironclad, reducing manual work for the AE. |
| **Control panel**          | The AE can view their KPIs, load, forecasts; RevOps operators assist with issues. |
| **SalesLoft integration**   | Sequences for follow‑up are automatically created, but the AE can manage them manually. |

**Human actions not replaceable by automation:**
- Conducting demos and negotiations – core skills requiring empathy and persuasion.
- Interpreting complex customer requirements and adapting proposals.
- Making decisions on discounts and contract terms (within delegated authority).
- Engaging with C‑level customers.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2.7. INTERACTION WITH OTHER ROLES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Role            | Interaction                                                              |
|-----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Sales Strategist** | Receives qualified Opportunities; may participate in initial meetings for support. |
| **RevOps**      | Collaborates with RevOps to adjust forecasts, reports data issues. RevOps configures Win_Probability models. |
| **CSM**         | Hand over the customer after deal closure (onboarding); AE may participate in the handoff. |
| **AM**          | When expansion signals appear during a deal (e.g., customer requests more licenses) – AE coordinates with AM to incorporate into the contract. |
| **Legal**       | Interaction during contract preparation (via Ironclad and directly).     |

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.2.8. TRAINING AND ONBOARDING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Onboarding:** 4–6 weeks: familiarisation with the product (technical details), MEDDPICC methodology, tools (Salesforce, Ironclad), forecasting processes.
- **Mentoring:** first 3 months the AE works with a mentor (experienced colleague) on large deals.
- **Certification:** internal certification on MEDDPICC and product expertise.
- **Continuing education:** quarterly training on negotiations, competitive analysis, using data in sales.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**CONCLUSIONS ON THE ACCOUNT EXECUTIVE ROLE**

- The AE is a key link in converting opportunities into revenue, relying
  on MEDDPICC methodology and accurate forecasting.
- Automation (financial agents, reminders, contracts) frees the AE
  from routine work, allowing focus on negotiations and strategy.
- KPIs focus on quality (win rate, forecast accuracy) and efficiency
  (cycle length), not just volume.
- The role requires high proficiency in negotiations, technical literacy,
  and the ability to work with data.
