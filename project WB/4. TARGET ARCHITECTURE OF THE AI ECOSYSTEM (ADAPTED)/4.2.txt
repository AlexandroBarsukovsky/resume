**4.2. MULTI‑LEVEL ORCHESTRATION (4 LEVELS)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Multi‑level orchestration is the central mechanism of the target architecture,
implementing the principle of division of labour, proven effective in historical
simulations (from Roman quaestors to modern corporations). Each level has clearly
defined functions, independent scalability, and standardised data exchange interfaces.
Communication between levels is carried out via an event bus (Redis Streams / Kafka),
ensuring asynchronicity, reliability, and traceability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**4.2.1. LEVEL 1 – ANALYTICAL AGENTS (6)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Level 1 is the “eyes” of the system. Six specialised agents collect, enrich and
structure data from all available sources, transforming raw information into
analysis‑ready features.

**4.2.1.1. Competitive Intelligence**
- **Purpose:** monitoring competitor prices, promotions, assortment, and ratings.
- **Sources:** Wildberries (product card parsing), 6sense (optional), manual input.
- **Tracked parameters:** price, promotion status, rating, review count, stock availability, delivery time.
- **Frequency:** every 30 minutes.
- **Signals:** `competitor_price_drop` (price drop >5% in 24 hours), `competitor_promotion_started`, `competitor_new_product`.
- **Technologies:** Python, BeautifulSoup4/requests, Redis (cache), PostgreSQL.

**4.2.1.2. WB API Monitor**
- **Purpose:** collection of all operational data from the Wildberries personal account via the official API.
- **Endpoints:** orders, stock, reviews, sales statistics, finances, shipment requests.
- **Frequency:** real‑time (webhooks) for reviews, pull every 5–30 min for others.
- **Critical events:** `order.created`, `stock.low`, `review.new`, `finance.commission_updated`.
- **Technologies:** Python (aiohttp), Celery (periodic tasks), webhooks on FastAPI.

**4.2.1.3. Demand Analyst**
- **Purpose:** building sales forecasts (7, 30, 90 days) based on history, seasonality, weather.
- **Models:** Prophet (primary), ARIMA (short‑term), Holt‑Winters (medium‑term), ensemble.
- **Output:** forecast values with confidence intervals, MAPE, MAE.
- **Frequency:** daily (02:00) and on request.
- **Technologies:** Python (prophet, statsmodels, scikit‑learn), PostgreSQL.

**4.2.1.4. Review Processor (NLP)**
- **Purpose:** sentiment analysis, topic extraction, defect identification, draft response generation.
- **Models:** ruBERT (sentiment classification), embedding clustering, keyword extraction.
- **Output:** `sentiment`, `topics`, `defect_type`, `embedding` (pgvector), `auto_response`.
- **Frequency:** real‑time (on review arrival).
- **Technologies:** Python (transformers, pgvector), LLM (YandexGPT / GigaChat).

**4.2.1.5. News Monitor**
- **Purpose:** collecting and filtering news about Wildberries, metal products, competitors, legislation.
- **Sources:** RSS, Telegram channels, official notifications, Google News API.
- **Classification:** `marketplace_rule_change`, `competitor_news`, `industry_trend`, `general`.
- **Output:** daily digest, `critical_news` events for important changes.
- **Frequency:** collection every 30 min, digest once per day.

**4.2.1.6. Seasonal Forecaster**
- **Purpose:** calculating seasonal coefficients accounting for weather and holidays.
- **Method:** STL decomposition, adjustment via linear regression (temperature, precipitation).
- **Output:** `seasonal_factors` (baseline and adjusted) for each SKU.
- **Frequency:** monthly, also on anomalous weather deviations.
- **Technologies:** Python (statsmodels, scikit‑learn), integration with OpenWeatherMap.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**4.2.2. LEVEL 2 – STRATEGIC AGENTS (6)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Level 2 is the “brain” of the system. Agents at this level make decisions based on
analytics from Level 1 and current business objectives.

**4.2.2.1. Pricing Strategist**
- **Purpose:** determining the optimal price considering cost, competitor prices, demand elasticity, and seasonality.
- **Model:** logistic regression (purchase probability vs price and competitor prices) + minimum margin rule.
- **Input:** cost (from 1С), competitor prices (from agent 1), demand forecast (from agent 3), current margin.
- **Output:** recommended price, signal `price_adjustment_needed`.
- **Rule:** if deviation from current price >20% – requires owner confirmation; otherwise automatic application via Scriptor #3.
- **Frequency:** on every competitor price or cost change, also weekly.

**4.2.2.2. Assortment Strategist**
- **Purpose:** generating new product ideas, eliminating unprofitable items, optimising the assortment matrix.
- **Input:** sales by SKU, reviews (identified defects, wishes), competitor data (new products), trends (from agent 5).
- **Model:** ABC‑XYZ analysis for product classification, Pareto rule (20% of SKUs generate 80% of revenue).
- **Output:** proposals for new SKUs (via GENESIS), recommendations for removal, production focus shifts.
- **Frequency:** weekly, also when new trends appear.

**4.2.2.3. Risk Manager**
- **Purpose:** calculating the business Health Score, identifying risks of shortage, overstocking, margin decline.
- **Formula:** `H = 0.4·(1 – shortage_norm) + 0.3·(turnover_norm) + 0.3·(margin_norm)`, where `_norm` is normalisation 0..1.
- **Thresholds:** green zone (H ≥ 0.7), yellow zone (0.5 ≤ H < 0.7), red zone (H < 0.5).
- **Output:** colour indicator, `risk_alert` signals when entering the red zone, launch of retention playbook.
- **Frequency:** daily.

**4.2.2.4. Marketing Analyst**
- **Purpose:** optimising advertising budget allocation across products and channels.
- **Input:** advertising campaign data (CTR, conversion, ROMI), sales forecasts, seasonal coefficients.
- **Model:** linear optimisation (maximising ROMI under budget constraints).
- **Output:** recommended budget allocation by SKU and period, signal `budget_optimization`.
- **Frequency:** weekly.

**4.2.2.5. Quality Control**
- **Purpose:** analysing defects identified in reviews and providing feedback to production.
- **Input:** data from agent 4 (reviews with `defect_type`), defect reports from production.
- **Method:** defect clustering, frequency calculation, technology improvement suggestions.
- **Output:** defect reports, `quality_alert` signals when defect rate rises >10% per month.
- **Frequency:** weekly.

**4.2.2.6. Expansion Analyst**
- **Purpose:** analysing new sales channels (Ozon, Yandex.Market) and searching for expansion niches.
- **Input:** competitor data on other platforms (parsing), commissions, delivery conditions, audience size.
- **Method:** comparative analysis (entry costs, potential revenue increase).
- **Output:** recommendations for entering new platforms, channel prioritisation.
- **Frequency:** monthly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**4.2.3. LEVEL 3 – TACTICAL COORDINATORS (8)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Level 3 adapts strategy to product groups and coordinates execution.

**4.2.3.1. Product Group Coordinators (4)**
- **Specialisation:** pickets, angle brackets, wind strips, flashings.
- **Functions:**
  – receive strategic recommendations from level 2,
  – adapt them to the group’s characteristics (e.g., more aggressive pricing for pickets in season, year‑round stability for angle brackets),
  – distribute tasks among executive agents,
  – track execution and collect feedback.
- **Technologies:** Python, JSON rules, event‑bus integration.

**4.2.3.2. Scriptor #1 (Link)**
- **Purpose:** ensuring reliable data transfer between levels (especially between the core and the executive coalition).
- **Functions:**
  – receive events from levels 1 and 2,
  – form task queues for level 4,
  – delivery control (retry, DLQ),
  – maintain logs for audit.
- **Technologies:** Python, Redis Streams, REST API.

**4.2.3.3. Archivist**
- **Purpose:** storing and indexing all reports, logs, events.
- **Functions:**
  – save all agent operation protocols,
  – prepare data for dashboards (aggregation),
  – backup (daily to S3),
  – enable historical search (by SKU, date, event type).
- **Technologies:** PostgreSQL, S3, Python.

**4.2.3.4. Supervisor**
- **Purpose:** monitoring agent load, restarting failed ones, load balancing.
- **Functions:**
  – monitor task queues (Redis),
  – alerts for delays >30 sec or errors >2%,
  – automatic container restart (via orchestrator API),
  – escalate to operator on critical failures.
- **Technologies:** Python, Docker API, Kubernetes (when scaling).

**4.2.3.5. Production Scheduler**
- **Purpose:** generating production tasks based on forecasts, stock, and orders.
- **Input:** demand forecast (from agent 3), warehouse stock (from agent 2), current orders.
- **Output:** production task (date, SKU, quantity), assignment to teams.
- **Rules:** priority – fulfilling existing orders, then replenishing stock to target level.
- **Frequency:** daily.
