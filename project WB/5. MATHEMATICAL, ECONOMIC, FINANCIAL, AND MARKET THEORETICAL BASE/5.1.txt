**5.1.1. DEMAND FORECASTING**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Model:** combination of ARIMA(p,d,q) with parameter optimisation and Prophet for incorporating external factors.

- **ARIMA(p,d,q) with parameter optimisation**  
  For each SKU, the orders (p,d,q) and seasonal (P,D,Q) are automatically selected by minimising AIC (auto_arima). Forecasts are built for 7, 30, 90 days.

- **Accounting for seasonality**  
  **Monthly coefficients** are used, calculated from historical data over 2 years.  
  Peak season: **March – October** (coefficients 1.1–1.4), off‑peak: November – February (0.6–0.9).

- **Impact of external factors (Prophet)**  
  Regressors are added to the Prophet model:
  – average temperature (data from OpenWeatherMap),
  – holidays (calendar: New Year, May holidays, March 8, February 23, etc.).
  This allows the forecast to be adjusted for anomalous weather or holiday periods.

- **Accuracy assessment**  
  Key metric – **MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error)**.  
  Target: **MAPE < 15%** for the 30‑day forecast.  
  If the threshold of 20% is exceeded, model retraining and input data quality checks are triggered.

**5.1.2. PRICE ELASTICITY (LOGIT MODEL)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The probability that at least one purchase of a product will occur on a given day is described by logistic regression:

\[
P(\text{purchase}=1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(\beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot \text{price} + \beta_2 \cdot \text{competitor\_price} + \beta_3 \cdot \text{rating})}}
\]

- **price** – current product price,
- **competitor_price** – average price of a similar product from major competitors (data from the “Competitive intelligence” agent),
- **rating** – average product rating (1–5) from reviews.

**Coefficient estimation:**  
Coefficients \(\beta_0, \beta_1, \beta_2, \beta_3\) are estimated by maximum likelihood on **historical Wildberries data** (sales, prices, competitor prices, ratings) for the last 6 months. The model is recalculated monthly. Expected signs: \(\beta_1 < 0\) (price increase reduces probability), \(\beta_2 > 0\) (increase in competitor prices raises our attractiveness), \(\beta_3 > 0\) (high rating increases probability).

**5.1.3. INVENTORY OPTIMISATION**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

For inventory management, the **(s, S) model** is applied, accounting for demand and lead time.

- **Parameters:**
  - \(s\) – reorder point (lower stock level),
  - \(S\) – target replenishment level (upper stock level).

- **Parameter calculation:**
  \[
  s = \mu_L + z \cdot \sigma_L
  \]
  \[
  S = s + Q_{\text{opt}}
  \]
  where \(\mu_L\) – average demand during lead time \(L\), \(\sigma_L\) – standard deviation of demand over \(L\), \(z\) – service level factor (95%, \(z=1.645\)), \(Q_{\text{opt}}\) – economic order quantity (Wilson formula, accounts for ordering and holding costs).

- **Objective function:**  
  Minimisation of total costs:
  \[
  \min \left( \text{holding cost} + \text{shortage cost} \right)
  \]
  Shortage cost is estimated as lost marginal profit when the product is out of stock.

The model is recalculated daily based on the demand forecast (Section 5.1.1) and actual stock levels.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**5.1.4. BUSINESS HEALTH SCORE**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Integral business “health” indicator:

\[
H = 0.4 \cdot \text{turnover}_\text{norm} + 0.3 \cdot \text{shortage}_\text{norm} + 0.3 \cdot \text{margin}_\text{norm}
\]

- **Turnover_norm** – normalised inventory turnover (target 30 days, normalised to interval 0–1, where 1 is ideal).
- **Shortage_norm** – inverse share of days with shortage for fast‑moving SKUs (1 – share of days with shortage).
- **Margin_norm** – ratio of current margin to target (35%), capped at 0–1.

**Colour zones and automatic playbook:**
| H              | Zone     | Action                                                       |
|----------------|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| ≥ 0.7          | Green    | Monitoring, investments in expansion permitted               |
| 0.5 ≤ H < 0.7  | Yellow   | Daily analysis, price/procurement adjustments                |
| < 0.5          | Red      | **Automatic “Retention” playbook** – pause auto‑procurement, root cause analysis, owner manual intervention |

Health Score is recalculated daily and displayed in the console.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**5.1.5. ACCOUNT SCORE FOR TASKS**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Ranking of tasks requiring operator attention (confirmation queue in the console):

\[
S = 0.5 \cdot \text{urgency} + 0.3 \cdot \text{revenue\_impact} + 0.2 \cdot \text{available\_resources}
\]

- **Urgency** – inverse time until deadline (normalised 0–1, where 1 is urgent).
- **Revenue_impact** – expert assessment (0–1) from agents, accounting for potential loss or gain.
- **Available_resources** – readiness of resources to perform the task (1 = all resources available, 0 = missing).

Tasks with \(S \ge 0.7\) are displayed in red in the console queue and require priority consideration. Thresholds and weights can be adjusted by the operator via settings.
