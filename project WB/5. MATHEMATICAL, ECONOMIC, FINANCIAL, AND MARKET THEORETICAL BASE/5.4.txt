**5.4. MARKET THEORY**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Market theory provides an understanding of the processes of new product diffusion,
the structure of competition on Wildberries, and consumer segmentation. The models
in this section are used by the “Assortment strategist” agent (level 2), the
“Expansion analyst” agent (level 2), and the “Competitive intelligence” agent (level 1).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**5.4.1. BASS MODEL (DIFFUSION OF INNOVATIONS) FOR NEW PRODUCTS**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**5.4.1.1. Model Formulation**
The Bass model describes the spread of a new product in the market as a result of
interaction between innovators (trendsetters) and imitators (followers). The
cumulative adoption share by time \(t\) is:

\[
F(t) = \frac{1 - e^{-(p+q)t}}{1 + \frac{q}{p} e^{-(p+q)t}}
\]

where:
- \(p\) – coefficient of innovation (probability of purchase influenced by external
  factors, such as advertising),
- \(q\) – coefficient of imitation (probability of purchase influenced by reviews
  and social proof).

**5.4.1.2. Application to New Metal Products**
When launching a new SKU (e.g., decorative picket fence), the “Assortment strategist”
agent estimates parameters \(p\) and \(q\) based on:
- similar competitor products (historical data),
- product category (for building materials, \(q\) is usually higher than \(p\)).

**5.4.1.3. Sales Forecast for a New Product**
Expected number of sales by time \(t\):
\[
N(t) = M \cdot F(t)
\]
where \(M\) is the potential market capacity for that SKU (estimated from demand
analytics and competitor data).

The model is used to:
- decide whether to launch a new product (if forecasted volume exceeds a threshold),
- plan production volumes (phased ramp‑up),
- estimate the time to peak sales (typically 3–6 months for metal products).

**5.4.1.4. Model Calibration**
Parameters \(p, q, M\) are calibrated using early sales data of the new product
(first 2–3 months) via nonlinear regression. The forecast is recalculated monthly,
and the production plan is adjusted accordingly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**5.4.2. MARKET STRUCTURE ANALYSIS (COMPETITION ON WILDBERRIES)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**5.4.2.1. Concentration Ratio (CRₙ)**
For each product category (pickets, angle brackets, strips, flashings), the market
share (by revenue) of the top \(n\) sellers is calculated. CR₃ and CR₅ indicate the
level of competition:
- CR₃ > 70% – high concentration, oligopoly,
- 40% < CR₃ < 70% – moderate concentration,
- CR₃ < 40% – fragmented market.

**5.4.2.2. Herfindahl–Hirschman Index (HHI)**
\[
HHI = \sum_{i=1}^{N} s_i^2
\]
where \(s_i\) is the market share of seller \(i\) (in percent). Values:
- HHI < 1500 – competitive market,
- 1500 ≤ HHI < 2500 – moderately concentrated,
- HHI ≥ 2500 – highly concentrated.

For the “metal picket fence” category on Wildberries, according to data from the
“Competitive intelligence” agent, HHI ≈ 1800 (moderate concentration).

**5.4.2.3. Competitive Positioning Map**
The agent constructs a two‑dimensional map with axes:
- **Price** (low / medium / high),
- **Quality / uniqueness** (standard / designer).

This allows identification of:
- direct competitors (similar price, similar assortment),
- indirect competitors (other materials: corrugated sheet, wood),
- niches for differentiation (high price + unique design).

**5.4.2.4. Entry and Exit Barriers**
- **Entry:** low (registration on WB, start of production), but initial advertising
  investment is required to obtain first reviews.
- **Exit:** moderate (liquidation of stock, loss of advertising investments).

The “Expansion analyst” agent uses barrier analysis to assess the feasibility of
entering a new category.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**5.4.3. AUDIENCE SEGMENTATION (B2C, CONSTRUCTION TEAMS)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**5.4.3.1. Main Customer Segments for Metal Products**

| Segment             | Description                                                              | Behaviour on Wildberries                                               |
|---------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **B2C (individuals)** | Owners of private houses, summer residents. Buy pickets, angle brackets, strips for fences and small repairs. | Impulse purchases, price‑sensitive, guided by reviews and rating. Peak demand: spring‑autumn. |
| **Construction teams** | Professional contractors performing orders for fences, canopies, etc.    | Buy in bulk (10–50 units at a time), sensitive to stock availability, require consistent quality. Often look for specific sizes and types. |
| **Small business**   | Building materials stores, resellers.                                    | Buy regularly, seek low prices, ready for large batches.               |
| **Wholesalers**      | Large distributors (rarely on WB, more often through direct contracts).  | Not considered a target segment for WB.                                |

**5.4.3.2. Behavioural Characteristics of Segments**

| Characteristic                | B2C                         | Construction teams          |
|-------------------------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Average basket (RUB)          | 1500–5000                   | 5000–30000                  |
| Price sensitivity             | High                        | Medium (availability more important) |
| Influence of reviews          | Critical                    | High (reliability)          |
| Purchase frequency            | 1–2 times per season         | Weekly (in season)          |
| Preferred time                | Weekends, evening           | Weekdays, morning           |

**5.4.3.3. Marketing Adaptation by Segment**
- **For B2C:** focus on visuals (fence photos), “buy one get one” promotions,
  automatic review replies, geo‑targeting (gardening communities).
- **For construction teams:** bulk discounts (from 10 units), dedicated listings
  with guaranteed availability, fast shipping, pre‑order capability, technical
  documentation (drawings, instructions).

**5.4.3.4. Segmentation via the “Demand Analyst” Agent**
The agent analyses order history to identify:
- orders with quantity >10 units (likely construction teams),
- orders with quantity 1–3 units (B2C),
- orders with regular repetition (small business).

Based on this, demand forecasts are adjusted by segment, improving the accuracy of
production and procurement planning.
