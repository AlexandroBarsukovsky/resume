**8.4. FINANCIAL MODEL (DCF)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) method is used to assess the economic efficiency of
investments in the AI ecosystem. The model accounts for forecast cash flows, the
discount rate, and terminal value. All calculations are automated by the “Financial
controller” agent (D5) and are available in the operator console.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**8.4.1. REVENUE FORECAST (CONSERVATIVE / OPTIMISTIC)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The revenue forecast is based on:
- Historical sales data (first 12 months of operation).
- Demand forecast from agent A3 (see Section 5.1.1).
- The impact of agent implementation (increased conversion, reduced shortages, higher margins).
- Macroeconomic factors (construction activity, seasonality).

**Conservative scenario** assumes:
- Phased agent implementation lagging the plan by 2–3 months.
- Continuation of current market growth dynamics.
- Cautious competitor reactions.

**Optimistic scenario** assumes:
- Accelerated implementation (all agents operational within 6 months).
- Rapid market adaptation and market share growth due to dynamic pricing and assortment expansion.
- A “snowball effect” from creative agents.

Forecast revenue values (monthly and annual) are generated by agent D5 on a 1‑month
step over a 5‑year horizon. For each scenario, the following are calculated:
- ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue),
- growth rates (YoY, QoQ),
- seasonal coefficients.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**8.4.2. NPV, IRR, PAYBACK PERIOD**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**8.4.2.1. Free Cash Flow (FCF)**
\[
FCF_t = \text{Revenue growth}_t - \text{OPEX}_t - \text{CAPEX}_t + \text{Change in working capital}_t
\]
- **Revenue growth** – the difference between revenue after implementation and revenue without implementation (or historical baseline).
- **OPEX** – operating expenses (cloud resources, licenses, support, training).
- **CAPEX** – one‑time investments (development, integration, equipment). Distributed across periods according to implementation phases.
- **Change in working capital** – accounts for growth in accounts receivable (WB payment delays) and inventory (raw materials, finished goods).

**8.4.2.2. Discount rate (WACC)**
The rate is taken as the weighted average cost of capital for a small manufacturing
business. Calculations use a **fixed rate** determined by the operator (e.g., 12–18%).
Agent D5 allows sensitivity analysis by varying the rate within a range.

**8.4.2.3. Net Present Value (NPV)**
\[
NPV = \sum_{t=0}^{T} \frac{FCF_t}{(1 + r)^t}
\]
where \(r\) is the discount rate and \(T\) is the planning horizon (typically 5 years).

**8.4.2.4. Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**
\[
\sum_{t=0}^{T} \frac{FCF_t}{(1 + IRR)^t} = 0
\]
IRR is computed numerically. If IRR > discount rate, the project is considered
economically viable.

**8.4.2.5. Payback Period**
The smallest \(t\) such that the cumulative discounted (or simple) cash flow becomes
non‑negative:
\[
\text{Payback} = \min\left\{ t : \sum_{i=0}^{t} FCF_i \ge 0 \right\}
\]

All these metrics are calculated by agent D5 for both scenarios and presented in the
console as tables and graphs. The operator can modify input parameters (discount rate,
horizon, investment amounts) and observe how NPV, IRR, and Payback change.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**8.4.3. ROI OVER 3 YEARS**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Return on Investment (ROI) over 3 years is calculated as the ratio of total net
profit (including all costs) to the investment amount:

\[
ROI = \frac{\sum_{t=1}^{3} (\text{Net profit}_t) - \text{CAPEX}_0}{\text{CAPEX}_0} \times 100\%
\]

where **Net profit** = Revenue – Cost – WB commissions – Advertising expenses – Other
operating expenses – Taxes.

ROI can be calculated based on actual data (after implementation) as well as forecast.
The target for the project is **>1000%** over 3 years (optimistic scenario). The
conservative scenario yields more modest but still positive values.

Agent D5 automatically updates ROI as new data arrives and allows comparison of actual
results with forecasts.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**8.4.4. AUTOMATION OF FINANCIAL CALCULATIONS**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **Frequency:** monthly (after period close) and on demand.
- **Input data:** tables `orders`, `costs`, `campaigns`, `budget`, plus manual
  adjustments by the operator (discount rate, scenarios).
- **Output data:** report in the console (charts, tables), ability to export to Excel.
- **Algorithm:** Python script using `numpy`, `pandas`, `scipy.optimize` (for IRR).
  All calculations are cached for speed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**8.4.5. SENSITIVITY AND RISK ANALYSIS**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Agent D5 also performs sensitivity analysis by varying key parameters (revenue growth,
OPEX, discount rate) by ±10% and ±20%. Results are presented as a “spider chart” in
the console, allowing the operator to assess which factors most strongly affect NPV.

Additionally, **Value at Risk (VaR)** is calculated for monthly cash flow (based on
historical volatility) to help determine the required size of the contingency fund.
