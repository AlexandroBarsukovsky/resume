**2.1. ABOUT THE COMPANY: METAL PRODUCTS MANUFACTURER**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**2.1.1. Product line and market niche**
The company specialises in the production and sale of metal products for construction and landscaping. Main product categories:

| Category                | Purpose                                                                 | Share of Sales | Average Price (₽) |
|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Metal pickets           | Fencing elements (Euro pickets, planks of various profiles)              | 65%            | 6200             |
| Angle brackets          | Fastening elements for assembling structures, reinforcing joints        | 15%            | 1800             |
| Wind strips             | Decorative and protective elements for roofs and facades                | 12%            | 4500             |
| Flashings               | Protection of window and door openings from moisture                    | 8%             | 3200             |

**2.1.2. Production capacity**
- Workshop area of 350 m², equipped with sheet metal bending machines (3 units), cutting machines (2), powder coating booth.
- Production staff: 8 people (welders, benders, coating operators).
- Capacity: up to 2,500 products per month at full load (current load ≈ 1,100 products/month).

**2.1.3. Sales statistics (history of 1 year 3 months)**
- **Total sold:** 13,918 units
- **Average monthly sales:** 930 units (seasonality: peak March‑October – 1,200–1,500, slump November‑February – 400–600)
- **Revenue:** ≈6.1 million ₽/month (average)
- **Ratings:** 2,876 reviews, of which 87% positive (rating 5), 9% neutral (4), 4% negative (1–3). Average rating – **4.9 out of 5**.
- **Average price:** 5,548 ₽ (weighted by assortment).

**2.1.4. Sales channel**
The only channel is the Wildberries marketplace (seller account, FBO model – full responsibility for warehouse and shipment).  
Advantages of the channel: low entry barrier, access to a million‑strong audience, built‑in logistics.  
Disadvantages: high commissions (10–15% depending on category), limited analytics tools, constant need to monitor competitors.

**2.2. CURRENT BUSINESS PROCESSES (DETAILED DESCRIPTION)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**2.2.1. Production planning and inventory management**
- **Source data:** a manual Excel spreadsheet is maintained with finished goods stock, raw material consumption, and customer orders.
- **Update frequency:** daily (takes 1–2 hours).
- **Planning method:** intuitive, based on the owner’s “feel”. Raw material procurement (rolls of galvanised steel, paint) is triggered when visual stock levels drop.
- **Problems:** shortages during peak season (revenue loss up to 15%), overstocking during off‑season (inventory turnover 45 days vs target 30).

**2.2.2. Pricing**
- Formula: `Price = (Cost + Delivery) × (1 + Markup)`, where markup is fixed (35% for pickets, 40% for angle brackets, 50% for strips and flashings).
- **Competitor monitoring:** once a week the owner reviews 10–20 product cards of similar items, manually adjusting prices when large discrepancies appear.
- **Problems:** delayed reaction (up to 7 days), suboptimal markup, no consideration of demand elasticity.

**2.2.3. Handling reviews**
- **Process:** the owner checks new reviews daily (average 5–10 per day), responds selectively (only to negative ones, occasionally to positive ones).
- **Response time:** average 48 hours (up to 5 days).
- **Analytics:** none; causes of negativity are not systematised.
- **Problems:** missed opportunities to improve rating, customer loss due to unresolved issues, lack of feedback for production.

**2.2.4. Advertising and promotion**
- Tools: participation in Wildberries promotions (2–3 promotions per month), advertising campaigns within the marketplace (configured via the seller account).
- **Budget:** 50–100 thousand ₽ per month (allocated “by eye”).
- **Effectiveness evaluation:** not performed; ROMI not calculated.
- **Problems:** inefficient budget allocation, absence of A/B testing, loss of potential sales.

**2.2.5. Financial accounting and taxes**
- Revenue accounting: export from Wildberries personal account to Excel, manual matching with cost.
- **Taxes:** simplified taxation system (revenue minus expenses) at 15%, calculation once per quarter, preparation of returns – with outsourced accountant (5,000 ₽/month).
- **Problems:** lack of operational margin control, errors during data transfer, high risk of fines.

**2.2.6. Assortment management and new product development**
- New products appear at the owner’s initiative (1–2 per year) – based on personal observations or customer requests.
- **Process:** idea → sketch → production of a trial batch → listing on Wildberries.
- **Problems:** long cycle (2–3 months), high risk of unsuccessful novelties, no systematic trend analysis.

**2.3. PAIN POINTS (IDENTIFIED DURING DIAGNOSIS)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**2.3.1. Data fragmentation and information gaps**
| Data Source               | Format        | Information Used                                  | Synchronisation Problem                     |
|---------------------------|---------------|---------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Wildberries API           | Web interface | Orders, stock, prices, reviews, statistics        | No automatic export to accounting system    |
| Excel                     | Spreadsheets  | Production plans, raw material stock, taxes       | Manual entry, transfer errors               |
| 1С (basic)                | Documents     | Cost, raw material purchases                      | Not integrated with WB or Excel             |
| Bank                      | Statements    | Payments, WB commissions                          | No automatic reconciliation                 |

**Consequence:** the owner spends 10–15 hours per week gathering and reconciling data instead of strategic development.

**2.3.2. Peak‑season shortages, off‑season overstocking**
- **Cause:** lack of demand forecasting models, seasonality consideration, competitor stock monitoring.
- **Numbers:** March–October monthly potential revenue loss of 10–15% due to shortages; November–February warehouse stocks exceed demand by 30–40%.

**2.3.3. Inaccurate revenue and inventory forecasts**
- Current practice: no forecasts are made; all decisions are made post‑factum.
- **Consequences:** suboptimal use of working capital, difficulties in raw material procurement planning (lead time 2–3 weeks), inability to predict need for credit.

**2.3.4. Missed assortment expansion opportunities**
- Competitors regularly introduce novelties (decorative pickets, thermal wood, combination fences). The company has no system for tracking trends.
- **Loss:** up to 30% of potential revenue from unrealised categories.

**2.3.5. High workload on the owner**
- **Typical week:** 15 hours on operational management (accounting, shipments, replies), 5 hours on taxes/finance, 10 hours on production planning, 5 hours on advertising and analytics.
- **Total:** 35+ hours of routine, leaving little time for strategy, negotiations, searching for new channels.

**2.4. PROJECT OBJECTIVES**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**2.4.1. Strategic objectives**
1. **Automate operational activities** – reduce the share of manual work from 95% to 15–20% within 12 months.
2. **Improve financial efficiency** – increase margin from 27% to 35–40% through optimisation of pricing, procurement, and assortment.
3. **Accelerate market response** – reduce the time from signal appearance (competitor price change, negative review, seasonal surge) to action from days to hours.
4. **Expand assortment and channels** – ensure automatic generation and launch of new products (5–10 per month), integration with Ozon and Yandex.Market.

**2.5. PROJECT CONSTRAINTS**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**2.5.1. Technological constraints**
- Preserve existing accounting systems (Excel, 1С) – modifications via API or import/export are allowed, but not replacement.
- Use official Wildberries APIs (FBS/FBO) while respecting rate limits (no more than 100 requests/min).
- Deploy in cloud infrastructure (Yandex Cloud / Selectel) with guarantee of compliance with Federal Law‑152 (for personal data – if required).
- Avoid complex analytical databases (Snowflake) in favour of PostgreSQL + Redis (optimised for small business).

**2.5.2. Organisational constraints**
- **Human in the loop:** all strategic decisions (large purchases, assortment changes, entry into new channels) remain with the owner.
- **Training:** the owner must learn the operator console (game‑like interface) for manual intervention and control.
- **Implementation time:** no more than 12 months (with phased rollout possible).

**2.5.3. Legal and compliance constraints**
- Compliance with personal data protection legislation (Federal Law‑152) when processing reviews and customer contact data.
- Prohibition on using agents to manipulate ratings or illegally collect competitor data.
- Retention of copyright on developed software – intellectual property of the business owner.
