**1. INTRODUCTION: WHY DO YOU NEED THIS THING?**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

You are the owner of a business that makes metal fences, angle brackets, wind strips,
and flashings. You sell on Wildberries. Business is going well: in just over a year
you have sold almost 14,000 items, with an average rating of 4.9 out of 5. But you
feel that you cannot continue like this.

Currently, you or your employees do everything manually:
- monitor stock (in Excel, by eye),
- set prices (cost + markup, without looking at competitors),
- reply to reviews (when you remember),
- run ads (whatever feels right),
- calculate taxes and revenue (once a quarter, with errors).

As a result:
- in season you run out of goods and lose money;
- in the off‑season the warehouse is full, money is tied up;
- you spend 35+ hours a week on routine;
- new products appear once a year, but they could appear every month.

**What do we propose?**
Build a whole ecosystem of 38 smart helpers (we call them “agents”) that will take
over all the boring work. They will monitor the market, forecast, make decisions,
and take action. You remain the boss – only now you will not be a “worker” but a
“commander”. You will have a game‑style console (like in a strategy game) where you
can see everything that is happening and intervene at any moment.

This document is a full description of how it all works. It is written so that even
a “smart monkey” can understand it. That is, no complicated words, but with complete
accuracy.

**3. WHAT WE PROPOSE (THE SOLUTION)**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

We are creating a “factory” of 38 agents. They are divided into four floors (levels):

**3.1. First floor – Analysts (6 agents)**
They collect information:
- **Competitive intelligence** – every 30 minutes looks at competitor prices and promotions.
- **WB API monitor** – constantly receives your orders, stock, reviews.
- **Demand analyst** – builds sales forecasts for a week, a month, three months.
- **Review processor** – reads all reviews, understands sentiment (good/bad),
  extracts topics (quality, delivery) and remembers them.
- **News monitor** – follows news about Wildberries, the market, competitors.
- **Seasonal forecaster** – calculates which months have higher sales (March‑October is peak).

**3.2. Second floor – Strategists (6 agents)**
They decide what to do:
- **Pricing strategist** – calculates what price will give the maximum profit
  considering cost and competitor prices.
- **Assortment strategist** – decides which products to remove, which to add,
  which new products to launch.
- **Risk manager** – calculates the “health” of the business (Health Score) and
  raises the alarm if it falls.
- **Marketing analyst** – allocates the advertising budget to get maximum return.
- **Quality control** – analyses complaints to improve production.
- **Expansion analyst** – looks for new platforms (Ozon, Yandex.Market) and niches.

**3.3. Third floor – Tacticians (8 agents)**
They adapt the strategy to specific products and coordinate work:
- 4 agents – one for each product group (pickets, angle brackets, wind strips, flashings).
- **Scriptor #1** – transfers tasks between floors.
- **Archivist** – saves everything, keeps history.
- **Supervisor** – ensures all agents are working, restarts failed ones.
- **Production scheduler** – gives tasks to the team when something needs to be made.

**3.4. Fourth floor – Executors (18 agents)**
They do concrete things:
- **Tax calculator** – calculates taxes, generates payment orders.
- **Ad optimiser** – starts/stops ads, adjusts bids.
- **Inventory manager** – orders raw materials when needed.
- **Logistics dispatcher** – ships goods to Wildberries.
- **Financial controller** – keeps track of money.
- **Legal assistant** – checks contracts, helps with claims.
- **Customer service** – replies to reviews (automatically, but you can adjust).
- **Document flow** – creates invoices, waybills.
- **Scriptor #2 (Treasury)** – makes sure you do not run out of money.
- **Scriptor #3 (Execution)** – communicates with Wildberries, the bank, 1С.

Plus 8 **creative agents** that come later:
- **Creative designer** – designs new products.
- **Process engineer** – checks if they can be made.
- **Creative marketer** – writes beautiful descriptions.
- **Test analyst** – tests new products in small batches.
- **Trend scout** – follows fashion.
- **System architect** – improves the agents themselves.
- **Partner integrator** – connects new channels (Ozon, social media).
- **Quantum synthesizer** – invents hybrid products (e.g., a fence with solar panels).

**4. HOW IT WORKS (PRINCIPLES)**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**4.1. Everything is recorded (atomicity)**
Every order, every review, every price change – the system remembers it as a
separate event. From these bricks you can build any kind of analytics.

**4.2. Real‑time reaction (event‑driven)**
As soon as something happens (a competitor lowers the price, a review appears,
goods run out) – the system reacts immediately. It does not wait for you to look;
it instantly suggests a solution.

**4.3. Division of labour (4 floors)**
Each agent does its own narrow job. This is like a good company: some collect data,
others think, others do.

**4.4. You are the boss (hybrid governance)**
Agents propose, you approve or adjust. Big decisions (large purchases, launching
new products) are yours. Routine is done by them.

**5. HOW IT LOOKS ON SCREEN (THE OPERATOR CONSOLE)**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The console is your command centre. It is designed like a strategy game to be
engaging and easy to understand.

**Main screen:**
- Large numbers: revenue, margin, turnover, Health Score.
- Product map: 4 blocks (pickets, angle brackets, wind strips, flashings). Colours:
  green – all good, yellow – needs attention, red – act now.
- Agent list: icons with indicators. Click – you see their log, settings.
- Task queue: e.g., “A competitor lowered the price, we suggest changing the price to 5850?”
  Buttons: “Yes”, “No”, “Configure”.

**Settings:**
- You can change thresholds (e.g., at what competitor price to react).
- You can turn automatic actions on/off.
- You can view the history of all events.

**Exception handling:**
If something goes wrong (e.g., the Wildberries API did not respond), the event
lands in the “unprocessed queue”. You can see what happened and manually retry
or fix it.

**6. THE MATHEMATICS BEHIND IT (FOR THE CURIOUS)**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

We will not overload you with formulas, but we want you to know that it is serious.

**6.1. Sales forecast**
The computer looks at sales history, adds seasonality (April‑May sales are higher),
weather (people build fences less in rain), holidays (May holidays are quiet). It
mixes three methods (Prophet, ARIMA, smoothing) and produces a forecast. The error
is usually less than 15% – so it guesses pretty accurately.

**6.2. Price**
Logit model: how purchase probability depends on price, competitor price, and rating.
The agent finds the price that makes you the most profit without losing sales.

**6.3. Stock**
(s, S) formula: when stock falls below threshold s – order. Order enough to reach
level S. It considers the lead time and how much you would lose if you run out.

**6.4. Business Health Score**
Three indicators combined: shortage (how often you ran out), turnover (how fast
goods sell), margin (how much stays in your pocket). If the total falls below 0.5 –
alarm, you need to investigate.

**6.5. Finance**
We calculate how much money the system will bring. Payback – about 6–8 months.
Over three years, every rouble invested returns 10–15 roubles.

**8. HOW WE KEEP THINGS FROM FALLING APART (RISKS AND PROTECTION)**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**8.1. If you do not trust the robots (organisational risk)**
We make the interface clear, like a game. You can always cancel any decision.
The first phases will be run on a small number of products so that you can see
the results.

**8.2. If Wildberries breaks (technical risk)**
We have a backup plan: you can upload data manually via Excel, and the system
will keep working.

**8.3. If the budget runs out (financial risk)**
We allocate money in stages. If something goes wrong at any stage, we can stop.
We will set aside 10% for unforeseen expenses.

**8.4. If data is stolen (security)**
Everything will be encrypted, access only with a password. You will see who logged
in and when. No leaks.

**8.5. If a new product infringes copyright (intellectual property)**
The legal agent will check patent databases to avoid accidentally copying something.
And the final decision is yours.
