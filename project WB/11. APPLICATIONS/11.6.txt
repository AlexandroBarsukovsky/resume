**11.6. GLOSSARY OF TERMS**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The glossary contains definitions of key terms used in the architectural document
and during the operation of the AI ecosystem. Terms are listed in alphabetical order.
For each term, a brief definition is provided along with a reference to the section
of the document where the term is explained in detail.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Account Score**
Priority score for tasks displayed in the operator console. Formula: S = 0.5·urgency
+ 0.3·revenue_impact + 0.2·available_resources. Tasks with S ≥ 0.7 are shown in
red and require priority attention. *See Section 5.1.5.*

**Ad Optimiser (D2)**
Executive agent that manages bids and budgets for advertising campaigns on
Wildberries, calculates ROMI. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Agents**
Software components that perform automated actions within the AI ecosystem. Agents
are divided into 4 levels: analytical (data collection and processing), strategic
(decision‑making), tactical (coordination and adaptation), executive (execution of
operations). There are 38 agents in the system. *See Section 4.2.*

**Assortment Strategist (B2)**
Level 2 agent responsible for analysing the assortment matrix, generating ideas for
new products (together with GENESIS), and eliminating unprofitable items.
*See Section 4.2.2.*

**Atomicity of Data (Data Atomicity)**
The principle according to which every significant event (order, review, price
change) is recorded as an indivisible unit with full context. This is the foundation
for event‑driven architecture and accurate analysis. *See Section 4.1.1.*

**Audience Segmentation**
Division of buyers into groups (B2C, construction teams, small business) to adapt
marketing and pricing policies. *See Section 5.4.3.*

**Bass Model**
Diffusion of innovations model describing the spread of a new product in the market
as the interaction of innovators and imitators. Used by the assortment strategist
to forecast sales of new products. *See Section 5.4.1.*

**Bridge (Scriptor #1)**
Link between the core (levels 1–3) and the executive coalition (level 4), ensuring
reliable task transfer via Redis Streams. *See Section 4.3.1.*

**Budget**
The set of limits by expense category (raw material purchase, advertising, taxes,
reserve), managed by Scriptor #2. *See Sections 4.3.2, 6.1.7.*

**Competitive Intelligence (A1)**
Level 1 agent that monitors competitor prices, promotions, assortment, and ratings
on Wildberries. *See Section 4.2.1.*

**Control Panel Operator**
The business owner, who interacts with the system through the operator console,
approves critical decisions, and handles exceptions. *See Section 4.4.*

**Creative Agents**
A group of 8 emergent agents (E1–E8) responsible for idea generation, design,
marketing, A/B testing, and channel expansion. Activated in Phase 4.
*See Section 4.2.4.*

**Creative Designer (E1)**
Creative agent that generates 3D models and drawings of new products based on trends.
*See Section 4.2.4.*

**Creative Marketer (E3)**
Creative agent that creates names, descriptions, and creatives for new products.
*See Section 4.2.4.*

**Customer Service (D7)**
Executive agent that automatically replies to reviews (via LLM) and escalates
complex cases to the operator. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Dead Letter Queue (DLQ)**
Queue of unprocessed events that could not be processed after 3 attempts. The operator
can view and reprocess them via the console. *See Sections 4.3.1, 7.1.2.*

**Deadline**
The due date for a production task set by the production scheduler.
*See Section 6.1.6.*

**Demand Analyst (A3)**
Level 1 agent that builds sales forecasts based on historical data, seasonality,
weather, and holidays using an ensemble of models (Prophet, ARIMA, Holt‑Winters).
*See Sections 4.2.1, 5.1.1.*

**Demand Forecast**
Expected sales quantity over a horizon of 7, 30, or 90 days, calculated by an
ensemble of models (Prophet, ARIMA, Holt‑Winters) taking into account seasonality,
weather, and holidays. *See Section 5.1.1.*

**Document Flow (D8)**
Executive agent that generates invoices, waybills, certificates in PDF/Excel format.
*See Section 4.2.4.*

**Event‑driven Architecture**
An architecture where actions are triggered by events (data changes, time signals,
parsing results). Implemented via Redis Streams. *See Section 4.1.2.*

**Executive Agents (Level 4)**
The lowest level of the hierarchy, performing concrete operations: tax calculations,
advertising optimisation, inventory management, logistics, financial accounting, etc.
*See Section 4.2.4.*

**Expansion Analyst (B6)**
Strategic agent that analyses new sales channels (Ozon, Yandex.Market) and niches
for assortment expansion. *See Section 4.2.2.*

**Financial Controller (D5)**
Executive agent that records revenue, commissions, cost, generates P&L and cash flow,
and controls the budget. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**GENESIS (E1)**
Creative agent that generates new product ideas based on trends, reviews, and
competitor data. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Glossary**
Reference section containing definitions of all key terms. *See Section 11.6.*

**Health Score (Business Health Score)**
Composite indicator calculated as H = 0.4·turnover_norm + 0.3·shortage_norm +
0.3·margin_norm. Used to monitor business health and launch the “Retention” playbook
when H < 0.5. *See Sections 5.1.4, 5.4.*

**Hybrid Governance**
The principle whereby agents perform routine, predictable operations, while the
human (business owner) approves critical decisions and handles exceptions.
*See Section 4.1.4.*

**Inventory Manager (D3)**
Executive agent that creates production or raw material purchase orders when stock
falls below threshold s, and tracks deliveries. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Inventory Optimisation**
(s, S) model for inventory management that minimises holding and shortage costs.
Parameters s (reorder point) and S (target replenishment level) are calculated based
on demand forecast and lead time. *See Section 5.1.3.*

**Legal Assistant (D6)**
Executive agent that checks contracts, generates claims, and monitors legal risks.
*See Section 4.2.4.*

**Level 1 – Analytical Agents**
The first hierarchical level, responsible for collecting, enriching, and structuring
data from all available sources, and for initial scoring. There are 6 agents in this
level. *See Section 4.2.1.*

**Level 2 – Strategic Agents**
The second hierarchical level, making decisions based on analytics: pricing,
assortment, risks, marketing, quality, expansion. There are 6 agents in this level.
*See Section 4.2.2.*

**Level 3 – Tactical Coordinators**
The third hierarchical level, adapting strategy to product groups and coordinating
execution. Includes 4 product‑group coordinators and 4 special coordinators
(Scriptor #1, Archivist, Supervisor, Production Scheduler). *See Section 4.2.3.*

**Logistics Dispatcher (D4)**
Executive agent that creates shipment requests on Wildberries, prints labels, and
tracks shipments. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Logit Model**
Price elasticity model describing purchase probability as a function of price,
competitor price, and rating: P = 1 / (1 + exp(-(β₀ + β₁·price + β₂·competitor_price + β₃·rating))).
*See Section 5.1.2.*

**Marketing Analyst (B4)**
Strategic agent that optimises the allocation of advertising budget across products
and channels to maximise ROMI. *See Section 4.2.2.*

**Multi‑level Orchestration**
The principle of dividing functions into 4 hierarchical levels (analytical,
strategic, tactical coordinators, executive), which ensures scalability and
flexibility. *See Section 4.1.3.*

**News Monitor (A5)**
Level 1 agent that collects news about Wildberries, metal products, competitors,
and legislation from RSS, Telegram, and official notifications. *See Section 4.2.1.*

**Operator Console**
Web interface in the style of a strategy game, through which the business owner
manages agents, views metrics, approves decisions, and handles exceptions.
*See Section 4.4.*

**Partner Integrator (E7)**
Creative agent that searches for and automatically connects new suppliers and
marketplaces. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Playbook**
A predefined sequence of automated actions triggered by a specific event. Key
playbooks: “Retention”, “Price War”, “Assortment Expansion”, “Shortage”.
*See Section 7.2.*

**Pricing Strategist (B1)**
Strategic agent that determines the optimal price based on cost, competitor prices,
demand forecast, and elasticity, using a logit model. *See Sections 4.2.2, 5.1.2.*

**Process Engineer (E2)**
Creative agent that checks the manufacturability of new products, calculates cost,
and selects materials. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Production Scheduler (L3)**
Tactical coordinator that creates production tasks based on forecasts, stock, and
orders, and distributes them among teams. *See Section 4.2.3.*

**Quality Control (B5)**
Strategic agent that analyses defects from reviews and provides feedback to
production. *See Section 4.2.2.*

**Quantum Synthesizer (E8)**
Creative agent that creates hybrid products (e.g., pickets with solar panels).
*See Section 4.2.4.*

**Review Processor (A4)**
Level 1 agent that performs NLP analysis of reviews (ruBERT): sentiment classification,
topic extraction, defect identification, and embedding generation. *See Section 4.2.1.*

**Risk Manager (B3)**
Strategic agent that calculates Health Score, identifies risks of shortage,
overstocking, and margin decline, and launches the “Retention” playbook.
*See Section 4.2.2.*

**Scriptor #1 (Bridge)**
Link between the core (levels 1–3) and the executive coalition (level 4), ensuring
reliable task transfer via Redis Streams. *See Section 4.3.1.*

**Scriptor #2 (Treasury)**
Budget management: checks limits, blocks spending when limits are exceeded, notifies
the operator. *See Section 4.3.2.*

**Scriptor #3 (Execution)**
Unified layer for calling external APIs (Wildberries, 1С, bank), with caching,
retry, and fallback to manual input. *See Section 4.3.3.*

**Seasonal Forecaster (A6)**
Level 1 agent that computes seasonal coefficients for each SKU based on historical
sales, weather data, and holiday calendars. *See Section 4.2.1.*

**Supervisor (L3)**
Tactical coordinator that monitors agent load, restarts failed components, and
balances load. *See Section 4.2.3.*

**System Architect (E6)**
Creative agent that optimises agent hierarchy and performs self‑learning.
*See Section 4.2.4.*

**Tax Calculator (D1)**
Executive agent that calculates taxes (simplified tax system), generates payment
orders, and reminds of deadlines. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Test Analyst (E4)**
Creative agent that runs A/B tests for new products, analyses conversion and reviews,
and assesses potential. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Trend Scout (E5)**
Creative agent that analyses social media and search queries to detect emerging
trends. *See Section 4.2.4.*

**Trigger**
An event (data change, timer signal, parsing result) that initiates an automated
action or playbook. There are 16 triggers defined in the system (T01–T16).
*See Section 7.1.*

**WB API Monitor (A2)**
Level 1 agent that collects operational data from the Wildberries personal account
via the official API (orders, stock, reviews, statistics). *See Section 4.2.1.*
