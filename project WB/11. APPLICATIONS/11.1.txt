**11.1. COMPLETE SET OF FORMULAS**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This appendix presents all the mathematical formulas used in the AI ecosystem.
For each formula, a description of the variables, scope of application, data source,
and calibration method is provided. The formulas are implemented in agent code and
can be viewed and adjusted via the operator console.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**11.1.1. DEMAND FORECASTING (ARIMA, PROPHET)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Purpose:** forecasting sales for 7, 30, and 90 days for each SKU.

**11.1.1.1. Ensemble model**
\[
\hat{y}_t = w_1 \cdot \hat{y}_t^{(Prophet)} + w_2 \cdot \hat{y}_t^{(ARIMA)} + w_3 \cdot \hat{y}_t^{(HW)}
\]
where:
- \(\hat{y}_t^{(Prophet)}\) – forecast from the Prophet model (Facebook),
- \(\hat{y}_t^{(ARIMA)}\) – forecast from the ARIMA model (auto.arima),
- \(\hat{y}_t^{(HW)}\) – forecast from exponential smoothing (Holt‑Winters),
- \(w_1, w_2, w_3\) – weights, optimised monthly by minimising MAPE on a test set (last 30 days).

**11.1.1.2. Prophet model**
\[
y(t) = g(t) + s(t) + h(t) + \varepsilon_t
\]
- \(g(t)\) – trend (piecewise linear or logistic),
- \(s(t)\) – seasonality (multiplicative, period 7 and 365 days),
- \(h(t)\) – holiday effects (added as regressors),
- \(\varepsilon_t\) – noise.

Regressors: price, temperature, precipitation, holiday flag.

**11.1.1.3. ARIMA model (auto.arima)**
\[
\phi_p(B)(1-B)^d y_t = \theta_q(B) \varepsilon_t
\]
Orders \((p,d,q)\) and seasonal orders \((P,D,Q)_m\) are automatically selected by AIC.

**11.1.1.4. Exponential smoothing (Holt‑Winters)**
\[
\hat{y}_{t+h|t} = (l_t + h b_t) \cdot s_{t+h-m(k+1)}
\]
- \(l_t\) – level,
- \(b_t\) – trend,
- \(s_t\) – seasonal component (period 7 and 365).

**11.1.1.5. Weather factor adjustment (linear regression)**
\[
y_t = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot T_t + \beta_2 \cdot P_t + \varepsilon
\]
Coefficients are recalculated monthly; \(T_t\) – temperature, \(P_t\) – precipitation.

**11.1.1.6. Accuracy assessment (MAPE)**
\[
\text{MAPE} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{t=1}^{n} \left| \frac{y_t - \hat{y}_t}{y_t} \right| \times 100\%
\]

**11.1.2. PRICE ELASTICITY (LOGIT MODEL)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Purpose:** determining purchase probability as a function of price, competitor prices, and rating.

**11.1.2.1. Logistic regression**
\[
P(\text{purchase} = 1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(\beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot p + \beta_2 \cdot p_c + \beta_3 \cdot r)}}
\]
where:
- \(p\) – current product price,
- \(p_c\) – average competitor price,
- \(r\) – average rating (1–5),
- \(\beta\) – coefficients estimated by maximum likelihood on historical data (6 months).

**11.1.2.2. Interpretation of coefficients**
- \(\beta_1 < 0\) – price increase reduces probability,
- \(\beta_2 > 0\) – competitor price increase raises our attractiveness,
- \(\beta_3 > 0\) – high rating increases probability.

**11.1.2.3. Price optimisation objective function**
\[
\max_{p} \quad \pi(p) = (p - c) \cdot \mathbb{E}[Q(p)] \cdot P_{\text{purchase}}(p)
\]
where:
- \(c\) – cost,
- \(\mathbb{E}[Q(p)]\) – expected number of buyers (from demand forecast),
- \(P_{\text{purchase}}(p)\) – logit probability.

The solution is found numerically (golden section search) on the interval \([c(1+m_{\min}), \max(p_c, c(1+m_{\max}))]\).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**11.1.3. INVENTORY OPTIMISATION (s, S)**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Purpose:** determining order and replenishment thresholds to minimise costs.

**11.1.3.1. Basic parameters**
- \(L\) – lead time (days),
- \(\mu_L = \hat{y}_{L} \cdot L\) – mean demand during lead time,
- \(\sigma_L = \sqrt{L \cdot \sigma^2}\) – standard deviation of demand during lead time,
- \(z = 1.645\) – service level factor for 95%,
- \(K\) – fixed order cost,
- \(h\) – holding cost per unit per day (≈0.5% of cost).

**11.1.3.2. Reorder point (s)**
\[
s = \mu_L + z \cdot \sigma_L
\]

**11.1.3.3. Economic order quantity (Wilson formula)**
\[
Q_{\text{opt}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot \mu_L \cdot K}{h}}
\]

**11.1.3.4. Target replenishment level (S)**
\[
S = s + Q_{\text{opt}}
\]

**11.1.3.5. Total cost minimisation objective**
\[
\min \left( h \cdot \text{average inventory} + K \cdot \frac{\text{annual demand}}{Q_{\text{opt}}} + \text{shortage cost} \right)
\]
Shortage cost is estimated as lost marginal profit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**11.1.4. HEALTH SCORE, ACCOUNT SCORE**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**11.1.4.1. Business Health Score**
\[
H = 0.4 \cdot \text{turnover}_\text{norm} + 0.3 \cdot \text{shortage}_\text{norm} + 0.3 \cdot \text{margin}_\text{norm}
\]
where:
- \(\text{turnover}_\text{norm} = 1 - \frac{\text{turnover} - 15}{60 - 15}\) (clipped to [0,1]),
- \(\text{shortage}_\text{norm} = 1 - \frac{\text{days with shortage}}{\text{days in month}}\),
- \(\text{margin}_\text{norm} = \frac{\text{margin} - 20}{50 - 20}\) (clipped to [0,1]).

**11.1.4.2. Task Account Score**
\[
S = 0.5 \cdot \text{urgency} + 0.3 \cdot \text{revenue\_impact} + 0.2 \cdot \text{available\_resources}
\]
- **urgency** – inverse time to deadline (0–1),
- **revenue_impact** – estimate (0–1) from agents,
- **available_resources** – resource readiness (0–1).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**11.1.5. DCF, ROI, VaR**
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**11.1.5.1. Free Cash Flow (FCF)**
\[
FCF_t = \Delta \text{Revenue}_t - \text{OPEX}_t - \text{CAPEX}_t + \Delta \text{Working capital}_t
\]

**11.1.5.2. Net Present Value (NPV)**
\[
NPV = \sum_{t=0}^{T} \frac{FCF_t}{(1 + r)^t}
\]
where \(r\) is the discount rate (15%), \(T\) the horizon (5 years).

**11.1.5.3. Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**
\[
\sum_{t=0}^{T} \frac{FCF_t}{(1 + IRR)^t} = 0
\]
Computed numerically.

**11.1.5.4. Payback Period**
\[
\text{Payback} = \min\left\{ t : \sum_{i=0}^{t} FCF_i \ge 0 \right\}
\]

**11.1.5.5. Return on Investment (ROI) over 3 years**
\[
ROI = \frac{\sum_{t=1}^{3} \text{Net profit}_t - \text{CAPEX}_0}{\text{CAPEX}_0} \times 100\%
\]

**11.1.5.6. Value at Risk (VaR)**
\[
\text{VaR}_{\alpha} = \inf\{ x : F_L(x) \ge \alpha \}
\]
where \(F_L(x)\) is the loss distribution (monthly expenses). Estimated by historical simulation (1,000 scenarios) based on volatility of raw material prices, WB commissions, advertising costs.
